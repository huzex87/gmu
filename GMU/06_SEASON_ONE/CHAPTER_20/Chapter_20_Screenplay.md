# GIDAN MULKI UNIVERSE
## Season One, Chapter 20 — "The Crown's Price"

*Directly continues from Chapter 19 ("Power is Earned") — same scene-day continuity, no time skip unless noted in the first scene heading below. Second half of the original Episode 10 ("Power is Earned").*

### ACT ONE

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — THRONE ROOM — DAY**

The coronation itself — the full council, HIGH CLERIC NAFISA, KAMAL, HALIMA, MALIK, the assembled nobility of Zamar. FATIMA stands before the throne. The Kwarafi contract, signed, sits formally displayed beside the ceremonial regalia — a deliberate visual reminder that this crown was not won cleanly.

**HIGH CLERIC NAFISA**
(to the assembly)
Let it be recorded, by law and by the Covenant's blessing both: Fatima bint Adamu, first daughter of Sultan Adamu the Third, is Sultana of Zamar. The first woman to hold this throne in the history of this kingdom's law.

The crown is placed. A long beat — the hall's reaction genuinely mixed: awe, relief, uncertainty, history landing in real time on people who lived through everything that made it possible.

**FATIMA**
(her first words as Sultana, to the hall)
I will not stand here and tell you this crown was won the way Zamar's law intended it to be. It cost soldiers' lives at a border pass. It cost a scholar's life inside this palace's own walls. It cost my brother a throne he would have worn honorably, and it will cost this kingdom twenty years of trade income I signed away this morning because the alternative was a treasury too weak to survive what's still coming.

(beat, her voice steadying)

I say this not to diminish the crown, but because I intend to rule remembering exactly what it cost, every single day I wear it. That is the only promise I can honestly make you today.

The hall's response is not triumphant applause — it's something quieter, more considered, an audience genuinely weighing an unusually honest coronation speech.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. TSAURI KEEP — KASAR DUTSE — DAY (SAME TIME)**

GARBA, receiving word of the coronation via a rider, standing on his wall overlooking the still Kasar-Dutse-held pass.

**WAR-CAPTAIN**
Zamar has its Sultana, my king. The first woman ever to hold that throne.

**GARBA**
(considering it, genuinely)
Good. I respect what she survived to get there more than I expected to, this season.

**WAR-CAPTAIN**
And the pass, my king? Do we return it, now that Zamar has settled its crisis?

**GARBA**
(a long, hard silence)
No. Not yet. A new ruler on an unsteady throne is not the moment to test whether she can hold what's hers. I'll return it when I judge she's strong enough that giving it back means something — a gift between equals, not a concession to weakness. That may be a year from now. It may be longer.

**WAR-CAPTAIN**
(carefully)
That sounds like patience, my king. Or like leverage, held indefinitely.

**GARBA**
(unreadable)
With me, War-Captain, it has rarely been only one or the other.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT ONE.**

### ACT TWO

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — PRIVATE ANTECHAMBER — NIGHT**

The coronation feast concluded, FATIMA and MALIK alone for the first time since the crown was placed.

**MALIK**
(genuine, no bitterness left in it)
Sultana. I should probably start practicing how that sounds.

**FATIMA**
(a real, tired laugh)
You could just call me Fatima, in private. I intend to still answer to it.

**MALIK**
(a beat, more serious)
I meant what I said in Episode Nine. I still believe it. You earned this the way it was supposed to be earned, even with everything it cost.

**FATIMA**
(quiet, honest)
It didn't feel earned today. It felt purchased. Kwarafi's contract, Garba's pass, Yusuf's cell beneath this palace. I keep waiting to feel like I won something, and mostly I feel like I survived something.

**MALIK**
(gently)
Maybe that's what winning actually is, in a kingdom like this one. Father used to say power is earned. Nobody ever told either of us that "earned" and "clean" were supposed to mean the same thing.

**FATIMA**
(a small, real smile — the tagline landing with its full weight for the first time)
Power is earned. Legacy is chosen. I used to think that was just a phrase carved somewhere in this palace. I don't think I understood it until tonight.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — HALIMA'S QUARTERS — NIGHT**

HALIMA alone with FATIMA, a brief, quiet mother-daughter scene closing out her Episode 6 thread.

**HALIMA**
(gently)
Your father wore that crown for forty years and never once, that I saw, looked as tired as you do tonight wearing it for the first hour.

**FATIMA**
(honest)
Does it get easier, Mother?

**HALIMA**
(a long, loving, unflinching pause)
No. I won't lie to you the way this palace lies to everyone else. It doesn't get easier. You get stronger. Those are different things, and I think you're about to find out exactly how different.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — BALCONY — NIGHT**

FATIMA, alone, steps out onto the same balcony where — unseen by the audience until now — her father once stood. Birnin Zamar spreads below her, lit by lamplight, the city that survived a season it never fully understood was happening around it.

She holds the crown, taken off for a private moment, looking at it in the moonlight — not triumphant, not defeated, something harder to name.

In the far distance, faint but unmistakable, the sound of drums begins — not Zamar's, not Kasar Dutse's rhythm, not anything the audience has heard all season. Something new. Something coming.

Fatima's head lifts toward the sound, listening, her expression shifting from private exhaustion to the first flicker of a ruler's vigilance.

**FATIMA**
(quiet, to the city, to herself, to no one)
Ninety days to survive a crisis I thought was the hardest thing this crown would ever ask of me.

(beat, listening to the drums)

I don't think that's true anymore.

She sets the crown back on her head, turns from the railing, and walks back inside — the city, and the unknown drums beyond it, left glowing in the dark behind her.

**FADE OUT.**

**END OF SEASON ONE.**

**END OF CHAPTER 20.**

## Notes on the Complete Episode (carried from the original Episode 10 draft)

- Delivers all GMU-003 Episode 10 beats: Fatima's ratification (already voted in Episode 9, formally crowned here) through a costly binding trade concession to Kwara; Garba retaining the seized pass rather than a clean resolution; Bashir's death only partly resolved (Abubakar held responsible, Yusuf's fourth power still at large and unnamed); and the final balcony image with distant drums signaling Season Two, exactly as scoped.
- Resolves Yusuf's immediate fate (imprisoned, not executed or freed) per Episode 9's flagged open thread — deliberately leaves him alive and contained rather than eliminated, preserving him as a potential Season Two thread per the character's function as the audience's window into the unnamed fourth power.
- The finale's tagline payoff ("Power is Earned. Legacy is Chosen.") lands directly in Fatima and Malik's Act Four dialogue, tying the franchise's working tagline (GMU README, GMU-002) directly into the text of the season's final act.
- Garba's decision to hold the pass indefinitely rather than return it either as a gift or an ongoing occupation is intentionally ambiguous — consistent with Theme 4's treatment of him throughout, and gives Season Two a live, unresolved territorial thread independent of the fourth-power mystery.
- The closing drums are a deliberate, intentionally unspecified Season Two hook — no sub-bible or Encyclopedia entry currently defines their source; this should be treated as a placeholder mystery for the Showrunner to resolve only once Season Two's actual premise (per GMU-003 Section 1's "series-level premise" note on escalating stakes) is drafted, consistent with the "just enough, just in time" worldbuilding principle.
