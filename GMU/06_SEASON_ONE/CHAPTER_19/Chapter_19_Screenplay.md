# GIDAN MULKI UNIVERSE
## Season One, Chapter 19 — "Power is Earned"

*Restructured from the original Episode 10 ("Power is Earned") into two shorter chapters for a ~15–20 minute-per-chapter format. This chapter covers the first half of the original episode. Continues in Chapter 20 ("The Crown's Price").*

**Complete first draft — Teaser through Act Four.** Fatima is ratified Sultana — Zamar's first female ruler — but only through a binding trade concession to Kwara and the loss of her brother's trust. Garba holds the seized pass; Bashir's death is only partly resolved. Final image: Fatima alone on the palace balcony with the crown, as distant war drums signal Season Two.

---

### TEASER

**FADE IN:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — THRONE ROOM — DAWN**

A room we have never seen used in the series — grand, formal, prepared for a coronation. Servants make final preparations. FATIMA stands alone at its center, in ceremonial dress for the first time all season, looking at the empty throne.

**FATIMA**
(quiet, to herself)
Forty days, the law said. It's been over ninety.

KAMAL enters, formally dressed himself.

**KAMAL**
The council is ready, Sultana-elect. As is the city. As, I imagine, is everyone who spent this season wondering whether Zamar's law would actually survive being tested this hard.

**FATIMA**
(a beat, genuinely uncertain)
Did it, Kamal? Survive, I mean. Or did we simply run out the clock until the alternative got too costly for everyone to keep fighting for.

**KAMAL**
(gently, honestly)
I don't know, Princess. I suspect that's a question historians will still be arguing about long after both of us are gone. Today, it doesn't have to matter. Today you simply have to walk through that door.

**MAIN TITLES.**

---

### ACT ONE

**INT. KWARAFI MERCHANT HOUSE — COUNTING HALL, BIRNIN ZAMAR EMBASSY — DAWN (SAME MORNING)**

AMINA BELLO KWARAFI, in Zamar for the coronation, reviewing a formal contract with her CLERKS — the twenty-year tariff terms first raised in Episode 4.

**AMINA**
(to a clerk)
Have it delivered to the palace within the hour. She'll sign it before the crown ever touches her head, or she won't be wearing one today. That was always the arrangement.

**CLERK**
And if she refuses, Speaker? After everything she's survived this season — the investigation, Yusuf, her brother's withdrawal?

**AMINA**
(a thin, knowing smile)
She won't refuse. She's spent this entire season learning exactly what a throne costs. She's not about to balk at the final invoice.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — PRIVATE ANTECHAMBER — DAWN**

FATIMA, alone, reads the delivered contract — the twenty-year river-tariff concession, exactly as Amina promised in Episode 4, now formalized and final. MALIK enters, finding her with it.

**MALIK**
That's Kwarafi's contract.

**FATIMA**
(grim)
Signed the moment I'm crowned, or the coronation doesn't happen. Twenty years of Zamar's river trade, gone before I've ruled a single day.

**MALIK**
(a beat, quiet)
You don't have to sign it. The Diwan already ratified you at the vote. Kwarafi's leverage was always softer than she wants you to believe.

**FATIMA**
(shaking her head, resigned)
Was it? Look at what's outside those walls, Malik. Garba still holds the pass. The treasury's strained from a season of war footing. I need Kwara's trade income to rebuild what this crisis cost us, or Zamar limps into whatever comes next too weak to survive it. She knows that. That's exactly why the price is what it is.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT ONE.**

---

### ACT TWO

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — HOLDING CHAMBER — DAY**

FATIMA visits YUSUF one final time before the coronation — a scene deliberately mirroring their Episode 9 confrontation, now with the power dynamic reversed.

**FATIMA**
I came to tell you what happens to you today. Not out of mercy. Because I think you'd genuinely prefer to know.

**YUSUF**
(genuinely curious, unafraid)
Go on, then, Sultana-to-be.

**FATIMA**
You'll live. In a cell beneath this palace, for as long as I rule, unless whoever you truly serve decides you're worth trading for. Abubakar will face Zamar's law in full, since he held the blade and you did not. You'll simply be kept — watched, contained, useful only as a warning of exactly how close this kingdom came to being unmade from inside its own walls.

**YUSUF**
(a slow, genuine nod — something almost like respect)
A patient sentence, for a patient man. That's more thoughtful than I expected from you, three months ago.

**FATIMA**
(cold, final)
Three months ago I hadn't yet learned what you taught this entire season, Yusuf, whether either of us wanted the lesson. That trust has to be earned continuously, or it isn't real trust at all. I intend to rule remembering that. Every day.

She leaves without waiting for a response — the last word deliberately hers.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. ZAMAR CAVALRY GROUNDS — DAY**

MALIK, alone, in the same grounds where he refused acclamation in Episode 3, now clearing out his formal command quarters — not abandoning the army, but stepping back from the war-footing leadership role he's held all season. TAHIR finds him.

**GENERAL TAHIR**
You're stepping down from field command. On today of all days.

**MALIK**
(steady)
Not stepping down. Stepping into a different role. Zamar needs a Sultana today, not a rival prince standing at her coronation in uniform, however loyal I actually am. I'll serve her army. I won't command it as though it's still mine to offer or withhold.

**GENERAL TAHIR**
(a long beat, then, genuinely)
For what it's worth, my prince — the sixty men who signed that petition back in Episode Three. Most of them would follow you into exactly this kind of quiet sacrifice faster than they'd have followed you onto a stolen throne. I think you finally understand why.

**MALIK**
(a real, tired smile)
Took me long enough.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT TWO.**
