# GIDAN MULKI UNIVERSE
## Season One, Chapter 07 — "The Merchant's Price"

*Restructured from the original Episode 4 ("The Merchant's Price") into two shorter chapters for a ~15–20 minute-per-chapter format. This chapter covers the first half of the original episode. Continues in Chapter 08 ("A Costly Loyalty").*

**Complete first draft — Teaser through Act Four.** Fatima confronts Amina directly in Kwarafi and discovers Kwara's "neutrality" is a fiction — both factions are being financed simultaneously. Amina, cornered, offers Fatima real backing in exchange for future trade rights: a costly bargain. Malik secures a key general's loyalty through methods that compromise him for the first time.

---

### TEASER

**FADE IN:**

**EXT. THE RIVER CONFEDERACY OF KWARA — TRADE QUARTER — DAY**

A different world from Zamar's stone corridors: open canals, floating markets, a skyline of merchant-house banners in emerald, white, and silver. FATIMA arrives by river barge, KAMAL beside her, visibly out of her element for the first time in the series.

**KAMAL**
(quiet, as they disembark)
This is not Zamar, Princess. Here, the law is whoever's holding the better contract. Speak carefully.

**FATIMA**
(steadying herself)
I've spent my whole life in a palace built on law. I can survive an afternoon in one built on money.

**KAMAL**
(not entirely reassuring)
We'll see.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. KWARAFI MERCHANT HOUSE — COUNTING HALL — DAY**

Vast, airy, lined with ledgers instead of scripture — Kwara's answer to a Diwan hall. AMINA BELLO KWARAFI presides from a raised desk, surrounded by CLERKS moving figures with practiced speed.

**AMINA**
(seeing Fatima enter, genuinely pleased)
Princess. I confess I expected a summons, not a visit. You've saved me the trouble of sending a very expensive gift to soften whatever you came to say.

**FATIMA**
(level)
Save the gift. I came to ask about a shipment of silver, four days before Elder Bashir died, routed through a house he was auditing.

Amina's expression doesn't change — but the clerks around her go very slightly quieter, the way a room does when it's listening harder than it's pretending to.

**AMINA**
(smooth)
Then let's not do this standing in front of my staff.

**MAIN TITLES.**

---

### ACT ONE

**INT. KWARAFI MERCHANT HOUSE — AMINA'S PRIVATE OFFICE — DAY**

Just Fatima, Kamal, and Amina now. Amina pours tea with the same unhurried control Yusuf has shown all season — a deliberate echo the audience should register.

**AMINA**
(pouring)
Yes. That shipment moved through my house. No, I did not kill your Elder Bashir, and no, I did not order anyone else to.

**FATIMA**
That's a very precise denial for someone I haven't accused of anything yet.

**AMINA**
(a small, genuine laugh)
Princess, I have been precisely denying things since before you were born. It's the only kind of honesty that survives in this trade. Ask me the real question.

**FATIMA**
(direct)
Why was your silver moving through a house Bashir was already watching, the same week he died?

**AMINA**
(setting down the teapot, choosing her words)
Because that house owed me a debt, and Bashir's audit threatened to expose exactly how large it was — which would have exposed exactly how deeply I've financed certain interests in Zamar that I would very much prefer stayed private a while longer.

**FATIMA**
(sharpening)
What interests.

Amina studies her for a long beat — deciding, visibly, how much truth to spend.

**AMINA**
(finally)
Yours. And your brother's.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. ZAMAR CAVALRY GROUNDS — DAY**

MALIK meeting privately with a senior officer we haven't seen before — COMMANDER RAFIU, older, weathered, clearly respected. Tahir observes from a distance, arms folded.

**COMMANDER RAFIU**
My garrison holds the eastern approach to the capital, my prince. Sixteen hundred men. I did not sign Tahir's petition — not because I doubt you, but because I do not sell my men's loyalty on parchment. I sell it in person, to whoever earns it.

**MALIK**
(carefully)
And what earns it, Commander?

**COMMANDER RAFIU**
(a long pause)
My son serves in the border garrison at the pass. Undersupplied, underpaid three months running, because the treasury's been quietly redirected toward the capital garrisons who might actually be asked to acclaim someone soon. Fix that, and you'll have earned something worth more than a petition.

Malik absorbs this — a real cost attached to a real request, nothing abstract about it.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT ONE.**

### ACT TWO

**INT. KWARAFI MERCHANT HOUSE — AMINA'S PRIVATE OFFICE — DAY**

**FATIMA**
(cold, working it through)
You financed me. And you financed Malik. At the same time.

**AMINA**
(unbothered, matter-of-fact)
Of course I did. Kwara does not bet on a single horse in a two-horse race, Princess — that would be terrible business. I offered you my backing that first week, and yes, I made a mirror offer to your brother's camp through channels that didn't carry my name. Whichever of you wins, Kwara wins. That is not treachery. That is the entire function of a merchant power that wants to survive both outcomes.

**FATIMA**
(quiet, furious)
It's treachery to me specifically. You let me believe I had an ally.

**AMINA**
(gently, not unkindly)
You have an ally. You simply also have a rival who has the same one. Welcome to actual politics, Princess — I suspect it looks very different from the philosophy your tutors taught you in the Diwan library.

**FATIMA**
(a beat, recalibrating, dangerous now)
Does Malik know?

**AMINA**
Not yet. I would greatly prefer he learns it from someone other than me.

**FATIMA**
(standing)
That's not your choice to protect anymore.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. ZAMAR CAVALRY GROUNDS — WAR TENT — DAY**

Malik and Tahir. Malik has clearly just made a decision he isn't fully comfortable with.

**MALIK**
Redirect the eastern garrison's supply allotment back to the border pass. Immediately. Full back-pay for the last three months, out of my own household's funds if the treasury won't move fast enough.

**GENERAL TAHIR**
(surprised, then approving)
That will cost you personally. And it will look, to anyone paying attention, exactly like buying a commander's loyalty with coin instead of earning it with conviction — the very thing you refused to do in front of a hundred officers three days ago.

**MALIK**
(a hard beat — he knows it)
I know how it looks.

**GENERAL TAHIR**
Does that trouble you?

**MALIK**
(honest, uncomfortable)
Yes. It troubles me considerably. I'm doing it anyway, because Rafiu's son is standing at that pass eating half rations while I make speeches about honor from a warm tent. Some costs are worth being troubled by.

Tahir studies him — the first crack, however principled, in Malik's Episode 3 stance.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT TWO.**
