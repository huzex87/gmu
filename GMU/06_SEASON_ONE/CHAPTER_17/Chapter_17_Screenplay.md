# GIDAN MULKI UNIVERSE
## Season One, Chapter 17 — "The Diwan Convenes"

*Restructured from the original Episode 9 ("The Diwan Convenes") into two shorter chapters for a ~15–20 minute-per-chapter format. This chapter covers the first half of the original episode. Continues in Chapter 18 ("The Confession").*

**Complete first draft — Teaser through Act Four.** The ratification vote finally happens amid full crisis. Yusuf al-Kanem's true loyalty — and the identity of the unseen fourth power he serves — is exposed, recontextualizing earlier episodes. Malik, disillusioned by what winning would now require of him, makes a costly, defining choice.

---

### TEASER

**FADE IN:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — DIWAN LIBRARY — NIGHT**

FATIMA, DANLADI (freshly arrived from Episode 8, still travel-worn), and KAMAL, assembling everything: the coin, the silver receipts, the wax seal fragment, Danladi's testimony about the night of the original border skirmish, and — new — a ledger page recovered from Abubakar's abandoned quarters that Kamal's people finally decoded.

**KAMAL**
(laying down the decoded page)
Abubakar wasn't just moving evidence to mislead you, Princess. He was reporting. Weekly. To a name coded throughout this ledger only as "the Steward's master" — payments, instructions, confirmation that Bashir's ledgers had been successfully removed the night he died.

**FATIMA**
(reading it, cold)
Does it name him?

**KAMAL**
(grim)
No. But it names a meeting place. Tonight. The old customs house near the river docks — abandoned since before your father's reign.

**FATIMA**
(a beat, decision made)
Then that's where I'm going.

**DANLADI**
(immediately)
Not alone, Princess.

**MAIN TITLES.**

---

### ACT ONE

**EXT. OLD CUSTOMS HOUSE — RIVER DOCKS — NIGHT**

A derelict stone building, long disused. FATIMA, DANLADI, and a handful of trusted GUARDS approach carefully. Inside, through a gap in the shutters, they see ABUBAKAR — the vanished steward — meeting with a hooded figure.

**FATIMA**
(whispering)
That's him. Abubakar.

The hooded figure lowers their hood: it's YUSUF.

**DANLADI**
(stunned, whispering)
The Crown's own advisor.

**FATIMA**
(quiet, grim — confirmation, not surprise, given Episode 7)
I know.

**INT. OLD CUSTOMS HOUSE — NIGHT (THROUGH THE SHUTTERS)**

**ABUBAKAR**
(nervous, urgent)
They're close, Yusuf. The princess found the seal fragment. She'll have connected it to you by now.

**YUSUF**
(calm, utterly composed, unlike anything we've seen from him all season)
Then it's fortunate I never planned for this to stay hidden forever. Only long enough.

**ABUBAKAR**
Long enough for what?

**YUSUF**
(a thin, humorless smile)
Long enough for whichever sibling wins this crown to have already made every compromise required to survive winning it. A ruler forged by Kwara's gold, Kasar Dutse's patience, and her own brother's doubt is a far more useful ruler to us than a Sultan who simply inherited a peaceful throne, Abubakar. Chaos was never the goal. It was the method.

Outside, Fatima's hand tightens on Danladi's arm — she's heard enough.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT ONE.**

---

### ACT TWO

**INT. OLD CUSTOMS HOUSE — NIGHT**

FATIMA steps into the doorway, guards behind her, torches raised. YUSUF turns, unsurprised — as if he'd expected this exact moment all along.

**FATIMA**
Yusuf al-Kanem. Or is that even your name.

**YUSUF**
(unbothered, almost pleased)
It's the name I've answered to in this palace for eleven years, Princess. Names are the least important thing about a person, in my experience. Ask your real question.

**FATIMA**
Did you kill Elder Bashir.

**YUSUF**
(simply)
No. Abubakar did, on my instruction, when Bashir's audits came too close to exposing the ledger trail I'd spent a decade building. I take responsibility for the order. I did not deliver the blade myself.

Abubakar, cornered, says nothing — the guards moving to restrain him.

**FATIMA**
(the next question costing her visibly)
My father. Did you kill him too.

**YUSUF**
(a long, genuinely considering pause — the first moment all series where Yusuf seems to weigh a real answer rather than perform one)
No. Your father's death was exactly what it appeared to be — an old man's heart, tired after forty years of exactly this kind of pressure. I did not need to kill him, Princess. I only needed to make certain no one was ready when it happened.

**FATIMA**
(a complicated wave of relief and horror both)
You didn't cause the crisis. You simply made sure it would break us when it came.

**YUSUF**
(almost admiring)
You understand faster than most. That is precisely correct.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. OLD CUSTOMS HOUSE — NIGHT (MOMENTS LATER)**

MALIK arrives at speed with additional soldiers, having been summoned by Danladi's earlier rider. He takes in the scene — Yusuf, restrained now, Abubakar in custody.

**MALIK**
(to Fatima, urgent)
Is it done? Is it him?

**FATIMA**
(grim)
It's him. And it's bigger than him.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT TWO.**
