# GIDAN MULKI UNIVERSE
## Season One, Chapter 15 — "The Ground Beneath Them"

*Restructured from the original Episode 8 ("The Ground Beneath Them") into two shorter chapters for a ~15–20 minute-per-chapter format. This chapter covers the first half of the original episode. Continues in Chapter 16 ("The War-Captains' Doubt").*

**Complete first draft — Teaser through Act Four.** A deliberate pressure-release episode before the finale run: focuses on Danladi and the border villages actually living with Kasar Dutse's pass seizure, and on Garba's court at Tsauri Keep — his honor code, his war-captains' doubts about how far he's willing to go. Ends with Danladi forced into the choice his season-long doubt has been building toward.

---

### TEASER

**FADE IN:**

**EXT. BORDER VILLAGE OF KANTIN RAFI — DAY**

A small farming village, now sitting just inside Kasar Dutse's newly held territory since Episode 7's seizure — modest stone-and-thatch homes, a well at the center, life visibly disrupted: fewer people in the fields, KASAR DUTSE SOLDIERS posted at the village edge, watchful rather than hostile.

DANLADI, out of uniform, moves through the village with a small supply cart — clearly here on some errand outside official channels. An OLD FARMER, ADAMU-NOT-THE-SULTAN (a different Adamu, a common name, deliberately not royal), watches him warily.

**OLD FARMER**
You're Zamar. That uniform under your cloak isn't hiding as well as you think.

**DANLADI**
(quiet, unthreatening)
I'm not here as Zamar today. My cousin's family lives two houses from that well. I came to see if they're still fed.

**OLD FARMER**
(a long, appraising look, softening slightly)
Kasar Dutse hasn't starved us. Say that much for them — they took the pass, not our harvest. But nobody's told us yet which kingdom we belong to next season, and a farmer needs to know that before he plants.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. TSAURI KEEP — KASAR DUTSE — DAY**

GARBA reviewing reports from the newly occupied border villages, WAR-CAPTAIN beside him.

**WAR-CAPTAIN**
Kantin Rafi and four other villages are formally inside our new line now, my king. Some of the men are asking whether we should be collecting their harvest taxes as our own, given that.

**GARBA**
(a long beat, considering)
No. Not yet. I took that pass to protect my border, not to annex Zamar's farmers by accident because no one asked the question properly.

**WAR-CAPTAIN**
(careful)
Some would say that's a fine distinction to draw once you're already standing on their land, my king.

**GARBA**
(hard, honest)
Some would be right to say it. I intend to draw it anyway.

**MAIN TITLES.**

### ACT ONE

**EXT. BORDER VILLAGE OF KANTIN RAFI — DAY**

DANLADI, finishing his errand, is stopped by the KASAR DUTSE SOLDIERS posted at the village edge — younger men, wary but not cruel.

**KASAR DUTSE SOLDIER**
You're not from here.

**DANLADI**
(calm, hands visible)
No. Family, though. I mean no trouble — I'll be back across the line before dusk.

**KASAR DUTSE SOLDIER**
(studying him, then, unexpectedly)
You were at the pass. Twelve weeks back. I recognize you — you pulled one of ours out from under a fallen horse during the skirmish, before it turned into what it turned into.

**DANLADI**
(surprised, then, quietly)
I remember. I didn't think anyone on your side would.

**KASAR DUTSE SOLDIER**
(a beat, something unspoken passing between them — soldiers who've stood on opposite sides of the same terrible night)
Go on. Before dusk. I didn't see you.

Danladi nods, moves on — a small, human moment of mercy neither side's leadership will ever know happened.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — WAR TENT — DAY**

MALIK and TAHIR, reviewing the aftermath of Episode 7's losses and Episode 8's uneasy status quo at the pass.

**GENERAL TAHIR**
The border garrison wants to know if they're retaking the pass, holding the new line, or waiting on your sister's investigation to finish before anyone decides anything. Morale won't survive much more waiting, my prince.

**MALIK**
(steady, though tired)
Tell them: we hold, we don't provoke, and we wait for proof before we spend one more life on a war neither side actually wants, if Garba's restraint since the seizure is anything to judge by.

**GENERAL TAHIR**
(a genuine question, not a challenge)
And if the proof, when it comes, isn't Kasar Dutse at all?

**MALIK**
(quiet, having clearly thought about this)
Then we'll have held the line for the right reason the whole time, instead of the wrong one. I can live with that outcome better than the alternative.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT ONE.**
