# GIDAN MULKI UNIVERSE
## Season One, Chapter 16 — "The War-Captains' Doubt"

*Directly continues from Chapter 15 ("The Ground Beneath Them") — same scene-day continuity, no time skip unless noted in the first scene heading below. Second half of the original Episode 8 ("The Ground Beneath Them").*

### ACT ONE

**EXT. TSAURI KEEP — KASAR DUTSE — DAY**

GARBA meeting privately with his own council of war-captains — a scene structurally mirroring Zamar's Diwan hall, giving Kasar Dutse the same interiority the audience has had for Zamar all season.

**WAR-CAPTAIN**
My king, several of us have served you since before the succession crisis began. We ask plainly: was taking the pass truly about our dead, or was it about testing how far Zamar's paralysis would let you go before anyone stopped you?

**GARBA**
(a long silence — this question clearly lands harder than the political ones from earlier episodes)
Both. I would be lying to you, and to myself, if I said it was only grief for six soldiers. I have wanted that pass under Kasar Dutse's control since before I ever wore this crown — my father wanted it before me, and his father before him. Their deaths gave me a reason the rest of you could stand behind without shame. That does not make the grief false. It makes the truth complicated.

**WAR-CAPTAIN**
(genuinely searching)
And where does complicated truth stop, my king? At the pass? At the five villages? At Birnin Zamar's own gates, if Zamar's crisis drags on long enough to tempt you further?

**GARBA**
(hard, final, meeting his eyes)
It stops at the pass. I have said so publicly, I have said so to Zamar's own herald, and I am saying it now to the men who would have to die carrying it further. If I ever give you an order past that line, you have my permission — my instruction — to ask me this question again, in front of every captain in this room.

A long beat — the war-captains, and the audience, left to judge whether that promise will hold.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. BORDER VILLAGE OF KANTIN RAFI — DAY (LATER)**

DANLADI's small act of connection from Act One has clearly been noticed. He's approached, back on the Zamar side of the line now, by COMMANDER RAFIU.

**COMMANDER RAFIU**
Word travels fast at a border this small, soldier. You crossed the line without orders, to check on family in occupied territory.

**DANLADI**
(bracing for discipline)
Yes, Commander. I'll accept whatever's coming for it.

**COMMANDER RAFIU**
(unexpectedly, not angry)
What's coming for it is this: I want you assigned to village liaison duty. Officially, this time, with my authorization. If there are Zamar families sitting inside Kasar Dutse's new line, somebody with your instincts should be the one checking on them — not sneaking, asking.

**DANLADI**
(surprised, genuinely moved)
Sir. Thank you.

**COMMANDER RAFIU**
(a beat, more serious)
Don't thank me yet. That line's still fragile, and you're about to be standing on it more than anyone else in this garrison. That's not a reward, soldier. It's a request that you keep doing exactly what got you in trouble today, only louder and more often.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT ONE.**

### ACT TWO

**EXT. BORDER VILLAGE OF KANTIN RAFI — DAY**

DANLADI, now in an official liaison capacity, moving openly between the Zamar and Kasar Dutse lines with village elders from both sides of the new border, coordinating something practical — water rights from a shared well now split by the occupation line.

**VILLAGE ELDER (ZAMAR SIDE)**
The well's always served both halves of Kantin Rafi. If your soldiers cut it off from our half because of where a map line got drawn twelve weeks ago, people on both sides go thirsty over nothing either of them chose.

**KASAR DUTSE SOLDIER (from Act One, now a recurring presence)**
(to Danladi, respectfully)
I'll take that to my captain. For what it's worth — I don't think King Garba wants a war fought over a well either.

**DANLADI**
(a small, real hope in his voice)
That's the first thing either of us has agreed on in twelve weeks that didn't involve counting bodies.

The scene plays as the episode's thematic heart: ordinary people, on both sides, finding small practical ways to keep living inside a war their leaders started, without either side actually wanting to fight it.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. TSAURI KEEP — KASAR DUTSE — NIGHT**

GARBA alone on his wall again — the show's recurring image of him — WAR-CAPTAIN approaching with unexpected news.

**WAR-CAPTAIN**
My king. Word from the border liaisons. Zamar and our own men have been quietly cooperating — a shared well, grazing rights, small things. Nothing authorized on either side.

**GARBA**
(a long beat, something unguarded crossing his face — not anger, something closer to relief)
Let it continue. Quietly, as you said. I would rather my soldiers find peace with Zamar's farmers at a well than either of us find a reason to justify the next skirmish.

**WAR-CAPTAIN**
(carefully)
That's not the king who addressed us at the start of this season, my king.

**GARBA**
(quiet, honest)
No. I took a pass I'd wanted my whole life and discovered it tastes like six graves and a border full of frightened farmers instead of the victory I imagined as a young man. I'm allowed to have learned something from that.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT TWO.**

---

### ACT THREE

**EXT. BORDER VILLAGE OF KANTIN RAFI — DAY**

DANLADI, in the middle of village liaison work, is interrupted by an URGENT ZAMAR RIDER arriving at speed from the direction of Birnin Zamar.

**URGENT RIDER**
Danladi! Orders from the capital — General Tahir requests you return immediately. Something's broken loose in the palace investigation. They need every soldier who was present at the original skirmish for testimony, and you specifically, given your liaison work since.

**DANLADI**
(torn, looking back at the village — the elders, the shared well, the fragile peace he's helped build)
Now? In the middle of this?

**URGENT RIDER**
Now. They wouldn't have sent me at a gallop if it could wait.

Danladi looks between the village — his small, hard-won piece of the season's larger peace — and the capital, where the real machinery of the war and the murder investigation is finally moving. This is his forced choice: leave the human-scale work he's built, or ignore an order that might matter more than he can yet see.

**DANLADI**
(to the Village Elder, a genuine goodbye)
Keep the well arrangement running. Whatever's called for from the capital, don't let it wait on me.

**VILLAGE ELDER (ZAMAR SIDE)**
(a real, warm farewell)
Go, soldier. You did more good here in a season than most officers manage in a lifetime. That doesn't stop being true because the capital finally noticed you.

Danladi mounts up, riding hard back toward Birnin Zamar — the ground-level thread the season has followed since Episode 1 now converging, at last, with the palace's central plot.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — WAR TENT — NIGHT**

MALIK and TAHIR, awaiting Danladi's arrival.

**GENERAL TAHIR**
He'll be here by morning. Fastest rider we have.

**MALIK**
(quiet, resolved)
Good. If what Fatima's found is what I think it is, we're going to need every honest witness this kingdom has, before this is over.

**GENERAL TAHIR**
(a beat, something like real respect now, season-long tension finally settling into something steadier)
For what it's worth, my prince — whatever's coming, I don't think there's a soldier in this army who'd follow anyone else through it.

**FADE OUT.**

**END OF CHAPTER 16.**

## Notes on the Complete Episode (carried from the original Episode 8 draft)

- Delivers the GMU-003 Episode 8 beat precisely as scoped: a pressure-release episode centered on Danladi and the border villages, giving Garba's court real interiority (per Theme 4, "no one is entirely good or evil") ahead of the finale run, and ending with Danladi's forced choice — reinterpreted here as choosing to answer the capital's summons and leave his liaison work, which pays off his entire season-long arc from reluctant border sentry to a soldier whose instincts for peace get formally recognized.
- Introduces **Commander Rafiu's continued arc** (last seen Episode 4/5) formalizing Danladi's liaison role, and a recurring unnamed **Kasar Dutse Soldier** who mirrors Danladi's arc from the opposing side — recommend promoting both to the background-characters file given their thematic weight.
- Garba's private admission to his war-captains ("I have wanted that pass... since before I ever wore this crown") is the season's most direct acknowledgment of his own mixed motives — intentional per Theme 4, avoiding a clean redemption while still complicating any simple villain reading of him.
- The shared-well subplot is this episode's clearest dramatization of "politics before violence" operating at the level of ordinary people rather than princes and kings — deliberately included as the tonal counterweight the beat sheet calls for.
- Ends with Danladi's thread converging into the palace plot for the first time all season, setting up his presence as a witness in Episode 9.
