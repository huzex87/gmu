# GIDAN MULKI UNIVERSE
## Season One, Chapter 09 — "Ironhand's Gambit"

*Restructured from the original Episode 5 ("Ironhand's Gambit") into two shorter chapters for a ~15–20 minute-per-chapter format. This chapter covers the first half of the original episode. Continues in Chapter 10 ("First Blood").*

**Complete first draft — Teaser through Act Four.** Garba proposes a marriage alliance to whichever sibling ultimately wins — a political trap disguised as diplomacy — and reveals he holds evidence connected to Bashir's death, using it to manipulate both factions. A border skirmish leaves soldiers dead on both sides: the season's midpoint escalation into open bloodshed.

---

### TEASER

**FADE IN:**

**EXT. TSAURI KEEP — KASAR DUTSE — DAY**

GARBA IRONHAND on his fortress wall, as we've seen him before, but today with a formal delegation assembling below — banners, an honor guard, the trappings of a state visit being prepared.

**WAR-CAPTAIN**
The envoy leaves for Birnin Zamar within the hour, my king. Are you certain of the terms?

**GARBA**
(watching the mountains, unreadable)
I am certain of the offer. Whether Zamar is wise enough to accept it is their problem, not mine.

**WAR-CAPTAIN**
And if they refuse?

**GARBA**
(a thin, humorless smile)
Then I've lost nothing, and they've told me exactly how desperate they still are to avoid a war. Either answer is useful to me.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — DIWAN HALL — DAY**

The full council assembled — the largest gathering we've seen since Episode 1. FATIMA, MALIK, KAMAL, and the senior Diwan elders. A KASAR DUTSE ENVOY, formally dressed, stands before them, a sealed scroll in hand.

**KASAR DUTSE ENVOY**
King Garba Ironhand of Kasar Dutse sends greetings to the house of Adamu, and an offer, made in good faith, to end this season of tension between our kingdoms before it costs either of us more than pride.

He hands the scroll to Kamal, who breaks the seal and reads — his face changing as he does.

**KAMAL**
(reading, careful)
He proposes a marriage alliance. Between his own son and heir — and whichever of you two is ultimately ratified as Sultan or Sultana of Zamar.

The hall erupts into low, urgent murmuring. Fatima and Malik exchange a stunned glance.

**MAIN TITLES.**

---

### ACT ONE

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — RECEPTION ROOM — DAY**

Fatima, Malik, and Kamal alone, the envoy dismissed to await a response.

**MALIK**
It's a trap. Whichever of us wins, we're bound into marriage with Kasar Dutse's own bloodline. He gets a permanent claim on Zamar's throne either way, without ever having to win a single battle for it.

**FATIMA**
(working it through)
Worse. If we refuse outright, he tells every kingdom watching that Zamar rejected a peace offer during a succession crisis — and if we accept, we've handed him exactly what you just said. There isn't a clean answer here.

**KAMAL**
(grim)
There rarely is, with Garba. He didn't build Kasar Dutse's strength by making offers with only one useful outcome for him.

**MALIK**
(a beat)
Can we stall? Same as Fatima's been doing with the investigation.

**FATIMA**
(a dry, humorless look)
Everyone in this palace seems to think stalling is a strategy I invented specifically to be difficult. It has limits, Malik. Garba won't wait forever, and neither will the Diwan.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. ZAMAR–KASAR DUTSE BORDER PASS — DAY**

The same pass from Episode 2 — but the standoff has hardened. More soldiers on both sides now, permanent-looking encampments rather than a brief patrol. DANLADI, visibly more weathered, watches a ZAMAR SUPPLY CONVOY move up toward the border garrison — Rafiu's back-pay promise from Episode 4 finally arriving.

**DANLADI**
(to a fellow soldier, watching the convoy)
About time. Rafiu's boy's been eating half rations for three months while the princes argued about honor.

**FELLOW SOLDIER**
(nervous, watching the Kasar Dutse side)
Doesn't matter how well-fed we are if that lot decides today's the day the stones stop meaning anything.

Across the pass, we see, for the first time, Kasar Dutse cavalry beginning to move in formation — not crossing yet, but shifting in a way that reads as preparation rather than routine.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT ONE.**

### ACT TWO

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — DIWAN HALL — DAY**

The council reconvenes. FATIMA rises to address the Kasar Dutse envoy directly.

**FATIMA**
Tell your king that Zamar does not make binding marriages for a throne that hasn't yet been decided. If he wishes to discuss an alliance once ratification is complete, we will hear him then, as equals, not as a condition attached to our own succession.

**KASAR DUTSE ENVOY**
(unmoved, almost expecting this)
King Garba anticipated a version of that answer, Princess. He instructed me to say one more thing, privately, to whichever of you is willing to hear it.

**MALIK**
(wary)
Say it to both of us, or not at all.

**KASAR DUTSE ENVOY**
(a beat, then, carefully)
My king possesses a document. Recovered by his own agents near the border, in the weeks after Elder Bashir's death. He believes it names the party responsible. He is prepared to share it — as a gesture of good faith toward whichever of you is more receptive to his offer.

The hall goes dead silent. Fatima's investigation, Amina's silver trail, Malik's soldiers — all of it suddenly dwarfed by the possibility that Garba has simply had the answer the entire time.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — PRIVATE ANTECHAMBER — DAY**

Fatima and Malik alone, reeling.

**MALIK**
He's had it this whole time. Months of us tearing ourselves apart over coins and silver shipments, and he's been sitting on the actual answer, waiting for the right moment to sell it.

**FATIMA**
(cold, clear-eyed)
Or he's lying, and there is no document, and this is simply the most elegant lever anyone's pulled on us all season — because now neither of us can afford to ignore it, and both of us will be tempted to be "more receptive" than the other to get it.

**MALIK**
(a beat)
You think it's a bluff.

**FATIMA**
I think Garba Ironhand has never once acted this season without at least two possible benefits built into the move. Whether the document is real or not, the offer itself already worked — look at us. We're standing here suspicious of each other again.

They look at each other — the trap Fatima just named already, visibly, starting to work exactly as she described it.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT TWO.**
