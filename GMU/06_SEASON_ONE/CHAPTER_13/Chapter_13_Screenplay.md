# GIDAN MULKI UNIVERSE
## Season One, Chapter 13 — "Tsauri Pass"

*Restructured from the original Episode 7 ("Tsauri Pass") into two shorter chapters for a ~15–20 minute-per-chapter format. This chapter covers the first half of the original episode. Continues in Chapter 14 ("A Flag of Parley").*

**Complete first draft — Teaser through Act Four.** Garba's forces seize the contested mountain pass outright under a self-defense justification — the season's first unambiguous act of war. Fatima's investigation turns a corner: the trail behind Bashir's death doesn't lead outward to Kasar Dutse or Kwara, but inward — to someone inside Zamar's own court.

---

### TEASER

**FADE IN:**

**EXT. ZAMAR–KASAR DUTSE BORDER PASS — NIGHT**

The pass, weeks after Episode 5's skirmish — encampments now permanent fortifications on both sides. A ZAMAR SENTRY patrols the line. In the darkness beyond the Kasar Dutse encampment, torches begin moving — many of them, in disciplined formation.

**ZAMAR SENTRY**
(calling out, alarmed)
Movement! Torches on the ridge — that's not a patrol, that's—

He doesn't finish. A horn sounds from the Kasar Dutse side — not an attack signal exactly, but a formal, deliberate call to advance.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. TSAURI KEEP — KASAR DUTSE — NIGHT (SAME TIME)**

GARBA, fully armored for the first time in the series, addressing his assembled war-captains before they ride.

**GARBA**
(hard, controlled)
Six of my soldiers died at that pass twelve weeks ago, over an arrow no one has ever been made to answer for. Zamar's own investigation has produced nothing. Their princes are still arguing in a warm hall while my border villages sleep within bowshot of a line that killed my men and went unpunished.

(beat)

Tonight we hold that pass ourselves. Not to invade Zamar. To protect what's mine, since they have shown me, for twelve weeks, that they either cannot or will not.

**WAR-CAPTAIN**
And if Zamar calls it an invasion regardless of how we name it, my king?

**GARBA**
(a long, grim beat)
Then they call it what they will. I've spent this whole season trying to be the version of strength that doesn't need to prove itself. Tonight, I stop waiting for anyone else to decide when I'm allowed to.

**MAIN TITLES.**

---

### ACT ONE

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — WAR TENT — DAWN**

MALIK, GENERAL TAHIR, and COMMANDER RAFIU, receiving the news. The mood is entirely different from any previous war-room scene — this is real, immediate, no longer theoretical.

**GENERAL TAHIR**
He's taken the pass. Not a raid, not a provocation — an occupation. Zamar's border garrison withdrew rather than be overrun; they were outnumbered three to one.

**MALIK**
(cold, focused — a different Malik than we've seen)
Casualties?

**COMMANDER RAFIU**
Two dead in the withdrawal. It could have been far worse. Whoever ordered the retreat rather than a stand made the right call.

**MALIK**
(a beat, taking it in)
He's calling it self-defense. Six of his dead from Episode Five, unanswered, twelve weeks of us doing nothing but arguing in a hall.

**GENERAL TAHIR**
(carefully)
Is he wrong, my prince?

**MALIK**
(a long, honest silence)
No. That's what makes this so much worse than if he were.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — DIWAN LIBRARY — DAWN**

FATIMA, working late into the night before the news reaches her, has laid out the entire trail across the season — the Kasar Dutse coin, the Kwara silver, Bashir's original audits — alongside something new: a household staff roster from the palace, cross-referenced against who had access to Bashir's study the night he died.

**INSERT — a name on the roster, circled: a mid-level palace steward with access to the service stair identified back in Episode 2.**

**FATIMA**
(to herself, the pieces finally aligning)
Not a soldier. Not a merchant. Someone who works inside these walls, who had the coin and the silver receipts planted for me to find because someone needed me looking outward, at Kasar Dutse and Kwara both, instead of at my own household.

KAMAL enters, grim, before she can finish the thought.

**KAMAL**
Princess. Garba has taken the pass.

Fatima looks up — the two crises, investigation and war, colliding in the same instant.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT ONE.**

### ACT TWO

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — DIWAN HALL — DAY**

An emergency full council, the largest and angriest we've seen. FATIMA and MALIK together, facing a hall demanding action.

**DIWAN ELDER**
He has occupied Zamar territory by force, Princess. Whatever his justification, this is no longer a border dispute. This is war, whether we name it that or not.

**FATIMA**
(steady, though the pressure is visible)
Naming it war doesn't win back the pass, Elder. It commits soldiers who are already exhausted from a skirmish that started the same way — one act, poorly considered, spiraling past anyone's ability to control it.

**MALIK**
(stepping in, the season's clearest command voice yet)
I will not send soldiers to retake that pass by force while my sister's investigation is this close to an actual answer. Six of my men are already dead from exactly this kind of hasty response. I won't spend more before we understand what we're actually spending them for.

A ripple of unhappy murmuring — the hall wants action, and both siblings are, once again, choosing patience over force, at real political cost.

**DIWAN ELDER**
And how close, precisely, is the investigation, Princess? Twelve weeks of patience have bought this council remarkably little certainty.

**FATIMA**
(a beat — deciding, in real time, to finally say it)
Close enough that I no longer believe Kasar Dutse or Kwara killed Elder Bashir. I believe someone inside this palace did, and used both kingdoms' fingerprints to make sure we'd spend a season looking everywhere but here.

The hall goes utterly silent — this is the biggest single revelation Fatima has made publicly all season.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — SERVICE CORRIDOR — DAY**

FATIMA, KAMAL, and two GUARDS approach the steward's quarters identified from the roster. The steward — an older, unassuming man, ABUBAKAR — is not there. His room has been hastily emptied.

**KAMAL**
(surveying the empty room)
Someone warned him.

**FATIMA**
(grim, already suspecting the shape of it)
Or someone told him it was time to disappear before I got here. Either way, that means whoever's actually behind this still has eyes inside my own investigation.

**INSERT — on the floor, half-swept under the bed frame: a small scrap of paper with a partial wax seal, the same unidentified mark Yusuf used in Episode 3.**

Kamal picks it up, studying the fragment, not yet recognizing its significance. Fatima's eyes land on it too — a flicker of something crossing her face, though she says nothing yet.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT TWO.**
