# GIDAN MULKI UNIVERSE
## Season One, Chapter 06 — "A Mother's Silence"

*Directly continues from Chapter 05 ("The Weight of the Crown") — same scene-day continuity, no time skip unless noted in the first scene heading below. Second half of the original Episode 3 ("The Weight of the Crown").*

### ACT ONE

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — QUEEN MOTHER'S QUARTERS — DAY**

A new setting: quiet, book-lined, understated compared to the rest of the palace. QUEEN MOTHER HALIMA — Fatima and Malik's mother, composed, watchful, a woman who has clearly spent decades being careful — receives Fatima.

**HALIMA**
You look like your father did the week before every difficult decision he ever made. Like you already know the answer and you're negotiating with yourself about whether you're allowed to want it.

**FATIMA**
(a small, tired laugh)
I don't know the answer, Mother. That's rather the problem.

**HALIMA**
(gently)
Tell me anyway. I promise you I have kept harder secrets than whatever you're carrying.

Fatima, for the first time in the series, actually lays it out fully — the coin, the silver shipment, Bashir's audits, her fear of accusing Amina too early, her fear of Malik's officers moving without her if she waits too long.

**HALIMA (CONT'D)**
(after listening, carefully)
Your father used to say the crown doesn't reward the person who finds the truth fastest. It rewards the person who survives long enough to be believed once they finally say it out loud.

(beat)

Don't let your brother's officers set your clock for you. And don't let Kwarafi's gold set it either.

**FATIMA**
(quiet)
Whose clock should I let set it, then?

**HALIMA**
(a real, sad smile)
Nobody's. That's the part nobody prepares you for.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. ZAMAR CAVALRY GROUNDS — NIGHT**

MALIK alone this time, the petition from the Teaser still unsigned, spread on a table under lamplight. He stares at it a long time. DANLADI passes at a distance, on some errand, and pauses, seeing him.

**DANLADI**
(hesitant, but stepping closer)
My prince. Word's already moving through the ranks about what you said today.

**MALIK**
(not looking up)
Good things or bad things?

**DANLADI**
Both, honestly. Some men think you're the only honest prince this kingdom's had in a generation. Some think honesty's a fine thing to have when you're not the one standing at a border pass wondering if the men across it are still holding.

**MALIK**
(a beat, genuinely listening)
And you? Which is it for you?

**DANLADI**
(carefully)
I think you meant every word. I also think meaning it doesn't put more soldiers at that pass, and I'd like more soldiers at that pass, my prince, whatever the reason ends up being.

Malik looks at him — a common soldier's plain honesty landing harder than Tahir's polished argument did.

**MALIK**
(quiet)
Noted, soldier. That's the most useful thing anyone's said to me all day.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT ONE.**

### ACT TWO

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — PRIVATE ANTECHAMBER — NIGHT**

FATIMA has sent for MALIK. He arrives, still guarded from Act Two.

**FATIMA**
You asked what I wasn't telling you in the corridor. I'm telling you now.

She lays it all out — exactly as she did for their mother, holding nothing back this time.

**MALIK**
(taking it in, slowly)
Kwara silver, four days before Bashir died, routed through a house he was already auditing. And you sat on that in front of the Diwan today.

**FATIMA**
Because Amina Bello Kwarafi has already offered me her backing once. If I stand up and accuse her house of murder on a single shipment record, and I'm wrong, I've handed every skeptic in that hall a reason to say I only found what was convenient for me to find.

**MALIK**
(a beat — the anger draining into something more like fear)
And if you're right, and you sit on it too long, and something happens to whoever's actually holding the rest of that evidence—

**FATIMA**
(finishing it, grim)
Then I've protected the wrong person out of caution, and it's on me either way. I know, Malik. I've been awake with that thought for three nights.

A long silence between them — the first time in the series either sibling has actually seen how alone the other one is inside their own version of this crisis.

**MALIK**
(quieter, the edge gone)
I'm sorry. For the corridor. For "not here, not here" — I should have trusted there was a reason instead of assuming it was about me.

**FATIMA**
(small, honest)
It was a little about you. I didn't want you doing something with sixty armed signatures because I handed you a name before I was sure of it.

**MALIK**
(a real, tired laugh)
That's fair. I might have.

They sit with that — closer than they've been since the Teaser of Episode 1, and further from a clean answer than ever.

**FATIMA (CONT'D)**
I need more time before I can say a name out loud in that hall. Can you hold Tahir and the rest that long?

**MALIK**
(a beat, then, meaning it)
I held a hundred of them from acclaiming me Sultan this morning. I can hold them a little longer from anything else.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — YUSUF'S QUARTERS — NIGHT**

YUSUF alone — a rare private scene for him. He is writing something by candlelight, unhurried, controlled. We don't see the content. He seals it with a signet we haven't seen before — not Zamar's, not Kasar Dutse's, not Kwara's. Something else entirely.

He holds the sealed letter a moment, studying it, before setting it aside in a locked drawer among several others exactly like it.

**YUSUF**
(quiet, to no one, almost amused)
Patience, patience. Everyone in this palace is so certain that time is the enemy.

He blows out the candle.

**FADE OUT.**

**END OF CHAPTER 06.**

## Notes on the Complete Episode (carried from the original Episode 3 draft)

- Introduces **Queen Mother Halima** — Fatima and Malik's mother, previously only referenced. Per GMU-003 Episode 6's beat ("forced to publicly choose a side, fracturing the royal family"), her Episode 3 scene deliberately establishes her as currently neutral, warm, and wise, so that her forced public choice in Episode 6 lands as a real loss rather than an inevitability. Recommend adding a full Character Bible entry for her (next available principal-cast ID) before Episode 6 is drafted.
- The Kwara silver shipment is Fatima's half of this episode's investigation engine — it does not accuse Amina outright (consistent with Episode 4's beat, where Amina's dual-financing scheme is the actual reveal) but sets up exactly the confrontation Episode 4 requires.
- Malik's refusal to sign the acclamation petition is the episode's clearest "politics before violence" dramatization per GMU-003 Theme 1, and deliberately costs him goodwill with Tahir's faction rather than resolving cleanly — consistent with Theme 3, "every victory has a cost."
- The closing Yusuf scene (unnamed fourth-power signet) is the season's first direct visual confirmation — beyond behavioral hints — that Yusuf answers to an organization distinct from all three known powers. Keep this signet consistent (a specific, describable mark) once concept art is produced, since it needs to pay off cleanly in Episode 9.
- Danladi's brief scene keeps the "ground-level POV" thread alive between his larger beats in Episodes 2 and 8.
