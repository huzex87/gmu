# GIDAN MULKI UNIVERSE
## Season One, Chapter 05 — "The Weight of the Crown"

*Restructured from the original Episode 3 ("The Weight of the Crown") into two shorter chapters for a ~15–20 minute-per-chapter format. This chapter covers the first half of the original episode. Continues in Chapter 06 ("A Mother's Silence").*

**Complete first draft — Teaser through Act Four.** Malik's military faction escalates its pressure toward acclamation — the season's clearest direct test yet of "politics before violence." Fatima uncovers a financial trail tying Kwarafi gold to Bashir's death. First real rupture between the siblings.

---

### TEASER

**FADE IN:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — MALIK'S QUARTERS — DAWN**

MALIK, alone, in half-armor, staring at a folded parchment on the table: a petition, dozens of seals already pressed into the wax at its foot. GENERAL TAHIR waits by the door.

**GENERAL TAHIR**
Forty-one officers. By evening it will be past sixty. Every garrison commander between here and the southern wall has signed asking the army acclaim you Sultan by right of readiness, law or no law.

**MALIK**
(not touching it)
I didn't ask for this.

**GENERAL TAHIR**
No. They're offering it anyway. That's what loyalty looks like when it isn't asked for, my prince — it's rarer than the kind you have to request.

**MALIK**
(a long beat, staring at the seals)
And if I sign nothing, and it happens without me?

**GENERAL TAHIR**
Then it happens without you, and someone else decides what your name means from now on. I would rather it happened with your hand actually on it.

Malik picks up the parchment. Doesn't sign. Doesn't put it down either.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — DIWAN LIBRARY — DAWN**

FATIMA, awake before the household, cross-referencing the trade ledgers from Episode 2 against a second set of documents — customs receipts, dated and stamped.

**INSERT — a receipt: a shipment of Kwara silver, routed through a merchant house, dated four days before Bashir's death.**

**FATIMA**
(to herself, quiet)
Bashir, what were you actually looking at.

She sets the receipt beside the war-coin from Episode 2 — different currencies, same week, same edge of a pattern she still can't see whole.

**MAIN TITLES.**

---

### ACT ONE

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — RECEPTION ROOM — DAY**

FATIMA and KAMAL. She lays out the receipt and the ledgers.

**KAMAL**
(studying it)
A Kwara silver shipment, four days before he died. That is not proof of anything, Princess. Merchants move silver through this city every week of the year.

**FATIMA**
Not usually routed through a house Bashir personally audited twice in the month before his death. He wasn't looking at Kasar Dutse's coin because he thought that was the answer. He was looking at Kwara's silver.

**KAMAL**
(carefully)
Speaker Kwarafi has already offered you her backing. If you go public with a Kwara silver trail now, on top of that arrangement, it will look — to anyone who wants it to — like you're either accusing your own ally, or protecting her by controlling how the accusation surfaces.

**FATIMA**
(a beat — she hadn't fully weighed that)
Either way, it looks like I already know more than I do.

**KAMAL**
Yes.

Fatima sits with that — the trap of her own Episode 1 alliance closing slightly around her.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. ZAMAR CAVALRY GROUNDS — DAY**

MALIK before an assembled body of officers — more than we've seen at once, easily a hundred. He holds the unsigned petition. TAHIR stands a pace behind him.

**MALIK**
(to the assembly)
I know what this parchment says. I know what sixty seals mean when they're pressed by men who've bled for this kingdom and are tired of waiting for old men in the Diwan hall to decide if that's worth anything.

(beat)

I am not signing it today.

A ripple of unhappy murmuring — Tahir's jaw tightens.

**MALIK (CONT'D)**
Not because your loyalty isn't real. Because the day I take this throne by the weight of your swords instead of the weight of the law, I've already lost the only thing that makes it worth having. My father built forty years of peace on people believing the crown answers to something bigger than whoever's strongest that week. I won't be the one who proves them wrong to save myself six weeks of waiting.

**OFFICER (O.S.)**
And if the Diwan chooses her instead?

**MALIK**
(a long, honest beat)
Then it chooses her. And I expect every man who signed that parchment to serve her exactly as well as he'd have served me. That's not a request. That's the whole point of what I just said.

Silence — not agreement, exactly, but something quieter than the murmuring. Tahir watches Malik with an expression hard to read: frustration, and something almost like respect, both at once.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT ONE.**

---

### ACT TWO

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — WAR TENT — DAY**

Malik and Tahir alone, the aftermath of the speech.

**GENERAL TAHIR**
That was either the bravest thing I've seen you do, or the most expensive.

**MALIK**
Can it not be both?

**GENERAL TAHIR**
(almost a smile, gone quickly)
It can. It usually is, with your father's decisions too, if the histories are honest about it.

(beat, harder)

But hear this once, plainly. Some of those sixty men will not forgive being told no in front of a hundred witnesses. If your sister does something in the next thirty days to hand them a better reason than "the law says so," I will not be able to hold all of them a second time.

**MALIK**
(quiet)
I'm not asking you to hold them for her sake. I'm asking for mine.

Tahir studies him a long moment, then nods once — not agreement, just acknowledgment — and leaves.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — DIWAN HALL — DAY**

A formal session — more elders present than Episode 2's small meeting, though still not the full council. FATIMA presents her findings carefully, without naming Kwara directly yet.

**FATIMA**
Elder Bashir's final inquiries touched more than one kingdom's coin. I am not prepared to name a conclusion today. I am prepared to say the investigation is wider than a single planted coin at a border pass, and I would ask this council's patience while it's finished properly.

**DIWAN ELDER**
Patience has a cost too, Princess. Every week this goes unresolved is a week General Tahir's officers have to keep asking why the throne isn't already settled.

**FATIMA**
I'm aware of the cost, Elder. I would rather pay it in time than pay it in being wrong about who we accuse.

The elder studies her — not unsympathetic, but unconvinced. MALIK, present at the back of the hall, watches his sister hold the room, a complicated mix of pride and worry on his face.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — CORRIDOR — DAY (LATER)**

Fatima and Malik, alone for the first time since the Teaser.

**MALIK**
Tahir told me what you said in there. "Wider than a single coin." You know something you didn't put in front of the Diwan.

**FATIMA**
(careful)
I have a thread. Not a conclusion. If I pull it wrong in front of sixty armed men who already resent waiting on me, it tears, and we lose whatever's on the other end of it.

**MALIK**
(an edge creeping in)
Or you're protecting Kwarafi because you need her gold more than you need the truth out fast.

**FATIMA**
(stung, sharp)
That is not what this is.

**MALIK**
Then tell me what it is.

A held beat — Fatima doesn't answer, not because she's hiding it from him specifically, but because Kamal's warning from Act One is still sitting in her, and she genuinely doesn't trust the hallway not to have ears.

**FATIMA**
(quieter, not an answer, an apology shaped like one)
Not here.

Malik looks at her for a long moment — the first time in the season we see him actually doubt her, even slightly.

**MALIK**
(walking away)
That's twice now you've said "not here" to me, Fatima. I'm starting to wonder if there's ever going to be a "here."

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT TWO.**
