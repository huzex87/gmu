# GIDAN MULKI UNIVERSE
## Season One, Chapter 12 — "The Public Vow"

*Directly continues from Chapter 11 ("The Second Covenant") — same scene-day continuity, no time skip unless noted in the first scene heading below. Second half of the original Episode 6 ("The Second Covenant").*

### ACT ONE

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — GRAND COURTYARD — DAY**

A large public gathering has assembled — nobles, clergy, soldiers, ordinary Zamar citizens, drawn by word that the Queen Mother will speak. HALIMA stands on a raised dais, FATIMA and MALIK present but deliberately positioned apart from her and from each other — the staging should visually isolate all three.

**HALIMA**
(to the crowd, composed, though it's clearly costing her)
I have been asked, more than once these past days, to say which of my children should sit my husband's throne. I will not answer that question today, or any day before the Diwan itself has answered it first. That is the law your Sultan believed in for forty years, and I will not dishonor his memory by pretending my own preference should outweigh it.

A ripple of disappointment from some in the crowd — clearly hoping for a clearer answer.

**HALIMA (CONT'D)**
But I will say this, plainly, because I am done being asked to stay silent about it: whichever of my children is ratified, I expect the other to serve Zamar with the same honor either would have shown wearing the crown themselves. If either of you cannot promise me that, standing here, in front of this city — then neither of you has yet earned what you're both fighting for.

She looks directly at Fatima, then Malik — not choosing a side, but demanding something of both of them publicly, which is its own kind of costly choice.

**MALIK**
(stepping forward, without hesitation)
I promise it, Mother. In front of everyone here.

A beat — all eyes shift to Fatima.

**FATIMA**
(a long moment — this costs her something real, saying it in public, binding, unable to be walked back)
I promise it as well.

The crowd's murmur shifts — something like relief, though the tension between the siblings, visible in their body language, hasn't actually resolved, only been forced into a public vow neither can easily break.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — CORRIDOR — DAY (LATER)**

Fatima catches up to Halima, alone, away from the crowd.

**FATIMA**
(quiet, hurt)
You made us promise that in front of the entire city. Not privately. Publicly, where neither of us can take it back without looking like we never meant it.

**HALIMA**
(gently, but not backing down)
Yes. Because privately, promises get renegotiated the moment it's convenient, Fatima — I watched your father do it to his own council more than once, for good reasons and bad ones both. I needed this one to hold no matter which of you wins.

**FATIMA**
(a beat, some of the hurt softening into something more like understanding)
You're protecting both of us. From each other, and from ourselves.

**HALIMA**
(simply)
I'm protecting whichever one of you loses. That is the child who will need it kept most.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT ONE.**

### ACT TWO

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — PRIVATE ANTECHAMBER — NIGHT**

FATIMA and MALIK together, the day's events settling.

**MALIK**
The Covenant, the border dead, Mother forcing us to make that vow in front of half the city. It feels like everyone around us is trying to hold this together harder than we are.

**FATIMA**
(a tired, honest laugh)
Maybe they should be. We've spent this whole season one investigation and one border skirmish away from tearing it apart ourselves.

**MALIK**
(quiet)
Do you think she's right? That whoever loses is going to need protecting?

**FATIMA**
(a long beat, genuinely considering it)
I think whoever loses is going to have to watch the other one wear something that could have been theirs, every single day, for the rest of their life. I don't know what protects a person from that. I don't know if anything does.

They sit with that — no answer, only the shared weight of it.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. GREAT SANCTUARY OF THE COVENANT — NIGHT**

HIGH CLERIC NAFISA alone on the sanctuary steps, looking out over the city. YUSUF approaches — unexpected, unannounced, his presence here a small quiet alarm bell for anyone paying attention.

**YUSUF**
(pleasant, as always)
A fine address today, High Cleric. Very evenhanded. It must be difficult, holding a whole city's faith steady while everyone around you picks a side.

**HIGH CLERIC NAFISA**
(wary, though not visibly suspicious of him specifically — yet)
The Covenant doesn't pick sides, Advisor al-Kanem. That's rather the point of what I said today.

**YUSUF**
(a small, unreadable smile)
Of course. I only meant to say — should the Covenant ever need a quiet, discreet channel to either faction, one that doesn't require a public sermon to deliver its message, I would be glad to be of use.

**HIGH CLERIC NAFISA**
(a beat, something in his phrasing catching her attention properly for the first time)
That's a curious offer from an advisor whose loyalty, as far as I've ever heard, belongs entirely to the Crown.

**YUSUF**
(unbothered, already turning to go)
Loyalty is a complicated word, High Cleric. I find it means something slightly different to everyone who uses it.

He leaves her on the steps, unsettled — the first time in the series someone outside the palace has registered, even faintly, that something about Yusuf doesn't add up.

**FADE OUT.**

**END OF CHAPTER 12.**

## Notes on the Complete Episode (carried from the original Episode 6 draft)

- Delivers the GMU-003 Episode 6 beat precisely: the Covenant clergy raise the season's central theme ("politics before violence" / legitimacy through law) to open, public text, and Halima is forced into a public choice that fractures — or at minimum visibly strains — the royal family, per the beat sheet's description.
- Introduces **High Cleric Nafisa**, a new named clergy figure who should receive a Character Bible entry given her structural importance (she is the season's clearest voice of the "knowledge is power, literally" theme from outside the palace's political factions) and her direct scene with Yusuf, which plants a second character's suspicion of him ahead of the Episode 9 reveal.
- Halima's public dais scene is the direct dramatization of "forced to publicly choose a side" — note that she deliberately does NOT choose between Fatima and Malik, but instead forces a mutual public vow from both; this is intentional, reframing the beat sheet's language as "forced to make a public choice" rather than "forced to pick a winner," which better serves the finale's ambiguity. Flag this interpretation for Showrunner review against the original beat sheet intent.
- The closing Yusuf/Nafisa scene is this episode's seed for the "unseen fourth power" thread reaching beyond the palace and Kwara (Episode 4's ending) to now include the Covenant's own awareness — three independent threads (Fatima's suspicion, Amina's investigation, now Nafisa's unease) all converging toward Episode 9.
- No armed conflict in this episode by design, consistent with its focus on legitimacy, faith, and family rather than battlefield stakes — a deliberate tonal breather after Episode 5's skirmish.
