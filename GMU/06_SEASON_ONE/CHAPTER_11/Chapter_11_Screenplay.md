# GIDAN MULKI UNIVERSE
## Season One, Chapter 11 — "The Second Covenant"

*Restructured from the original Episode 6 ("The Second Covenant") into two shorter chapters for a ~15–20 minute-per-chapter format. This chapter covers the first half of the original episode. Continues in Chapter 12 ("The Public Vow").*

**Complete first draft — Teaser through Act Four.** The Covenant clergy publicly threaten to withhold religious legitimacy from anyone who takes the throne by force rather than law, raising the season's central theme to open text. Fatima and Malik's mother, previously neutral, is forced to publicly choose a side, fracturing the royal family in view of the whole city.

---

### TEASER

**FADE IN:**

**INT. GREAT SANCTUARY OF THE COVENANT — BIRNIN ZAMAR — DAWN**

A vast, austere hall of worship, unlike anywhere we've seen in the series — high windows, no throne, no war tent, only rows of scholars and clergy in prayer. HIGH CLERIC NAFISA, an elder cleric we haven't formally met, leads a small gathering in the pre-dawn devotion.

**HIGH CLERIC NAFISA**
(to the gathered clergy, quiet but carrying)
Six soldiers buried this week. A crown still unclaimed after fifty days when the law allows forty. And a prince and princess who have, to their credit, refused to seize by force what the law has not yet given them.

(beat)

We have stayed silent on the succession itself, as is proper. We will not stay silent any longer on what almost happened at that border.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — MALIK'S QUARTERS — DAWN**

MALIK receiving word from a CLERGY MESSENGER — a formal summons, sealed with the Covenant's own mark rather than the Diwan's.

**CLERGY MESSENGER**
The High Cleric requests your presence, and the Princess's, at the Great Sanctuary today. A public address. She would not say more than that it concerns the succession directly.

Malik reads the summons twice, uneasy.

**MALIK**
(to himself)
The clergy don't summon princes for pleasant news.

**MAIN TITLES.**

---

### ACT ONE

**INT. GREAT SANCTUARY OF THE COVENANT — BIRNIN ZAMAR — DAY**

A packed public gathering — nobles, soldiers, merchants, ordinary citizens, more people in one place than the series has shown before. FATIMA and MALIK stand before HIGH CLERIC NAFISA and a row of senior clergy. KAMAL and General TAHIR are both present, on opposite sides of the hall — a visual the staging should make pointed.

**HIGH CLERIC NAFISA**
(to the assembly)
Fifty days ago, Sultan Adamu the Third died, and this kingdom's law gave his children forty days to be judged worthy of his throne by the Diwan al-Ilm. That window has passed without a ratification. In its place, we have watched a murdered elder go unavenged, a border stained with the blood of six soldiers, and rumors — growing louder by the week — that the crown may yet be decided by whichever faction is willing to stop waiting.

(beat, her voice hardening)

Let it be said plainly, before this whole city: the Covenant will not grant its blessing, nor recognize the legitimacy, of any ruler who takes Zamar's throne by force rather than by law. Not Prince Malik, should his officers act without him. Not Princess Fatima, should the Diwan's patience be pressured past its honest judgment. Not any foreign king who mistakes our crisis for an opportunity.

A ripple moves through the hall — this is, functionally, the clergy publicly weaponizing their own legitimacy against every faction at once, including the two people standing in front of them.

**FATIMA**
(quiet, to Malik, as the murmuring rises)
She just told the entire city that neither of us has her blessing yet. That's not a warning. That's a countdown, said out loud.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. GREAT SANCTUARY — PRIVATE CHAMBER — DAY (LATER)**

Fatima and Malik alone with HIGH CLERIC NAFISA.

**MALIK**
With respect, High Cleric — I have refused acclamation twice this season for exactly the principle you just preached at me in front of half the city.

**HIGH CLERIC NAFISA**
(not unkindly, but firm)
I know, Prince Malik. I chose today, in front of that crowd, because you have refused it twice. I need it to hold a third time, and a fourth, for as long as this takes — and public pressure is a more reliable enforcer of that than my private confidence in your character.

**FATIMA**
(sharp)
And what pressure does the Covenant intend to put on the actual murder investigation, High Cleric? On Kasar Dutse's border seizure of six lives? Or is legitimacy only a lever you pull against the throne, never against the kingdoms circling it?

Nafisa studies Fatima for a long moment — genuine respect, and genuine unease at being challenged this directly.

**HIGH CLERIC NAFISA**
(carefully)
A fair question, Princess. One I don't have a fully honest answer to yet. Come back to me with that question once you're ratified, and I promise you an honest one.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT ONE.**

---

### ACT TWO

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — QUEEN MOTHER'S QUARTERS — DAY**

HALIMA, alone, has clearly heard about the Sanctuary address already. A COVENANT ELDER — a different figure from Nafisa, more political than devotional — waits with her, mid-conversation as Fatima enters.

**COVENANT ELDER**
(to Halima, not noticing Fatima yet)
The city remembers you, my lady. Adamu's widow, respected by both factions precisely because you have chosen neither. A word from you, publicly, in support of the law over either sibling's claim by force, would carry weight the High Cleric's sermon alone cannot.

**HALIMA**
(seeing Fatima, a flicker of guilt)
Fatima.

**FATIMA**
(carefully neutral, though clearly stung)
Mother. I didn't realize the Covenant was already asking you to weigh in.

**COVENANT ELDER**
(diplomatically)
Not in support of either claimant, Princess. Only in support of the law itself. Your mother's voice could steady this city at a moment it badly needs steadying.

**FATIMA**
(a beat, to her mother, more vulnerable than her public voice)
And if steadying the city means being seen standing apart from both your children, in public, while the whole kingdom watches to see which way you actually lean?

**HALIMA**
(quiet, honest)
Then that is exactly what it will look like, whatever I actually intend by it. I know that, Fatima. I have known it since the morning your father died.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. ZAMAR CAVALRY GROUNDS — DAY**

MALIK and TAHIR, the Sanctuary address's fallout already spreading through the ranks.

**GENERAL TAHIR**
The clergy just told sixty officers, publicly, that their loyalty to you means nothing without a Diwan seal attached to it. Some of them are asking me tonight whether that means their oath was worthless all along.

**MALIK**
(steady, though it costs him)
Tell them it means exactly what I told them in that speech three weeks ago. The crown that's worth having is the one that answers to something bigger than us. The Covenant just said the same thing, louder, to a bigger room. That's not a betrayal of what I asked of them. It's confirmation.

**GENERAL TAHIR**
(a long look, something almost like real loyalty in it now)
You believe that.

**MALIK**
(simply)
I have to. It's the only thing I've been consistent about all season.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT TWO.**
