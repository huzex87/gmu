# GIDAN MULKI UNIVERSE
## Season One, Chapter 08 — "A Costly Loyalty"

*Directly continues from Chapter 07 ("The Merchant's Price") — same scene-day continuity, no time skip unless noted in the first scene heading below. Second half of the original Episode 4 ("The Merchant's Price").*

### ACT ONE

**INT. KWARAFI MERCHANT HOUSE — COUNTING HALL — DAY (LATER)**

FATIMA, now composed again, returns to the main hall where Amina is conducting other business. Fatima waits until Amina notices her, then speaks loudly enough for nearby clerks to register it — a calculated move.

**FATIMA**
Speaker Kwarafi. I'd like to discuss the terms of Kwara's backing formally, in writing, witnessed by my own Diwan clerk. Not a private understanding anymore. A contract.

Amina's eyes narrow slightly — recognizing the move for what it is: Fatima taking the ambiguity Amina relies on and forcing it into daylight.

**AMINA**
(carefully)
That's a bold request, Princess, given what you just learned about my other arrangements.

**FATIMA**
(steady)
It's precisely because of what I just learned. I'd rather have your loyalty priced honestly than assumed generously. Tell me what you actually want.

**AMINA**
(a long beat, then, genuinely)
Trade rights. Exclusive river-tariff terms through the Zamar passes for twenty years, the moment you're ratified. Not before. I don't gamble on unfinished outcomes twice in one season.

**FATIMA**
(the weight of it landing)
Twenty years is a generation of Zamar's trade income signed away before I've even sat on the throne.

**AMINA**
(not unkind, simply factual)
Power is earned, Princess. Isn't that the phrase your father's house is so fond of? I'm merely quoting the price back to you.

Fatima says nothing for a long moment — this is the episode's title landing directly on her, the weight of what winning is actually going to cost.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. RIVER CONFEDERACY OF KWARA — TRADE QUARTER — DAY**

Fatima and Kamal walking back toward the barge, the negotiation unresolved behind them.

**KAMAL**
You didn't sign anything.

**FATIMA**
No. I wanted her to say the number out loud in front of witnesses first. Numbers said quietly get renegotiated. Numbers said in a hall full of clerks tend to stick.

**KAMAL**
(a flicker of real respect)
That was well played, Princess.

**FATIMA**
(grim)
It didn't feel like playing. It felt like pricing my own crown before I've won it.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT ONE.**

---

### ACT TWO

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — PRIVATE ANTECHAMBER — NIGHT**

FATIMA tells MALIK what she learned — Amina's dual financing, the twenty-year tariff demand, all of it.

**MALIK**
(stunned, then something harder)
She financed both of us. The whole time. And you're telling me you're still negotiating with her?

**FATIMA**
I'm telling you I'd rather negotiate with her in the open than let her keep playing us against each other in the dark. That ends today, one way or another.

**MALIK**
(a beat, then, quieter, guilty)
I did something today I'm not proud of either. I bought Commander Rafiu's loyalty. Not with conviction — with coin, and back-pay, and a promise I had to reach into my own household to keep.

**FATIMA**
(surprised, then, gently)
After the speech. After telling a hundred officers you'd rather lose the crown than take it by force.

**MALIK**
(honest)
I still believe every word of that speech. I also believe a soldier's son shouldn't eat half rations while I make speeches. Maybe those two things can't both be true forever. Today they were.

Fatima looks at her brother — both of them, in their own ways, having spent a piece of the principle they started this season holding onto.

**FATIMA**
(quiet)
Father used to say every victory has a cost. I don't think either of us has actually won anything yet, and we're already paying it.

**MALIK**
(a tired, real smile)
Welcome to the family business.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. RIVER CONFEDERACY OF KWARA — AMINA'S BALCONY — NIGHT**

AMINA alone, looking out over the canal-lit city, a cup of wine in hand. Her STEWARD approaches with a sealed letter.

**STEWARD**
From Zamar, Speaker. Unmarked seal.

Amina opens it — reads it in silence, her expression shifting from curiosity to something colder, more careful.

**AMINA**
(quiet, almost to herself)
Well. That's a name I haven't seen attached to Zamar's business in some time.

She folds the letter closed, tucking it away rather than discarding it — clearly something she intends to use, not react to immediately.

**AMINA (CONT'D)**
(to the Steward)
Send word to my agents in Birnin Zamar. Quietly. I want to know everything they can find about an advisor named Yusuf al-Kanem.

**FADE OUT.**

**END OF CHAPTER 08.**

## Notes on the Complete Episode (carried from the original Episode 4 draft)

- Confirms Amina's dual-financing scheme exactly per the GMU-003 Episode 4 beat, and reframes her Episode 1 offer to Fatima retroactively — a deliberate structural echo, not a contradiction, since Amina's own dialogue explicitly owns the move as standard Kwara practice rather than personal betrayal.
- Introduces **Commander Rafiu**, a new supporting-cast military figure whose loyalty costs Malik something real and specific (back-pay redirected from his own household) — this is the episode's clearest "every victory has a cost" beat for Malik, mirroring Fatima's cost with Amina. Recommend a Character Bible entry if Rafiu recurs past Episode 5's border skirmish.
- The formalized Kwara contract negotiation (Act Three) sets up the season finale's costly trade concession (Episode 10) directly — the twenty-year tariff terms proposed here should be the same terms Fatima ultimately signs in Episode 10, for continuity.
- The closing Amina scene is the episode's plant for Episode 9's reveal: Amina investigating Yusuf independently gives the audience a second, non-Fatima thread converging on the same mystery, and explains how Kwara ends up with leverage or information relevant to the finale.
- No armed conflict in this episode by design — per GMU-003's tone note, Season One has only two armed clashes (Episodes 5 and 8/finale-adjacent), and Episode 4 is deliberately pure negotiation and financial intrigue.
