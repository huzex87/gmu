# GIDAN MULKI UNIVERSE
## Season One, Chapter 18 — "The Confession"

*Directly continues from Chapter 17 ("The Diwan Convenes") — same scene-day continuity, no time skip unless noted in the first scene heading below. Second half of the original Episode 9 ("The Diwan Convenes").*

### ACT ONE

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — DIWAN HALL — DAY**

A tense, formal interrogation before KAMAL, FATIMA, and MALIK, with senior Diwan elders present. YUSUF, restrained but composed, answers with the same eerie calm.

**KAMAL**
Who do you serve, Advisor al-Kanem. Not Zamar. Not Kasar Dutse. Not Kwara. Say the name.

**YUSUF**
(a small, genuine smile — the first time all season he seems to be enjoying himself)
There isn't a name you'd recognize, Elder Kamal. We have gone by many, across many courts, across more years than this kingdom has existed. We do not seize thrones. We do not fight wars. We simply ensure that when a throne is won, it is won by someone who has already learned exactly what winning costs — because a ruler who has paid that price in full is a ruler who can be reasoned with, later, by people who understand the ledger of what they owe.

**DIWAN ELDER**
(alarmed)
You're describing an organization operating inside every kingdom on this continent.

**YUSUF**
(mild)
I'm describing patience, Elder. Something none of you have shown much talent for this season.

**MALIK**
(quiet, furious, stepping forward)
You used my sister. You used me. You let six of my soldiers die at that border so we'd spend a season distrusting each other instead of governing.

**YUSUF**
(looking at Malik directly, almost gently)
I didn't cause your distrust, Prince Malik. I only removed the reasons you both had to pretend it wasn't there. That distrust was always going to surface, the moment real power was actually on the line. I simply chose the timing.

Malik says nothing — the horrifying possibility that Yusuf might be partly right sitting visibly on his face.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — PRIVATE ANTECHAMBER — DAY (LATER)**

Fatima and Malik alone, reeling from the interrogation.

**MALIK**
(quiet, disillusioned)
Even knowing everything he did — the coin, the silver, Bashir, Rafiu's back-pay decision I still don't fully forgive myself for — I keep thinking about the one thing he said that I can't argue with. That the distrust between us was always there. That he only removed the reasons we had to pretend otherwise.

**FATIMA**
(gently, but firm)
He's a murderer and a manipulator who wants you to believe that, Malik, because a prince who believes his own doubts were inevitable is a prince who stops fighting them. Don't hand him that victory on top of everything else he's already taken from this season.

**MALIK**
(a long beat — and then, the episode's defining choice)
I've decided something. When the Diwan convenes for the ratification vote — I'm withdrawing my claim.

**FATIMA**
(stunned)
Malik—

**MALIK**
(steady, having clearly thought this through)
Not because you've won some argument, and not because of anything Yusuf said. Because I spent this whole season insisting the crown that's worth having is the one that answers to something bigger than the man wearing it. You've spent this whole season being exactly that kind of ruler — patient when it cost you, honest when it would have been easier not to be, careful with power even when everyone around you was begging you to be reckless with it. I can't say the same about myself. I bought a general's loyalty with coin. I nearly sent soldiers to retake a pass out of anger instead of proof. I am not ready to be the Sultan I've spent all season saying Zamar deserves. You are.

**FATIMA**
(overwhelmed, quiet)
That's not a small thing to give away, Malik.

**MALIK**
(a real, sad, genuine smile)
No. It's the biggest thing I have. That's rather the point of giving it.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT ONE.**

### ACT TWO

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — DIWAN HALL — DAY**

The full Diwan al-Ilm convenes — the largest, most formal gathering of the season, mirroring Episode 1's opening scale but transformed by everything since. HIGH CLERIC NAFISA presides alongside KAMAL. FATIMA stands before the council. MALIK stands beside her rather than opposite her, having withdrawn.

**KAMAL**
(formally, to the assembly)
Prince Malik ibn Adamu has withdrawn his claim to the throne, freely and by his own word, before this full council. The matter before the Diwan al-Ilm today is therefore the ratification of Princess Fatima bint Adamu as Sultana of Zamar.

A murmur moves through the hall — history being made in real time, the same skepticism from Episode 1 about a woman on Zamar's throne now tested against everything the audience has watched her survive.

**HIGH CLERIC NAFISA**
(to the assembly)
The Covenant will not withhold its blessing from a ruler ratified by law, however unprecedented. Let the council vote its true conscience, not its habit.

**DIWAN ELDER**
(the same skeptical elder from Episode 3, now, visibly, having reconsidered)
Princess Fatima uncovered a conspiracy that has manipulated three kingdoms for reasons we are still only beginning to understand. She did it with patience, with law, and — when it mattered — with courage. I did not expect to say this three months ago. I say it plainly now: she has earned this council's judgment, whatever that judgment ultimately is.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — HOLDING CHAMBER — DAY**

YUSUF, restrained, awaiting his fate elsewhere in the palace while the vote proceeds. KAMAL visits him, alone, one final exchange.

**KAMAL**
The vote is happening without you, Yusuf. Whatever you hoped to shape, you'll watch it finish from a cell.

**YUSUF**
(entirely unbothered, even now)
I don't need to watch it, Elder Kamal. I already know how it ends. She wins. She rules well, for a time, exactly as we intended. And somewhere, years from now, when Zamar's peace is tested again by something none of you can currently imagine, she will remember tonight, and she will remember exactly what surviving it cost her.

(beat, almost gently)

We are very patient, Elder. This was never about today.

Kamal says nothing, unsettled despite himself, and leaves.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — DIWAN HALL — DAY (LATER)**

The vote concludes. KAMAL reads the result to the assembled hall.

**KAMAL**
By the judgment of the Diwan al-Ilm, and the law of Zamar's founding covenant — Fatima bint Adamu is ratified. Zamar's first Sultana.

The hall erupts — not universal celebration, but something real: relief, history, uncertainty, all at once. FATIMA stands at the center of it, MALIK beside her, applauding along with the rest, his face a complicated mixture of grief and genuine pride.

Fatima's eyes find Malik's across the noise — a silent exchange neither of them could have imagined at the start of the season: gratitude, guilt, love, and something that isn't quite closure, all held at once.

**FADE OUT.**

**END OF CHAPTER 18.**

## Notes on the Complete Episode (carried from the original Episode 9 draft)

- Delivers all three GMU-003 Episode 9 beats: the ratification vote occurs, Yusuf's loyalty and the unseen fourth power are exposed (deliberately without a proper name, per GMU-003 Section 6's note that this is "revealed Episode 9" but the organization itself stays unnamed "so Season Two can be built around the reveal"), and Malik makes his costly, defining choice — reinterpreted here as a voluntary withdrawal of his claim rather than a loss at vote, which the Notes recommend as the stronger, more thematically consistent choice given his season-long arc (flag for Showrunner confirmation against original intent).
- Partially resolves the Sultan's death mystery per GMU-003 Section 6 ("resolved partially in Episode 9; fully resolved only in Season Two") — Yusuf explicitly denies causing Adamu III's death while admitting to exploiting the resulting instability, leaving open exactly how much the fourth power knew or orchestrated in advance, reserved for Season Two.
- Abubakar is confirmed as Bashir's actual killer, acting on Yusuf's instruction — closing the season's central murder mystery while keeping Yusuf's own hands technically (and chillingly) clean, consistent with his characterization all season.
- Yusuf's closing monologue to Kamal is deliberately unsettling rather than triumphant or defeated — he is neither gloating nor broken, reinforcing that the fourth power's real defeat (or victory) is not something this season is built to resolve.
- The ratification vote scene deliberately calls back to Episode 3's skeptical Diwan Elder, giving him a redemption beat that pays off Fatima's season-long patience-over-force approach.
