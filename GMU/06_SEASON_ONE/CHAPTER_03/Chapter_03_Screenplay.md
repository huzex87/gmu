# GIDAN MULKI UNIVERSE
## Season One, Chapter 03 — "Blood and Ink"

*Restructured from the original Episode 2 ("Blood and Ink") into two shorter chapters for a ~15–20 minute-per-chapter format. This chapter covers the first half of the original episode. Continues in Chapter 04 ("The Coin and the Crown").*

**Complete first draft — Teaser through Act Four.** Picks up immediately after Episode 1's discovery of Elder Bashir's body. Per GMU-003 Section 7's editorial note, this episode absorbs what was originally scoped as Episode 3's opening (the murder investigation, Kasar Dutse's cavalry reaching the border pass) — those beats now belong here rather than to a separate episode.

---

### TEASER

**FADE IN:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — ELDER BASHIR'S STUDY — MORNING**

The room exactly as Episode 1 left it — Bashir still at the desk, guards at the door, the overturned inkwell untouched. KAMAL stands over the scene, older and stiller than we've seen him. A DIWAN CLERK hovers with a wax seal.

**KAMAL**
(to the Clerk)
Seal it. No one enters without my word or the princess's — not a servant, not a guard captain, not a Diwan elder, myself excepted.

**CLERK**
And the body, Elder?

**KAMAL**
(a beat — this costs him something)
Let him be moved with the same care we'd give a Sultan. He served this Diwan longer than most of us have been alive.

The Clerk presses the wax seal against the doorframe. Kamal doesn't watch — he's looking at Bashir's empty chair like it might still have an answer in it.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — CORRIDOR — MOMENTS LATER**

FATIMA arrives at speed, still in yesterday's mourning white, unslept. MALIK is already there, arguing quietly with two GUARDS blocking the sealed door.

**MALIK**
(to the guards)
I understand Elder Kamal's order. I am also the son of the man that law exists to protect, and I would like five minutes in a room where an old friend of this family was murdered.

**FATIMA**
(arriving)
Malik.

**MALIK**
(not turning)
They won't let either of us in.

**FATIMA**
(to the guards, calmer, colder)
Then send for Kamal. Tell him the two people with the most reason on earth to want this solved are standing outside his door, and he can either let us help or explain to the whole Diwan tomorrow why he didn't.

A guard hesitates, then goes. Fatima and Malik stand together outside the sealed room — an echo of standing together outside their father's chamber, one episode and one lifetime ago.

**MALIK**
(quiet)
We keep ending up outside doors we're not allowed through.

**FATIMA**
(not quite a joke)
Get used to it. That's most of what ruling actually is.

**MAIN TITLES.**

### ACT ONE

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — ELDER BASHIR'S STUDY — DAY**

The seal broken. KAMAL admits Fatima and Malik together — a rare image, the two of them on the same side of a doorway. The body has been moved; the room is otherwise untouched.

**KAMAL**
Nothing leaves this room, nothing is moved, and nothing either of you finds here becomes a weapon against the other. I am trusting you both with this because I have no better option, not because I've stopped being careful.

**FATIMA**
Understood.

**MALIK**
(genuinely)
Thank you, Elder.

Kamal withdraws to the doorway — present, but letting them work.

**FATIMA**
(moving to the desk)
He was writing something. The letter's gone. The ledgers he told me about are gone. Whoever did this either read enough to know what mattered, or took everything rather than risk missing it.

**MALIK**
(examining the door)
No forced entry. Bashir let them in, or they already had a way in that didn't need him to.

**FATIMA**
(a beat — this lands)
Or someone with a standing reason to be in and out of this study at any hour, that no one would think to question.

They look at each other — both arriving at the same uncomfortable shape of a thought, neither saying Yusuf's name yet, because neither has anything but instinct.

**INSERT — Fatima's hand, running along the underside of the desk drawer. It catches on something.**

**FATIMA (CONT'D)**
(pulling it free)
A coin.

She holds it up — small, stamped with a mark neither of them immediately places.

**MALIK**
(taking it, turning it over)
That's not Zamar mint. Look at the strike.

**FATIMA**
(recognizing it, slowly)
That's a Kasar Dutse war-coin. Minted for paying mercenary escorts through the passes. It shouldn't be anywhere near a Diwan elder's study.

**MALIK**
(quiet, the military instinct in him lighting up)
Unless someone wanted us to find it.

**FATIMA**
(sharper)
Or it's exactly what it looks like, and we're the ones about to be too clever to see it.

A held beat — the episode's real question, stated plainly: is this evidence, or bait.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. ZAMAR–KASAR DUTSE BORDER PASS — DAY**

A narrow mountain corridor, marker stones driven into the ground at intervals. On one side, a small ZAMAR BORDER DETACHMENT — among them, DANLADI, further from the palace than we've seen him, watching the pass with the flat attention of a soldier who's done this rotation before and knows it isn't usually tense.

Across the pass: the leading edge of GARBA's cavalry column from Episode 1, halted in disciplined ranks exactly at the marker stones — not one hoof past them.

**ZAMAR OFFICER**
(low, to Danladi)
Count them.

**DANLADI**
(counting, unsettled)
More than a routine patrol. A lot more.

**ZAMAR OFFICER**
They're not crossing.

**DANLADI**
They don't have to cross for it to be a message.

The two forces hold position, watching each other across the stones — nobody moves, nobody speaks across the gap. The wind is the only sound.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT ONE.**

### ACT TWO

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — DIWAN LIBRARY — DAY**

FATIMA alone at a reading table, the Kasar Dutse coin in front of her next to a spread of trade ledgers. YUSUF enters, unhurried as always, two cups of tea in hand — an echo of Bashir's habit that neither of them acknowledges out loud.

**YUSUF**
(setting a cup down)
Kamal told me what you found. A war-coin, in a scholar's study. It doesn't sit right.

**FATIMA**
(watching him carefully, giving nothing away)
No. It doesn't.

**YUSUF**
If you'd like, I can quietly ask after any Kasar Dutse agents who've passed through the city these last weeks. Discreetly — nothing that reaches the Diwan floor before you're ready.

**FATIMA**
(a beat — testing him, gently)
Discreetly. The same way you were discreet with Bashir's ledger requests.

**YUSUF**
(without missing a beat, mild)
I was protecting your father's final weeks from becoming public speculation while the succession was still undecided. I'd make the same choice again, and I'll accept that it looks worse now than it did a week ago.

(beat)

I understand if that answer isn't enough for you.

**FATIMA**
(carefully neutral)
It's an answer. I haven't decided if it's enough.

Yusuf inclines his head, entirely unbothered, and leaves the tea. Fatima watches him go — Bashir's warning from Episode 1 sitting behind her eyes the whole time.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. ZAMAR CAVALRY GROUNDS — DAY**

MALIK moving through the ranks — not the war tent this time, the open training ground from Episode 1, talking to ordinary soldiers rather than senior officers. Warmer, more like the Malik we met first.

**MALIK**
(to a group of soldiers)
I'm not asking you to choose a side today. I'm asking you to remember that whatever happens in that council hall, we don't become the kind of army that solves it with a blade before the law's had its say.

**SOLDIER**
(genuine)
Even with Kasar Dutse cavalry sitting at the pass, my prince?

**MALIK**
(a beat, honest)
Especially then. That's exactly when it's easy to forget who we're supposed to be.

Off to the side, watching, GENERAL TAHIR — not pleased, but not moving to interrupt either.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. ZAMAR CAVALRY GROUNDS — WAR TENT — DAY (LATER)**

Malik and Tahir alone now, map table between them, the mood harder than the previous scene.

**GENERAL TAHIR**
You gave a very fine speech to men who'll be dead before the Diwan finishes debating procedure, if Kasar Dutse decides those marker stones are a suggestion rather than a treaty.

**MALIK**
Garba hasn't crossed. He won't, unless we hand him a reason.

**GENERAL TAHIR**
A Diwan elder is dead with a Kasar Dutse coin under his desk. If that's not a reason, my prince, I don't know what you're waiting for.

**MALIK**
(steady, but it costs him)
I'm waiting for proof instead of a coin someone could have left there on purpose. I'm waiting for my sister to finish looking before I decide whether to start a war on Kasar Dutse's behalf, since apparently that's what finding it too fast would actually be.

Tahir studies him — this is the sharpest disagreement they've had, and for once Malik doesn't flinch from it.

**GENERAL TAHIR**
(quiet, almost approving despite himself)
Your father used to argue with me exactly like that.

**MALIK**
(a small, tired smile)
I'll take that as a compliment until you tell me otherwise.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — RECEPTION ROOM — DAY**

FATIMA alone, a sealed letter and a small wrapped gift on the table in front of her — delivered, per the STEWARD who lingers at the door, "with Speaker Kwarafi's compliments."

**STEWARD**
She said you'd understand it was a gesture, not a reminder.

**FATIMA**
(dry, unwrapping it — a fine but plain piece of Kwara silverwork)
Amina Bello Kwarafi doesn't send gestures. She sends invoices that haven't been itemized yet.

(reading the letter, her expression tightening slightly)

She's asking, very politely, whether our arrangement is still something I intend to honor once I'm ratified. In writing. Which means she wants something she can hold over me later if I don't.

She sets both aside — unresolved, a debt she can't pay yet and can't refuse yet either.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT TWO.**
