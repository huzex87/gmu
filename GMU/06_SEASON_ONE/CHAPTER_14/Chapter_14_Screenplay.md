# GIDAN MULKI UNIVERSE
## Season One, Chapter 14 — "A Flag of Parley"

*Directly continues from Chapter 13 ("Tsauri Pass") — same scene-day continuity, no time skip unless noted in the first scene heading below. Second half of the original Episode 7 ("Tsauri Pass").*

### ACT ONE

**EXT. TSAURI KEEP — KASAR DUTSE — DAY**

GARBA, having taken the pass, receives a ZAMAR HERALD under a flag of parley.

**ZAMAR HERALD**
Prince Malik sends this message, King Garba: Zamar will not retake the pass by force today. He asks only that you allow a delegation safe passage to inspect the border villages under your new occupation, to confirm no further harm comes to Zamar citizens caught on the wrong side of the line.

**GARBA**
(considering it — genuinely, not as a trap)
Tell your prince I grant it. Tell him also that I have no intention of pushing further than this pass. I took what was already contested and cost my kingdom six lives. I have no appetite to take what was never mine to begin with.

**ZAMAR HERALD**
Some in Zamar will not believe that, King Garba.

**GARBA**
(a hard, tired look)
Some in my own court don't fully believe it either. That is the cost of the choice I made last night, Herald. I'll carry it either way.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — PRIVATE ANTECHAMBER — DAY**

FATIMA and MALIK, the day's events — the pass seizure, the vanished steward, the sealed fragment — laid between them.

**MALIK**
(examining the wax fragment Kamal recovered)
This mark. I've seen it before. On a letter I glimpsed on Yusuf's desk once, months ago — I didn't think anything of it at the time, he's always sealing correspondence for father's old business.

**FATIMA**
(the pieces snapping into place, cold)
Yusuf uses a seal that isn't Zamar's, isn't Kasar Dutse's, isn't Kwara's. He was the one who suggested discreetly investigating a Kasar Dutse trail in Episode 2. He had access to father's ledgers before anyone thought to question it. He passes in and out of this palace at every hour, on business no one ever fully verifies.

**MALIK**
(the weight of it landing)
You think Yusuf killed Bashir.

**FATIMA**
(careful, still not fully certain)
I think Yusuf knows who did, at minimum. I am not ready to say his name in that hall the way I said "someone inside this palace" today. Not until I have more than a scrap of wax and a bad feeling.

**MALIK**
(grim)
Then we watch him. Carefully. Starting now.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT ONE.**

---

### ACT TWO

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — YUSUF'S QUARTERS — NIGHT**

YUSUF, unaware — or perhaps entirely aware — that he is now being watched, calmly reviewing correspondence by candlelight, exactly as in Episode 3. A KNOCK at the door. It's KAMAL.

**KAMAL**
Advisor al-Kanem. The princess would like your counsel on tomorrow's response to Kasar Dutse's pass seizure.

**YUSUF**
(unhurried, gathering his papers — including, deliberately, sliding the locked drawer's contents out of casual sight)
Of course, Elder Kamal. Always glad to be of use.

He follows Kamal out. The camera lingers a beat longer on the room than it needs to — on the locked drawer, now empty of anything visible, Yusuf having clearly moved whatever mattered before answering the door.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — DIWAN HALL — NIGHT**

FATIMA, alone in the empty hall after the day's chaos, looking at a wall map of the kingdom — the pass now marked as Kasar Dutse-held, a small, real loss made visible.

**FATIMA**
(quiet, to herself)
Twelve weeks. A murdered elder, a burning border, and the answer was never on the other side of either one. It was here the entire time.

**KAMAL (O.S.)**
(entering)
Princess. Advisor al-Kanem is here, as requested.

Fatima turns — YUSUF entering behind Kamal, composed, unreadable as ever, meeting her eyes across the hall.

**YUSUF**
(pleasant, giving nothing away)
You wanted my counsel, Princess?

**FATIMA**
(matching his composure exactly, giving nothing away herself)
I did, Yusuf. I have a great many questions tonight.

A long, charged beat between them — the audience now aware of what Fatima suspects, and Yusuf's expression giving absolutely nothing back.

**FADE OUT.**

**END OF CHAPTER 14.**

## Notes on the Complete Episode (carried from the original Episode 7 draft)

- Delivers both GMU-003 Episode 7 beats precisely: Garba's unambiguous pass seizure (framed, per Theme 4, as morally defensible from his POV rather than simple villainy) and Fatima's investigation turning decisively inward, culminating in the first direct, named suspicion of Yusuf by both principal siblings.
- Introduces **Abubakar**, the vanished palace steward — the actual hands-on operative behind the coin/silver plants and likely Bashir's death, distinct from Yusuf, who functions as the mastermind/handler. This creates a clean two-tier reveal structure for Episode 9: Abubakar as the instrument, Yusuf (and the unnamed fourth power) as the true architect. Recommend a background-character or minor Character Bible entry for Abubakar given his narrative importance despite likely limited on-screen time.
- The unidentified wax seal, first shown in Episode 3, now pays off as physical evidence connecting Yusuf to the steward's quarters — this is the season's clearest piece of hard evidence yet, appropriately still short of full proof per Fatima's own caution in Act Three.
- Garba's parley scene reinforces his sincerity (condolences in Episode 5, restraint here) without softening the fact that he has, unambiguously, seized Zamar territory — the intended tension per Theme 4.
- The episode ends on a deliberate two-hander standoff between Fatima and Yusuf, escalating tension into Episode 8's pressure-release structure before Episode 9's full reveal.
