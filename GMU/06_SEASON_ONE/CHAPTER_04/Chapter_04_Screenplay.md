# GIDAN MULKI UNIVERSE
## Season One, Chapter 04 — "The Coin and the Crown"

*Directly continues from Chapter 03 ("Blood and Ink") — same scene-day continuity, no time skip unless noted in the first scene heading below. Second half of the original Episode 2 ("Blood and Ink").*

### ACT ONE

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — PRIVATE ANTECHAMBER — DAY**

FATIMA and MALIK together again — the same room where Fatima and Bashir sat in Episode 1, which neither of them comments on but the staging should make unmistakable.

**MALIK**
Tahir wants to move on Kasar Dutse using the coin. Publicly, formally, in front of the Diwan.

**FATIMA**
If we do that and we're wrong, we've started the exact war Garba's been patient enough not to start himself — and we've done it holding evidence that took less than a day to find in a room that was searched by someone who knew exactly what they were doing. That's not incompetence, Malik. Competent killers don't leave coins behind by accident.

**MALIK**
So what do we tell the Diwan?

**FATIMA**
(decisive, this is her scene)
Nothing, publicly. Not yet. We tell Kamal, in confidence, that the investigation continues and the coin is one lead among several — which is even true. We do not accuse a king of murder on the strength of one object we can't yet explain.

**MALIK**
(a beat, weighing it)
Tahir won't accept that quietly.

**FATIMA**
Tahir doesn't have to accept it. He has to obey it, and you have to be the one who tells him so.

Malik holds her gaze — this is, in its way, the first time in the season Fatima has directly asked him to spend his own authority on her behalf.

**MALIK**
(finally)
All right. Politics before violence. Father used to say that like it was easy.

**FATIMA**
(small, real smile)
He said a lot of things like they were easy.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — DIWAN HALL — DAY**

A smaller, less formal session than Episode 1's — KAMAL, FATIMA, and MALIK only, no full council. Kamal listens to their agreed position, weighing it.

**KAMAL**
A controlled silence. That is a very fine line to walk, and if it's ever discovered that this Diwan concealed a murder from itself, the cost will not fall only on the two of you.

**FATIMA**
I'm aware, Elder.

**KAMAL**
(a long beat, then, quieter)
Bashir would have approved of the caution. He'd have hated the secrecy. He spent forty years telling this council that the truth, however slow, was always the safer road.

(beat)

Continue the investigation. Report to me alone. And Princess — find who did this before that coin finds its way to General Tahir some other route. Men like him do not stay patient forever, and I would rather you control when this becomes public than have it happen to you.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. TSAURI KEEP — KASAR DUTSE — DAY**

GARBA on the fortress wall again, WAR-CAPTAIN beside him, looking out over the still-held pass.

**WAR-CAPTAIN**
Zamar hasn't moved on us. No formal protest, even.

**GARBA**
(unsurprised)
Because they don't know what to accuse us of yet. That tells me their investigation is still confused, which tells me whoever did this inside Zamar's own walls did clean work.

**WAR-CAPTAIN**
You think it was one of their own.

**GARBA**
(a long, considering pause)
I think a scholar died asking questions about a Sultan's death, and Kasar Dutse gained nothing from silencing him that we couldn't have gained just as easily from waiting. Ask yourself who actually needed him quiet.

(beat, almost to himself)

I intend to let Zamar exhaust itself suspecting me. It costs me nothing, and it buys whoever the real hand was a great deal of time.

He turns from the wall — Garba, alone among the players in this episode, already suspects the truth Fatima and Malik are still circling.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT ONE.**

---

### ACT TWO

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — ELDER BASHIR'S STUDY — NIGHT**

FATIMA alone this time, going back through the room a second time by lamplight, more patient than the Teaser's first pass. She examines the bookshelf, the floor, the window latch.

**INSERT — the window latch, closer. A very faint scratch in the frame, the kind a careful hand leaves prying something open from outside rather than being let in.**

**FATIMA**
(to herself)
Not through the door.

She crosses to the window, opens it, looks down — a narrow ledge, barely wide enough for a careful person to use, connecting to a service stair used by household staff moving between floors.

**FATIMA (CONT'D)**
(quiet, thinking aloud)
Not a stranger. Not a soldier climbing a wall. Someone who already knew this ledge existed — someone who works inside this palace.

She sits with that for a long moment — the coin evidence pointing outward to Kasar Dutse, and this new detail pointing stubbornly inward, and no way yet to reconcile the two.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — CORRIDOR — NIGHT**

Fatima walking back toward her chambers, deep in thought. YUSUF passes her going the other direction, unhurried as ever.

**YUSUF**
Still working, Princess. You should sleep. Grief makes for poor investigators.

**FATIMA**
(measuring him, carefully pleasant)
So does certainty, before the facts earn it. I'll take my chances with the grief.

**YUSUF**
(the faintest, unreadable smile)
Wise, as always.

He continues on. She watches him go — longer than a casual glance would require — and for the first time, we hold on her face long enough to know: she isn't sure yet, but she's started counting him among the possibilities.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. ZAMAR–KASAR DUTSE BORDER PASS — NIGHT**

DANLADI on watch, the Kasar Dutse cavalry still visible in the dark across the pass by their fires — neither side has withdrawn. A fellow soldier joins him.

**FELLOW SOLDIER**
Word from the palace says they found something. A Kasar Dutse coin, in the murdered elder's study.

**DANLADI**
(watching the distant fires, uneasy)
Then why haven't we been ordered to move on them?

**FELLOW SOLDIER**
Because someone up there is being careful.

**DANLADI**
(quiet)
Good. Because it's a lot easier to be careful in a palace than it is standing in a pass at night, looking at a few hundred men who could decide tonight's the night they stop being careful too.

He looks back out at the fires. Nothing happens. That, itself, is the tension the shot is built to hold — an entire episode's caution balanced on two armies simply choosing, again, not to move.

**FADE OUT.**

**END OF CHAPTER 04.**

## Notes on the Complete Episode (carried from the original Episode 2 draft)

- The Kasar Dutse coin is a deliberate red herring, consistent with Garba's own Act Three read on the situation — he correctly suspects an inside actor before Fatima or Malik do. This should pay off in Episode 7 ("Tsauri Pass," per GMU-003) when Fatima's investigation "turns a corner... inward."
- The window-ledge clue (Act Four) is the season's second real breadcrumb toward Yusuf, after his Episode 1 behavioral seeds — still circumstantial, still deniable, but Fatima's private suspicion starting here is an important character beat to preserve going forward.
- Fatima and Malik's "controlled silence" decision is this episode's clearest dramatization of Creative Principle "politics before violence" — note that it's also morally uncomfortable (they're concealing a murder from their own council), which is intentional per Principle 5, "no character is entirely good or evil."
- Amina's silverwork gift and letter (Act Two) keeps her financial leverage over Fatima active in the background without requiring her to physically appear — reserve her next full scene for Episode 4 ("The Merchant's Price," per GMU-003).
- Danladi's border-pass thread gives the episode a ground-level pulse to counterweight the palace-bound investigation scenes — recommend keeping this pattern (one procedural/political thread, one grounded human thread) for future episodes.
- Scene economy: 10 scenes across 5 locations (Palace interiors, the border pass, Tsauri Keep, and the cavalry grounds) plus the Teaser — slightly leaner than Episode 1, appropriate for a middle-of-arc investigation episode rather than a premiere.
