# GIDAN MULKI UNIVERSE
## Season One, Chapter 10 — "First Blood"

*Directly continues from Chapter 09 ("Ironhand's Gambit") — same scene-day continuity, no time skip unless noted in the first scene heading below. Second half of the original Episode 5 ("Ironhand's Gambit").*

### ACT ONE

**EXT. ZAMAR–KASAR DUTSE BORDER PASS — DAY**

Tension at the pass has become nearly unbearable. A KASAR DUTSE SCOUTING PARTY — a small group, ostensibly just watching — edges closer to the marker stones than any patrol has all season. DANLADI's commanding officer, on edge, orders a warning volley of arrows to land short of them, a display rather than an attack.

It goes wrong. One arrow, whether by wind or a nervous archer's poor aim, strikes a Kasar Dutse rider. He falls.

For a suspended, terrible beat, nothing happens — both sides frozen, staring at the fallen rider.

Then the Kasar Dutse line charges.

**DANLADI**
(shouting)
FORM UP! FORM UP NOW—

**QUICK, BRUTAL, CHAOTIC COMBAT** — not a grand battle, a short, ugly skirmish at close range. Danladi fights, genuinely afraid, alongside soldiers we've come to know across the season. When it ends — minutes later, both sides pulling back to their original lines, shaken — there are bodies on both sides of the marker stones.

**DANLADI (CONT'D)**
(breathing hard, staring at the dead, his own hands shaking)
That's it. That's what it looks like. Everyone who's been arguing about it in a warm hall should have to see this first.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. TSAURI KEEP — KASAR DUTSE — DAY**

GARBA receiving word of the skirmish, his WAR-CAPTAIN delivering it grimly.

**WAR-CAPTAIN**
Six dead, my king. Ours and theirs both. It started with a stray arrow — no order given on either side, as far as anyone can tell.

**GARBA**
(a long, heavy silence — this visibly costs him, unlike his usual calculation)
Six men dead over a mistake nobody intended, in a standoff I have deliberately kept short of exactly this outcome for months.

(beat, quieter)

Send Zamar my condolences for their dead. Genuinely, not as a maneuver. And send word that my offer — the marriage, the document — stands unchanged. I did not want this. I still don't.

**WAR-CAPTAIN**
(surprised by the sincerity)
Some in the court will read restraint as weakness, my king.

**GARBA**
(hard, final)
Then let them. I would rather be called weak by men who've never buried a soldier than be the king who turned an accident into the reason a real war started.

**SMASH CUT TO BLACK.**

**END OF ACT ONE.**

---

### ACT TWO

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — DIWAN HALL — NIGHT**

An emergency session, somber, the mood of the whole season shifted by the day's news. FATIMA and MALIK stand together, united by the shock of it in a way the season's tensions haven't allowed until now.

**MALIK**
(to the council)
Six of my soldiers are dead tonight over an arrow nobody ordered fired. I have spent this entire season telling my own officers that politics must come before violence. Today, six months of that principle didn't stop six men from dying anyway.

**FATIMA**
(taking his hand, a rare public gesture between them)
It didn't fail because the principle was wrong, Malik. It failed because we let a standoff sit unresolved for too long, and standoffs like that eventually find their own reason to break, with or without anyone's permission.

**KAMAL**
(to the hall)
What is the council's will?

**FATIMA**
(before anyone else can answer, firm)
Mourning first. Blame later, and only once we actually know whose arrow it was — not before. I will not let six real deaths become one more piece of ammunition in an argument, the way Bashir's murder already has.

The hall, unusually, offers no immediate objection — the day's cost has bought her that much authority, at least for tonight.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. PALACE OF ZAMAR — PRIVATE ANTECHAMBER — NIGHT**

Fatima and Malik alone, exhausted.

**MALIK**
Garba's offer. The marriage, the document about Bashir. Do we still consider it, after today?

**FATIMA**
(a long beat)
I don't know. I know that a king who sent condolences that sounded genuine, on the same day his own soldiers died, is not the simple villain Tahir's been describing all season. And I know that doesn't make his offer any less of a trap.

**MALIK**
(quiet)
Both things can be true.

**FATIMA**
(echoing Malik's own words back to him, gently)
Welcome to the family business.

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. ZAMAR–KASAR DUTSE BORDER PASS — NIGHT**

DANLADI, alone now, standing where the skirmish happened hours earlier — the ground disturbed, dark in the moonlight. He kneels, briefly, beside a marker stone, saying nothing, before rising and walking back toward the Zamar line.

Across the pass, unseen by him, a lone Kasar Dutse soldier does the same thing, on their side of the same stones — a mirrored, wordless grief neither side will ever know the other shared.

**FADE OUT.**

**END OF CHAPTER 10.**

## Notes on the Complete Episode (carried from the original Episode 5 draft)

- Delivers the season's midpoint armed clash per the GMU-003 tone note ("exactly one armed clash before the finale... Episode 6" — this episode absorbs and dramatizes that clash one episode early at the border pass rather than in a later formal battle; recommend updating GMU-003's tone note to reflect the skirmish landing in Episode 5, with Episode 7's pass seizure remaining the season's "first unambiguous act of war" as a distinct, larger-scale escalation).
- Garba's marriage-alliance offer and claimed Bashir-death document give him leverage over both siblings simultaneously without requiring him to lie outright — deliberately left unresolved (real document or bluff) for Episode 7 or 9 to pay off, consistent with the season's investigation not "turning inward" until Episode 7.
- The accidental nature of the border skirmish (a stray arrow, no order given) is an intentional structural choice: it keeps Garba sympathetic per Theme 4 ("no one is entirely good or evil") while still delivering real, felt stakes and casualties.
- Fatima and Malik's united front in Act Four is the clearest sibling-solidarity beat since Episode 1's teaser — deliberately placed here as a high point before Episode 6 fractures the royal family further with Halima's forced public choice.
- Danladi's closing image (mirrored grief across the border) reinforces the "no one is entirely good or evil" principle at the ground level, complementing Garba's version of the same theme above.
