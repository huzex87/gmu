# GMU-003 — Series Bible

Estimated full length: ~200 pages. This v0.2 draft covers premise, themes, the Season One arc, and a full 10-episode beat sheet — enough to write Episode One and to evaluate whether the world and cast (GMU-004, GMU-005) actually hold up under a real story. Deeper material (multi-season arc detail beyond Season One, full timeline of in-world events, additional POV characters) grows behind this as production proceeds.

## 1. Premise

**Logline:** When the Sultan of Zamar dies without a ratified heir, his two children — a scholar and a soldier who love each other and cannot both win — have forty days to secure the throne through Zamar's ancient law of council ratification, while a rival king and a merchant power both quietly work to profit from whichever one of them breaks first.

**Series-level premise (beyond Season One):** Each season follows a new crisis testing whether Zamar's constitutional model — legitimacy through law and ratification rather than force — can survive contact with power. Season One is a succession crisis; later seasons are intended to escalate the stakes (an external threat uniting or fracturing the three powers; Season One's antagonist Yusuf al-Kanem's true allegiance paying off).

## 2. Themes

Built directly on GMU-001's Creative Principles:

- **Politics before violence** — every major turn in Season One is a negotiation, a vote, or a betrayal before it is ever a battle.
- **Knowledge is power, literally** — Zamar's entire claim to legitimacy is that law and scholarship should outrank armies; the season is a stress test of whether that's actually true.
- **Every victory has a cost** — Fatima wins the throne in the finale and loses her brother's trust to do it; Malik keeps his honor and loses the crown.
- **No one is entirely good or evil** — Garba is a true believer in his own honor code, not a cartoon warlord; Amina profits from the crisis but isn't a cackling schemer, she's a woman who watched her home nearly starve once and will never let it happen again.

## 3. Central Conflicts and Factions

1. **Fatima's faction** — Diwan-aligned scholars, jurists, and clergy who believe the law should decide the succession.
2. **Malik's faction** — the Zamar military, who believe strength and popular loyalty should decide it.
3. **Kasar Dutse** — external pressure exploiting the internal Zamar split to seize the mountain passes.
4. **The River Confederacy** — financing both internal factions to secure trade terms regardless of outcome.
5. **The unseen fourth power** — represented in Season One only through Yusuf al-Kanem; not otherwise named yet, deliberately, so Season Two can be built around the reveal.

## 4. Tone and Genre Positioning

Political epic first, war drama second. Closer in spirit to a court-intrigue drama than a battle-heavy war story — Season One has exactly one armed clash before the finale (Episode 5) and one act of open military seizure (Episode 7); everything else is negotiation, investigation, and betrayal. Visual and tonal reference points: the scale and seriousness of prestige political drama, grounded in the Sahel-inspired world established in GMU-004.

## 5. Point-of-View Characters

Fatima bint Adamu (primary), Malik ibn Adamu (co-primary), with King Garba Ironhand and Speaker Amina Bello Kwarafi as secondary POVs giving the audience access to both rival powers' interiority rather than treating them as pure antagonists. Danladi provides a ground-level POV outside the palace. Full profiles: GMU-005.

## 6. Series-Level Mysteries / Dramatic Questions

- Was Sultan Adamu III's death natural, or was he killed — and if so, by whom? (Resolved partially in Episode 9; fully resolved only in Season Two.)
- Who does Yusuf al-Kanem actually serve? (Revealed Episode 9.)
- Can Zamar's law survive being tested this hard, or is Adamu III the last Sultan to inherit a peaceful system? (The season's central thematic question — deliberately left with a costly, ambiguous answer in the finale rather than a clean one.)

## 7. Season One — Episode Beat Sheet

**Editorial note (added after Episode 1 was actually drafted):** Writing Episode 1's full script (`06_SEASON_ONE/07_EPISODE_01/`) showed it naturally absorbed what this beat sheet originally scoped for Episode 2 — Fatima's Diwan campaign, Amina Bello Kwarafi's introduction and offer, and the Diwan elder's murder all landed better as the back half of Episode 1 than as a separate episode. Episode 1's beat below reflects what was actually written. Every subsequent episode has shifted down one number from the original beat sheet (old Ep3 → new Ep2, old Ep4 → new Ep3, and so on through old Ep9/10 → new Ep9/10), which freed one episode slot — used here for a new Episode 8 giving Garba and Danladi room the original ten-beat version didn't have. Treat this renumbering as provisional until Episode 2 is actually drafted and this gets confirmed again.

**Episode 1 — "The Sultan's Silence."** *(As drafted — see GMU-701.)* Sultan Adamu III dies suddenly and without a ratified heir; Fatima and Malik learn they have forty days before the Diwan. Fatima campaigns among the Diwan and faces open skepticism that Zamar has never had a female Sultan; Malik faces pressure from his generals to seize the throne by acclamation and refuses. Amina Bello Kwarafi arrives in person and offers Fatima discreet backing in exchange for future trade concessions. King Garba mobilizes cavalry toward the border "for stability." Ends with the senior Diwan elder, Bashir — who had warned Fatima that someone was blocking his access to Adamu III's final ledgers — found dead, and Yusuf al-Kanem's first behavioral hint of something withheld.

**Episode 2 — "Blood and Ink."** *(As drafted — see GMU-702. Absorbs former Episode 3's opening.)* The investigation into Bashir's death forces Fatima and Malik into a rare, uneasy cooperation. Kasar Dutse's cavalry reaches the border pass — a provocation that stays just inside treaty limits. Malik rallies further genuine military support even as Tahir keeps pressing the acclamation option. Yusuf al-Kanem continues operating as the Crown's trusted spymaster, still unsuspected.

**Episode 3 — "The Weight of the Crown."** *(As drafted — see GMU-703.)* Malik's military faction escalates its pressure toward acclamation — the season's clearest direct test yet of "politics before violence." Fatima uncovers a financial trail tying Kwarafi gold to Bashir's death (complicated, given Amina's Episode 1 approach to Fatima herself — is this evidence, or misdirection?). First real rupture between the siblings. Introduces Queen Mother Halima.

**Episode 4 — "The Merchant's Price."** *(As drafted — see GMU-704.)* Fatima confronts Amina directly in Kwarafi and discovers Kwara's "neutrality" is a fiction — both factions are being financed simultaneously, and Amina's Episode 1 offer to Fatima had a mirror offer to Malik behind it the whole time. Amina, cornered, offers Fatima real backing in exchange for future trade rights: a costly bargain. Malik, meanwhile, secures a key general's loyalty through methods that compromise him for the first time. Introduces Commander Rafiu.

**Episode 5 — "Ironhand's Gambit."** *(As drafted — see GMU-705.)* Garba proposes a marriage alliance to whichever sibling ultimately wins — a political trap disguised as diplomacy — and reveals he holds evidence connected to Bashir's death, using it to manipulate both factions. A border skirmish leaves soldiers dead on both sides: the season's midpoint escalation into open bloodshed.

**Episode 6 — "The Second Covenant."** *(As drafted — see GMU-706.)* The Covenant clergy publicly threaten to withhold religious legitimacy from anyone who takes the throne by force rather than law, raising the season's central theme to open text. Fatima and Malik's mother, previously neutral, is forced to publicly choose a side, fracturing the royal family in view of the whole city. Introduces High Cleric Nafisa.

**Episode 7 — "Tsauri Pass."** *(As drafted — see GMU-707.)* Garba's forces seize the contested mountain pass outright under a self-defense justification, the season's first unambiguous act of war. Fatima's investigation turns a corner: the trail behind Bashir's death doesn't lead outward to Kasar Dutse or Kwara, but inward — to someone inside Zamar's own court. Yusuf is directly suspected for the first time; the steward Abubakar is identified as the hands-on operative.

**Episode 8 — "The Ground Beneath Them."** *(As drafted — see GMU-708. New episode, fills the slot freed by the Episode 1/2 consolidation.)* A deliberate pressure-release episode before the finale run: focuses on Danladi and the border villages actually living with Kasar Dutse's pass seizure, and on Garba's court at Tsauri Keep — his honor code, his war-captains' doubts about how far he's willing to go. Gives the "no one is entirely good or evil" principle room to breathe for Garba specifically before the finale asks the audience to see him as an obstacle. Ends with Danladi forced into the choice his Episode 1–7 doubt has been building toward.

**Episode 9 — "The Diwan Convenes."** *(As drafted — see GMU-709.)* The ratification vote finally happens amid full crisis. Yusuf al-Kanem's true loyalty — and the identity of the unseen fourth power he serves — is exposed, recontextualizing earlier episodes. Malik, disillusioned by what winning would now require of him, makes a costly, defining choice: he voluntarily withdraws his claim.

**Episode 10 — "Power is Earned."** *(As drafted — see GMU-710.)* Season finale, titled for the franchise tagline. Fatima is ratified Sultana — Zamar's first female ruler — but only through a binding trade concession to Kwara and the loss of her brother's trust. Garba holds the seized pass; Bashir's death is only partly resolved. Final image: Fatima alone on the palace balcony with the crown, as distant war drums signal Season Two.
