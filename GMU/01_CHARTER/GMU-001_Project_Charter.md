# GIDAN MULKI UNIVERSE – Project Charter

## 1. Executive Summary

**Project Name:** GIDAN MULKI UNIVERSE (GMU)

**Working Tagline:** "Power is Earned. Legacy is Chosen."

**Vision:** To create Africa's most comprehensive original fictional cinematic universe inspired by the civilizations, architecture, governance, scholarship, commerce, and cultural richness of the Sahel, designed for global audiences through AI-assisted production and world-class storytelling.

**Mission:** To tell powerful African stories that celebrate leadership, knowledge, courage, diplomacy, justice, and innovation while creating a sustainable entertainment franchise capable of expanding into multiple forms of media.

**Core Values:** Authenticity, Excellence, Creativity, Respect, Integrity, Innovation, Collaboration, Long-term thinking.

## 2. Purpose

GMU exists to:

- Develop a premium scripted television series.
- Build a reusable fictional universe.
- Establish a sustainable African entertainment IP.
- Create production assets that can support future stories.
- Showcase the richness of Sahelian-inspired civilizations through original fiction.

## 3. Scope

The initial franchise will include:

**Television** — Season One (10 Episodes), Season Two, Season Three, Season Four, Season Five.

**Publishing** — Novels, Graphic Novels, Encyclopedias, Children's Editions.

**Digital** — Interactive Website, Character Database, Interactive Timeline, Interactive Map, Wiki.

**Gaming** — Mobile Strategy Game, PC Strategy Game, RPG, Card Game.

**Educational** — African History Companion, Architecture Companion, Leadership Companion, Storytelling Curriculum.

**Commercial** — Merchandise, Licensing, Collectibles, Art Books.

## 4. Strategic Objectives

**Year One:** Complete Charter, Series Bible, World Bible, Character Bible, Season One, Episode One screenplay, Pitch Deck, Visual Identity, Website Prototype.

**Year Two:** Produce AI Concept Art, Storyboards, Trailer, Pilot Episode, Investor Package.

**Year Three:** Begin production of the first season and expand audience engagement through digital platforms and publishing.

## 5. Success Metrics

The project should be measured not only by audience size but also by creative quality and long-term value:

- Complete canonical documentation.
- Production-ready screenplay package.
- Consistent visual identity.
- Strong community engagement.
- Distribution partnerships.
- Licensing opportunities.

## 6. Creative Principles

Every story decision must satisfy at least one of these principles:

1. **Character before spectacle.**
2. **Politics before violence.**
3. **Knowledge is power.**
4. **Every victory has a cost.**
5. **No character is entirely good or entirely evil.**
6. **Culture should be lived, not explained.**
7. **History shapes every present-day decision.**

## 7. Canon Rules

The following elements become fixed unless formally revised (see GMU-000 Repository Standards, Section 4 — Canon Change Control):

- Geography
- Calendar
- Kingdoms
- Major historical events
- Royal houses
- Constitutional structure
- Core character biographies

Minor details may evolve, but foundational canon should remain stable.

## 8. Audience

**Primary:** 18–45. **Secondary:** 15+.

Global target markets: West Africa, North Africa, Middle East, Europe, North America, Asia.

## 9. Franchise Position

The project is designed as an original African epic. Its goal is to establish a distinctive identity rather than emulate another franchise.

## 10. Production Philosophy

AI is used to accelerate creativity — not replace it. Human judgment guides: Story, Ethics, Culture, Performance, Final creative decisions.

## 11. Risk Management

**Potential risks:**

- Cultural inaccuracies.
- Visual inconsistency.
- Scope expansion.
- Tool changes.
- Budget constraints.
- Intellectual property protection.

**Mitigation strategies:** cultural review, version control, documentation, and staged development.

## 12. Quality Standards

Every major deliverable must meet defined standards before it is considered complete.

**Writing:** Clear narrative. Consistent canon. Strong dialogue. Distinct character voices.

**Visuals:** Consistent architecture. Consistent costumes. Recognizable symbols. Cohesive color language.

**Production:** Repeatable workflows. Organized assets. Version-controlled documentation.

## 13. Project Governance

Key decisions are documented and versioned. Major changes to canon require review to avoid unintended contradictions across the franchise. See GMU-000 Repository Standards for the formal process.

## 14. Roadmap

- **Phase 0** — Foundation
- **Phase 1** — Worldbuilding
- **Phase 2** — Story Development
- **Phase 3** — Screenwriting
- **Phase 4** — Visual Development
- **Phase 5** — AI Production
- **Phase 6** — Marketing
- **Phase 7** — Distribution

## 15. Long-Term Vision

The objective is not simply to launch one successful series. The objective is to establish a durable African entertainment universe capable of growing over decades through new stories, media formats, and creative collaborations.

## Addendum: Visual Identity (preliminary — full spec owned by GMU-002)

The universe should have a recognizable identity inspired by the Sahel without replicating any single historical kingdom:

- Earth-toned architecture with geometric ornamentation.
- Rich indigo, emerald, gold, white, and deep red textiles.
- Distinct royal insignia for each fictional kingdom.
- Strong emphasis on cavalry, scholarship, markets, and public institutions.

## Addendum: Branding Decision — RESOLVED

**Decision:** "GIDAN MULKI" is retained and locked as the flagship franchise and series title. It is not demoted to an in-world palace/institution name.

**Rationale:** Gidan Mulki is distinctive and already carries the project's identity; alternatives raised during founding discussion (*The Crown of Zamar*, *Empire of Zamar*, *The Last Sultan*, *The Twelve Kingdoms*, *Legacy of Zamar*) read as generic fantasy-epic titles that could belong to any franchise, trading away the most memorable asset for something more market-generic. If international legibility is a concern at the marketing stage, a subtitle (e.g., "Gidan Mulki: [Universe Name]") solves it without a rename. "Afrasah" remains available as the in-world continent name (see GMU-004) rather than a franchise-title candidate.

**Status:** Canon. Locked as of this revision. GMU-002 (Creative & Brand Bible) proceeds against this decision — logo system, wordmark, and brand identity should be built around "GIDAN MULKI," not a placeholder title.
