# Location Concept Brief — Tsauri Keep (Kasar Dutse)

The Kingdom of Kasar Dutse's capital fortress, built into the highland mountain pass that gives the kingdom its name ("Land of Stone," per GMU-004 §2.2). Covers two variants used in Episode 1.

## Shared Architectural Language

Grey stone construction, deliberately harder-edged and less ornamented than either the Palace of Zamar or the University — should read as a working fortress, not a court. Deep red banners are the primary color intrusion against the grey stone (Kasar Dutse's palette: deep red, iron-grey, black, per GMU-004 §2.2). Wind should be a constant implied element — this is a mountain-pass location, exposed and cold compared to Birnin Zamar's river-city warmth.

## Variant 1 — Fortress Wall (Act One)

**Key elements:** A high defensive wall overlooking the mountain pass itself — the pass should be visible and important in the composition, since it's the entire reason Kasar Dutse's wealth and power exist. Garba stands here alone with one messenger; the shot should emphasize scale (the pass, the wall) against his solitary figure.

> **AI image prompt:** High grey stone fortress wall overlooking a dramatic mountain pass, deep red banners snapping in strong wind, a single robed figure standing at the wall's edge looking out over the pass, overcast dramatic sky, harsh mountain light, photorealistic wide shot, Sahel-inspired highland fortress architecture.

## Variant 2 — Fortress Yard (Act Two)

**Key elements:** A large open yard within the fortress walls, cavalry assembling — hundreds of riders per the script. Should read as a mobilization in progress: organized, disciplined, unhurried (matching Garba's character) rather than chaotic.

> **AI image prompt:** Large fortress courtyard, grey stone walls, hundreds of mounted cavalry assembling in organized ranks, deep red banners throughout, disciplined and purposeful atmosphere rather than chaotic, overcast mountain light, wide establishing shot, photorealistic, Sahel-inspired highland military setting.
