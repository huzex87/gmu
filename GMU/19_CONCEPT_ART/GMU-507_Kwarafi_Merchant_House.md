# Location Concept Brief — Kwarafi Merchant House

Speaker Amina Bello Kwarafi's seat of power in the Port of Kwarafi, and (Episode 10) her Birnin Zamar embassy. Used in Episode 4 (Fatima's confrontation visit) and Episode 10 (Amina's coronation-morning contract dealings).

## Shared Architectural Language

A deliberate visual departure from both Zamar's earth-toned indigo/gold ceremonialism and Kasar Dutse's fortified stone — Kwara's architecture should feel open, mercantile, built for the movement of goods and information rather than defense or worship. Light timber and pale stone construction, open canal-facing walls, airy rather than monumental. Emerald and white as the dominant palette (per GMU-002), with silver accent details on ledgers, scales, and trim. Where Zamar performs law and Kasar Dutse performs strength, Kwara's architecture should perform transparency and liquidity — lots of visible activity, ledgers, moving water.

## Room 1 — Counting Hall (Episode 4 Teaser/Act One, Episode 10 Act One)

**Key elements:** Vast, airy, lined with ledgers and record-shelves rather than scripture or weapons — Kwara's answer to a Diwan hall or war tent. A raised desk for Amina to preside from, surrounded by clerks in constant, practiced motion. Should feel busy and alive even in establishing shots, reinforcing Kwara as a functioning economic engine rather than a static seat of power.

> **AI image prompt:** Vast airy merchant counting hall built in light timber and pale stone, open canal-facing walls admitting soft reflected water-light, rows of ledger-shelves and clerks' desks, a raised presiding desk, emerald and white banners with silver accent trim, constant background activity of clerks and scribes, photorealistic, cinematic wide interior shot, Sahel river-delta trade architecture.

## Room 2 — Amina's Private Office (Episode 4 Act One, Act Two)

**Key elements:** Smaller and more personal than the Counting Hall, but still transactional — a tea service as a recurring prop (echoing Yusuf's and Bashir's tea habits elsewhere in the series, a deliberate visual rhyme). Windows overlooking the canal trade quarter.

> **AI image prompt:** Elegant private merchant's office overlooking a canal trade quarter, light timber furnishings, a tea service on a low table, emerald and white color accents with silver trim, warm afternoon light through open canal-facing windows, composed and quietly powerful atmosphere, photorealistic interior.

## Exterior — Trade Quarter Canals (Episode 4 Teaser)

**Key elements:** Open canals, floating markets, a skyline of merchant-house banners — Fatima's entry point into a visually different world from Zamar's stone corridors.

> **AI image prompt:** Sahel river-delta trade quarter, open canals with floating market boats, timber and pale stone merchant buildings, skyline of emerald and white merchant-house banners, a formal river barge arriving, bustling daylight atmosphere, photorealistic wide exterior shot.

## Exterior — Amina's Balcony (Episode 4 Act Three closing scene)

**Key elements:** A private, elevated balcony overlooking the canal-lit city at night — Amina's personal space for the season's quieter, more reflective beats.

> **AI image prompt:** Private elevated balcony overlooking a canal-lit merchant city at night, warm lamplight reflected on water below, light timber railing with silver accent detail, quiet and contemplative, photorealistic night exterior shot.

## Room 3 — Birnin Zamar Embassy Counting Hall (Episode 10 Act One)

**Key elements:** A smaller, transplanted version of Room 1 — Kwara's temporary presence inside Zamar's capital for the coronation. Should read as clearly "Kwara architecture" even while surrounded by Zamar's indigo/gold city, a deliberate color intrusion consistent with the emerald/white Kwara banners established in GMU-502's Main Gate brief.

> **AI image prompt:** Compact merchant embassy counting hall set within a Zamar-style building, emerald and white Kwara banners and ledger-shelves standing out against indigo and gold Zamar architectural details visible through windows, clerks at work, dawn light, photorealistic interior, deliberate visual contrast between two kingdoms' architectural languages in one frame.
