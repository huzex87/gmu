# Location Concept Brief — Palace of Zamar (New Rooms, Episodes 3–10)

Additional Palace interiors introduced across Episodes 3–10, sharing the architectural language established in GMU-502 (indigo/gold geometric ornamentation, earth-toned walls, lattice windows, understated furnishings relative to the architecture itself). This brief covers only the new rooms — see GMU-502 for the shared language and the seven rooms already designed for Episode 1.

## Room 8 — Queen Mother's Quarters (Episode 3, Episode 10)

**Key elements:** Quiet, book-lined, understated compared to the rest of the Palace — should feel like the one room in the Palace that has genuinely stopped performing power. Warmer and more personal even than the Private Antechamber (GMU-502 Room 4), reflecting Halima's chosen distance from the succession crisis. A window seat or reading nook as a recurring visual anchor for her private scenes.

> **AI image prompt:** Quiet book-lined private royal chamber, warm earth-toned walls with restrained indigo accents (less gold than ceremonial rooms), a window seat with soft cushions, shelves of books and scrolls, soft afternoon light through a lattice window, deeply personal and unhurried atmosphere, photorealistic interior, Sahel-inspired palace architecture.

## Room 9 — War Tent, Interior Detail (Episode 3 continuation of GMU-504)

**Key elements:** GMU-504 already covers the Cavalry Grounds' war tent exterior/general setup — for Episode 3's more detailed war-tent scenes, emphasize a map table with the border pass region marked, and a formal petition/parchment as a key recurring prop (the unsigned acclamation petition).

> **AI image prompt:** Military war tent interior, canvas walls, a large map table marked with border territory, Zamar cavalry banners in indigo and gold, a sealed parchment petition with wax seals visible on the table, warm lamplight, tense focused atmosphere, photorealistic interior.

## Room 10 — Holding Chamber (Episode 9, Episode 10)

**Key elements:** A secure, austere detention space beneath the Palace — the visual opposite of the Palace's ceremonial rooms. Should read as functional and grim without being gratuitously grim; stone walls, minimal light, a single barred opening. This is where Yusuf is held from Episode 9 onward.

> **AI image prompt:** Austere stone detention chamber beneath a palace, minimal light from a single barred window opening, plain stone walls with no ornamentation, a simple bench, cold and functional atmosphere, photorealistic interior, restrained and non-graphic in tone.

## Room 11 — Throne Room (Episode 10)

**Key elements:** A room deliberately unused until the finale — grander and more formal than the Great Hall (GMU-502 Room 3) or the Diwan Hall (GMU-502 Room 2), reserved specifically for the act of coronation. The empty throne itself should be the room's visual and emotional center throughout Act One and Act Three, until Fatima finally sits upon it. Recommend the richest indigo/gold treatment of any room in the Palace, since this is the visual and narrative payoff of the entire season's production design.

> **AI image prompt:** Grand formal throne room, the richest indigo and gold geometric ornamentation in the palace, soaring ceilings, a single ornate throne as the central visual focus, ceremonial banners, tall lattice windows casting dramatic shafts of light, assembled nobility and clergy, ceremonial ritual atmosphere, photorealistic, cinematic wide interior shot, Sahel-inspired palace architecture at its most monumental.

## Room 12 — Palace Balcony (Episode 10 closing image)

**Key elements:** The season's final image — a private balcony overlooking Birnin Zamar at night. Should connect visually to the Main Gate exterior and City establishing shots (GMU-501, GMU-502) so the geography reads as continuous. Distant torch-light or signal fires on the horizon (the "war drums" cue) should be a subtle, almost-unnoticeable detail on first viewing.

> **AI image prompt:** Private stone balcony on a grand palace at night, overlooking a lamplit Sahel-inspired city skyline, a lone robed figure holding a crown, cool moonlight, distant faint torch-lights or signal fires on the horizon barely visible, quiet contemplative and slightly ominous atmosphere, photorealistic wide night exterior shot, cinematic.
