# Location Concept Brief — Zamar Cavalry Grounds

Zamar's military training and staging ground, on the city outskirts. Covers two variants used in Episode 1.

## Shared Architectural Language

Functional rather than ceremonial — packed earth, wooden fencing and practice equipment, simple utilitarian structures. Should look and feel like the visual opposite of the Palace: dust instead of polished stone, practical gear instead of ceremonial robes. Indigo/gold appear only as small unit banners, not architecture.

## Variant 1 — Training Grounds (Act One)

**Key elements:** Open sparring area, dust, practice weapons, a loose crowd of soldiers watching/training. Morning light, energetic and warm before the Rider's interruption turns the mood.

> **AI image prompt:** Dusty open-air cavalry training ground on a city's outskirts, wooden fencing and practice weapon racks, soldiers in practical leather training gear sparring and watching, morning sunlight, energetic and informal atmosphere, small indigo unit banners, photorealistic wide shot, Sahel-inspired military setting.

## Variant 2 — War Tent (Act Two)

**Key elements:** An enclosed command space within the grounds — a map table is the central prop (per the script, Malik and his generals stand over it). Should feel more serious and enclosed than the open training grounds, mourning-white cloth visible on the characters contrasting against the tent's practical canvas.

> **AI image prompt:** Military command tent interior, a large map table as central focus, canvas walls, practical utilitarian furnishings, several senior officers in dark cavalry uniforms with gold braid gathered around the table, serious tense atmosphere, warm interior lighting, photorealistic, Sahel-inspired military command setting.
