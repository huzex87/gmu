# Location Concept Brief — Border Village of Kantin Rafi

A small farming village straddling the Zamar–Kasar Dutse border, sitting inside Kasar Dutse's newly held territory following Episode 7's pass seizure. The episode's thematic heart — ordinary people living with the consequences of a war their leaders started. Used throughout Episode 8.

## Architectural Language

Deliberately humble and lived-in — the visual opposite of every palace, keep, and merchant hall in the series. Modest stone-and-thatch homes, a central well as the village's social and narrative anchor, farmland visible at the edges. No banners, no ceremonial color-coding — this is a space that belongs to neither Zamar nor Kasar Dutse emotionally, even though a map line now formally divides it. Warm, dusty, sun-worn palette (ochre, tan, weathered wood) rather than any kingdom's official colors, reinforcing that the village's loyalty is to itself and its people, not to either crown.

## Key Location — The Shared Well

**Key elements:** The village's literal and thematic center — a stone well serving both halves of the community, now sitting near (or on) the new occupation line. This is where the episode's water-rights subplot plays out, and should be staged so both Zamar and Kasar Dutse soldiers can plausibly occupy the same frame around it without it reading as either kingdom's territory.

> **AI image prompt:** Modest Sahel farming village well at the center of a small settlement, stone-and-thatch homes surrounding it, worn stone well with a rope and bucket, dusty sun-warmed earth tones, ordinary villagers and a few soldiers from different factions present but not in conflict, warm daylight, quiet lived-in atmosphere, photorealistic wide shot, unglamorous and human-scale.

## Key Location — Village Edge / Occupation Line

**Key elements:** The visible but informal boundary where Kasar Dutse soldiers are posted, distinct from the fortified, militarized border pass seen in earlier episodes — this should feel like an improvised checkpoint in the middle of ordinary farmland, not a proper military installation.

> **AI image prompt:** Informal checkpoint at the edge of a Sahel farming village, a few soldiers in Kasar Dutse deep red and iron-grey posted casually near farmland, no fortifications, dusty road, modest thatch-roofed structures nearby, late afternoon light, quiet tension rather than open hostility, photorealistic exterior shot.

## Key Location — Farmland

**Key elements:** Fields visible at the village's edges, some worked, some visibly neglected due to the disruption — a subtle visual indicator of the human cost of the occupation without needing dialogue to state it.

> **AI image prompt:** Sahel farmland at the edge of a small village, some fields being worked by farmers, others visibly untended, modest irrigation channels, warm golden-hour light, understated sense of disruption and uncertainty, photorealistic wide exterior shot.
