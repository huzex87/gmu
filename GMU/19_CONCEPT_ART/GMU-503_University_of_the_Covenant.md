# Location Concept Brief — University of the Covenant

Zamar's seat of learning (per GMU-004 §5), training the jurists who eventually sit on the Diwan. Covers two rooms used in Episode 1.

## Shared Architectural Language

Terraced white walls (the University is specifically called out as white-walled in the River Port establishing shot, GMU-501) — a deliberate visual contrast to the Palace's earth-toned indigo/gold richness. Should read as older, plainer, more austere: a place of study rather than display. High lattice windows for natural light over reading and writing spaces. Indigo appears here only as accent (robe trim, banners) rather than as an architectural color.

## Room 1 — Scholars' Hall (Act One)

**Key elements:** A lecture/debate hall — a lectern, tiered or semi-circular seating for scholars, high windows. Should feel like a place built for argument: acoustically open, sightlines centered on whoever's speaking.

> **AI image prompt:** Academic lecture hall with terraced white stone walls, high lattice windows casting bright natural light, a wooden lectern, semi-circular seating for robed scholars, austere and plain compared to royal palace architecture, sense of an active debate space, photorealistic interior, Sahel-inspired university architecture.

## Room 2 — Diwan Library (Act Three)

**Key elements:** Ledger and manuscript storage — tall shelving, reading tables, quieter and more enclosed than the Scholars' Hall. This is where Fatima physically searches through records, so the space needs to visually support that action (shelves she can walk along, ledgers she can pull).

> **AI image prompt:** Tall archival library within a university, floor-to-ceiling wooden shelving packed with ledgers and manuscripts, narrow aisles, a few reading tables, terraced white stone walls with minimal ornamentation, soft daylight from high windows, quiet scholarly atmosphere, photorealistic interior, Sahel-inspired architecture.
