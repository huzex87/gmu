# Location Concept Brief — Great Sanctuary of the Covenant

Birnin Zamar's central place of worship and the seat of the Faith of the Covenant's clergy (explicitly Islam — see GMU-004 §1). Used across Episode 6 (public legitimacy address, private chamber, closing steps scene) and Episode 9 (ratification vote presided over jointly with the Diwan).

## Architectural Language

Distinct deliberately from the Palace of Zamar's indigo/gold court architecture — this is a space that belongs to the faith, not the throne. High, austere hall architecture: soaring pillars, minimal ornamentation relative to the Palace, deep-set high windows admitting shafts of light rather than the Palace's patterned lattice glow. Neutral stone tones (sand, pale grey, warm white) with restrained geometric carving — echo the same geometric visual language as the Palace's lattice screens, since both draw on a shared Sahel architectural tradition, but keep the Sanctuary's palette cooler and more muted, avoiding the Palace's indigo/gold and any of the three kingdoms' colors entirely, consistent with High Cleric Nafisa's costume design (GMU-413) establishing the faith's visual independence from all political factions.

**Added per GMU-305 (Religion Bible) research pass:** grounded in real Sudano-Sahelian mosque architecture (the tradition behind real structures like Timbuktu's mudbrick mosques), the Sanctuary's full complex should include a **sahn** — an open courtyard, historically used for ablution before prayer, which can double as a naturalistic gathering/transition space between scenes — and a **mihrab**, the niche marking the direction of prayer, positioned inside the Main Prayer Hall (Room 1) even if it never becomes a shot's focal point. Neither needs to dominate any single frame, but both should exist consistently once a floor plan is locked, so the space reads as architecturally complete to anyone familiar with real mosque design.

## Room 1 — Main Prayer Hall (Episode 6 public address)

**Key elements:** Vast, high-ceilinged, capable of holding a large public gathering — nobles, soldiers, merchants, ordinary citizens together, the largest crowd scene in the series. A raised speaking position for the High Cleric, no throne or seat of political power anywhere in view. Morning or midday light through high windows, clear and unshadowed — visually the most "honest" light in the series, appropriate for a scene about public legitimacy.

> **AI image prompt:** Vast, high-ceilinged Islamic prayer hall in a Sahel-inspired architectural style, soaring stone pillars, deep-set high windows casting clear shafts of daylight, minimal geometric carved ornamentation in neutral sand and pale grey stone, a large diverse crowd of worshippers and dignitaries gathered, a raised speaking position for a religious leader, no throne or royal seating visible, photorealistic, cinematic wide interior shot, dignified and austere atmosphere.

## Room 2 — Private Chamber (Episode 6, High Cleric's meeting with Fatima and Malik)

**Key elements:** Smaller, plainer than the Palace's private antechamber — scholarly rather than royal, closer in feeling to a study than a throne room. Should reinforce that religious authority in this world operates through study and reasoned argument, not ceremony.

> **AI image prompt:** Small austere private chamber within an Islamic religious sanctuary, plain stone walls with minimal geometric carving, simple wooden furnishings, a few religious texts visible, soft daylight through a single high window, scholarly and contemplative atmosphere, photorealistic interior, Sahel-inspired architecture.

## Exterior — Sanctuary Steps (Episode 6 closing Yusuf/Nafisa scene)

**Key elements:** Broad formal steps leading up to the Sanctuary's entrance, overlooking the city — a public but quiet space, appropriate for the unsettling private exchange between Nafisa and Yusuf at night.

> **AI image prompt:** Broad stone steps leading up to a grand Islamic sanctuary entrance, overlooking a Sahel-inspired city skyline at night, warm lamplight from the city below, cool moonlit stone architecture, quiet and contemplative, photorealistic wide exterior shot.

## Room 3 — Joint Ratification Hall (Episode 9, shared with Diwan)

**Key elements:** For Episode 9's ratification vote, the Sanctuary's Main Prayer Hall (Room 1) can be reused, or alternatively staged in a hybrid space bridging Sanctuary and Diwan Hall architecture — production discretion. If reused, add formal ceremonial elements (banners, ceremonial seating for Diwan elders) not present in the Episode 6 version to visually mark the escalated stakes.
