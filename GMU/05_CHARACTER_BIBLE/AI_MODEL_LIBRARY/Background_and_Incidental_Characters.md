# Background and Incidental Characters — Episode 01

These roles appear in one scene each with no dialogue-driving presence beyond that scene, so they don't warrant full character sheets or Character Bible entries yet — per the "just enough, just in time" principle, they get lighter visual notes here instead. If any of them recur in later episodes, promote them to a full sheet at that point.

### Servant Girl (Teaser)

Sixteen, plain palace servant's dress, unbleached or muted fabric, minimal ornament — should read as genuinely young and unprepared for what she finds. No need for a locked design; a consistent age and costume language (matching general Zamar servant dress once `04_WORLD_BIBLE/COSTUME_BIBLE/` covers it) is enough for a single reaction shot.

### Rider (Act One)

Zamar messenger-cavalry, dust-covered from hard riding, plain functional gear distinct from Malik's officer dress — should look like he's been riding fast and long. One shot, urgency is the only real design requirement.

### Soldier (Act One, cavalry-grounds sparring partner)

Young Zamar cavalry trainee, similar base uniform language to Danladi's but even more junior/unranked. Comic-relief energy in his one line — costume should be plain, slightly disheveled from sparring.

### Messenger (Act One / Act Two, Kasar Dutse)

Kasar Dutse military messenger, red and iron-grey base uniform (see Garba's sheet, GMU-403, for the kingdom's palette), kneeling posture in his one shot. No distinguishing features needed.

### General(s), unnamed (Act Two, war tent — "three senior GENERALS")

Two unnamed generals flanking Tahir — same senior Zamar cavalry command uniform family as Tahir (GMU-408), varied slightly in age/build so the three don't read as visually identical. Silent presence only; no individual design needed beyond "reads as senior Zamar military."

### War-Captain (Act Two, Tsauri Keep)

Kasar Dutse senior cavalry officer, same red/iron-grey palette as Garba, riding gear rather than court dress. One exchange of dialogue with Garba — should look capable and weathered, a smaller-scale echo of Garba's design language.

### Young Clergyman (Act Three, Diwan Library)

Zamar Covenant clergy, junior rank, plainer robes than Kamal or Bashir, visibly uncomfortable in his scene with Fatima — costume should clearly mark him as clergy (a simpler variant of Diwan robes) without any of the seniority markers the named elders carry.

### Steward (Act Three, palace gate)

Palace household staff, formal but not aristocratic dress, welcoming Amina — plain, competent palace-official look, no need for further detail.

### Old Woman (Act Two, Great Market)

Elderly Zamar civilian, unbleached mourning cloth like everyone else in the procession crowd, weathered and grounded — her design should reinforce that the mourning period affects the whole city equally, ordinary people included, visually bridging the palace-centric costume language to everyday Birnin Zamar.

## Status

Draft. Sufficient for Episode 1 production. Revisit if any of these roles are written back into later episodes with expanded presence (the unnamed generals and war-captain are the most likely candidates, given Kasar Dutse and Malik's faction both recur heavily).
