# Character Design Sheet — Commander Rafiu

Appears in: Episode 04 (introduction), Episode 07 (casualty report), Episode 08 (authorizes Danladi's liaison role).

## Physical Reference

Man, late fifties, weathered in a way that reads as field experience rather than age alone — deep-set eyes, sun-worn skin, the build of a soldier who has spent a career in postings rather than at court. Should look visibly older and less polished than General Tahir, reinforcing the character-bible contrast between a career field officer and a senior court-adjacent general.

## Costume

Standard Zamar cavalry command insignia, but visibly plainer and more worn than Tahir's — practical repairs, sun-faded fabric, no unnecessary ornament. This is a commander who spends his rank allowance on his garrison, not his own appearance. Keep the Zamar indigo/gold present but muted and workmanlike rather than ceremonial.

## Expression and Performance Notes

Unimpressed by rank for its own sake, direct almost to the point of insubordination with anyone except Malik specifically, whom he treats with plain, unpolished respect. His Episode 4 scene should land as a father's real worry underneath a commander's professional request — allow a flicker of personal emotion when he mentions his son at the border before he recomposes into formal address.

## Voice Direction

Plainspoken, unhurried, direct — should sound like a man giving a field report even in a political conversation. Allow one audible crack of real feeling in the Episode 4 scene when discussing his son, quickly reined back in. No polish, no diplomatic softening — the opposite vocal register from Yusuf or Amina.
