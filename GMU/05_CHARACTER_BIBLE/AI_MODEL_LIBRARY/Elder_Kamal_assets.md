# Character Design Sheet — Elder Kamal

Appears in: Episode 01 — Act One, Act Four.

## Physical Reference

Man, sixties, composed and exacting — should look like institutional authority made flesh, in contrast to Bashir's gentler seniority. Neat, precisely groomed beard, upright rather than stooped posture (a deliberate visual contrast to Bashir). Face naturally set in a slightly stern default that a dry sense of humor keeps from tipping into cold.

## Costume

Diwan formal robes, more visibly rank-marked than Bashir's — heavier trim, a formal presiding sash for the Diwan Hall scene specifically. Kamal performs institutional authority visually where Bashir declined to, so his costume should read as one notch more ceremonial throughout.

## Expression and Performance Notes

Dry, testing, rarely fully unguarded — the Act One "almost smile" (provoking Fatima, then catching himself) is the character's single most important beat to get right: it needs to read as a man who respects her enough to argue with her seriously, not real hostility. Act Four is the inversion — his composure genuinely breaks for the first time in the episode ("nothing leaves this room until I say so. Nothing."), and that should land as a real character event, not a stock "shocked authority figure" beat.

**Act Four variant note:** posture and expression should visibly crack from the baseline — shoulders less upright, voice-matched expression of genuine shock rather than composed authority.

## Voice Direction

Precise, dry, formal cadence — delivers even provocations ("you'd have us negotiate with merchants the way we negotiate with children") in a measured, almost pleasant tone. The Diwan Hall proclamation ("the clock begins today") should be his most formally performed line in the episode. Act Four's "nothing leaves this room" should break that formality — hoarser, faster, the first unrehearsed thing he says all episode.
