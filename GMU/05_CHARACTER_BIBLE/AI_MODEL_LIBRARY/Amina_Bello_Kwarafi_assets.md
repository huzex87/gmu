# Character Design Sheet — Speaker Amina Bello Kwarafi

Appears in: Episode 01 — Act Three.

## Physical Reference

Woman, mid-forties, West African features, composed and expensively understated — the goal is a look that reads as wealthy to someone who knows what to look for and unremarkable to everyone else. Straight-backed, economical in movement, the opposite of ornamental. Hair worn simply — neat, controlled, no elaborate styling. Minimal but clearly high-quality jewelry (fine metalwork, not gemstones) — the point is quality of material and tailoring rather than visible quantity of wealth.

## Costume

Emerald and white, tailored rather than draped — a cut that's distinct from both Zamar's flowing scholar robes and Kasar Dutse's utilitarian layers, reflecting Kwara's mercantile rather than martial or religious identity. Fine, plain fabric (a subtle sheen rather than embroidery signals the wealth). No royal or religious symbols worn — a small silver river-fish pin is the only house marking, and even that is minimal enough to double as plain jewelry to an outsider.

## Expression and Performance Notes

Pleasant and unreadable as a default — her face should not telegraph the calculation happening underneath, which is the entire point of the character. The one moment to break this: "that's the first sensible answer anyone in this palace has given me all day" should carry a flicker of a real smile, brief and genuine, immediately controlled again.

## Voice Direction

Pleasant, precise, slightly performative charm that never fully drops — even her warmest lines should carry a trace of transaction underneath. Faster and more clipped only when discussing numbers/terms specifically ("back your claim with Confederacy gold, quietly...") — money is the one subject where her guard relaxes into genuine directness.
