# Character Design Sheet — General Tahir

Appears in: Episode 01 — Act Two.

## Physical Reference

Man, sixties, hard-featured, economical in movement — a career soldier whose body language reads as controlled rather than aggressive. Greying hair worn short and practical. No excess gesture; when he moves it's purposeful. Should look like a man used to being obeyed without needing to raise his voice, similar register to Garba but Zamar-coded rather than Kasar Dutse-coded.

## Costume

Senior Zamar cavalry command uniform — more gold braid and rank marking than Malik's, older-style cut suggesting decades of service, practical rather than ceremonial even at his rank. Same base dark-tunic-with-gold-braid language as the rest of Zamar's military, one tier up in visible seniority.

## Expression and Performance Notes

Blunt and level — Tahir should never look angry, even when pushing Malik hard toward acclamation; his pressure comes from calm certainty, not heat. The final beat ("nods once and says nothing further") needs to read as genuine, if reluctant, respect — not a man biding his time to try again.

## Voice Direction

Blunt, level, unhurried — delivers hard truths at conversational volume, never a raised voice. Respect for Malik should be audible even in his most pressuring lines ("your sister will use every one of those two hundred years against you").
