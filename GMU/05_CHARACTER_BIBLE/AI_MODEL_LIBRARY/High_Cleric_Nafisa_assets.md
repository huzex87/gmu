# Character Design Sheet — High Cleric Nafisa

Appears in: Episode 06 (public legitimacy address, private chamber scene, Yusuf encounter), Episode 09 (presides over ratification vote).

## Physical Reference

Woman, sixties, upright and austere bearing — should read as spiritual and legal authority combined, distinct from every other authority figure in the series. Calm, unhurried features; the kind of face that has spent decades speaking to large public gatherings without needing to raise her voice.

## Costume

Unornamented formal Ulama dress — deliberately outside the indigo/gold (Zamar), red/grey (Kasar Dutse), emerald/white (Kwara) kingdom-color system established in GMU-002. Recommend a fourth, faith-specific palette: deep neutral tones (off-white, soft grey, muted olive or sand) with understated geometric embroidery echoing the Covenant's visual motifs (the open book, the compass rose) without borrowing directly from Zamar's state colors, reinforcing that the Faith of the Covenant's authority is independent of Zamar's throne even though it is centered in Zamar's capital. This is a departure worth flagging explicitly to GMU-002's owner for formal color-system approval before wide use.

## Expression and Performance Notes

Measured and precise even under pressure — her Episode 6 public address should be delivered with total composure, no visible nerves, so the weight of her words carries entirely through content rather than performance. Her private chamber scene with Fatima and Malik allows slightly more warmth and even self-deprecating honesty ("one I don't have a fully honest answer to yet"). Her Episode 6 closing scene with Yusuf should play with genuine, gradually dawning unease — the first character in the series to register something is wrong about him from a single conversation, without yet having any evidence.

## Voice Direction

Measured, carrying without needing volume — a voice built for large public spaces (the Sanctuary address) that can also drop to genuine warmth or unease in close, private exchanges. No theatrical flourish; her authority should sound entirely earned rather than performed.
