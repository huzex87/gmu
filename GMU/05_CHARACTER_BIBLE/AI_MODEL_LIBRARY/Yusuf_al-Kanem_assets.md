# Character Design Sheet — Yusuf al-Kanem

Appears in: Episode 01 — Act One, Act Two, Act Four.

## Physical Reference

Man, fifties, deliberately unremarkable — this is the single most important design instruction on this sheet. Medium height, medium build, no scars, no distinguishing marks, forgettable bone structure. Neutral, well-groomed but not stylish. The character-bible note that "his face is hard to describe five minutes after meeting him" needs to be an actual, deliberate design choice, not a failure to design a character — the temptation to make him more visually interesting or sinister-looking should be resisted. If anything, he should look slightly more reassuring/kindly than average, since that is how he functions in every scene.

## Costume

Zamar court dress, unornamented — same general silhouette language as other Zamar officials but without any of the gold trim, sashes, or rank markers that make Fatima, Malik, or Kamal immediately legible at a glance. Muted, neutral tones (grey-blue, muted brown) rather than Zamar's indigo/gold, so he visually recedes even while standing in Zamar spaces.

## Expression and Performance Notes

Soft, reasonable, endlessly composed — never shows urgency even when moving with unusual speed (the Act One entrance). The single visual "tell" the season is allowed, per the Episode 1 continuity notes: his silent letter-passing (Act Two) and his one-beat-too-controlled entrance at Bashir's study (Act Four) should both be played completely without emotional emphasis — no lingering, no meaningful glance to camera. The audience should only recognize these as significant on a second viewing, after Episode 9's reveal.

## Voice Direction

Soft-spoken, unhurried, reassuring — the vocal equivalent of the visual design. Should sound like the most trustworthy person in any room. No tonal shift during the letter-passing or study-entrance scenes; the wrongness has to be entirely visual/behavioral (timing, stillness), never vocal, until Episode 9.
