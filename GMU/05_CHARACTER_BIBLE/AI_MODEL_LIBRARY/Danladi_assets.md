# Character Design Sheet — Danladi

Appears in: Episode 01 — Act Two.

## Physical Reference

Man, 24, West African features, rougher-edged and less polished than the officers around him in later scenes — commoner-born and it should read in his bearing, which is capable but not aristocratic. Slightly built compared to Malik, wiry rather than bulky. Wears his rank insignia like it still faintly surprises him — a small but important physical note for whoever's directing performance: he should occasionally touch or adjust his rank markers, an unconscious tell that they're new to him.

## Costume

Junior Zamar cavalry uniform — same family of design as Malik's but visibly lower rank (less gold braid, plainer cut, no command markers). Off-duty in his one Episode 1 scene, so it should read as slightly informal — collar open, no ceremonial elements, closer to what a soldier wears on a day off than on parade.

## Expression and Performance Notes

Open, a little uncertain — his scene is built around not having an answer for the Old Woman, and his face should show him actually searching for one rather than being confidently dismissive. This is the audience's entry point into "ordinary Zamar," so his sincerity matters more than his charisma.

## Voice Direction

Plain, unpolished, sincere — noticeably less rhetorically trained than any of the palace characters, which should come through in simpler sentence rhythm as much as delivery. No performance of confidence he doesn't have.
