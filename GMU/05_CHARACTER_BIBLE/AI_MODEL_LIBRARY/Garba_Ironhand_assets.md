# Character Design Sheet — King Garba Ironhand

Appears in: Episode 01 — Act One, Act Two.

## Physical Reference

Man, mid-fifties, weathered and scarred, grey-streaked beard kept short and practical rather than groomed for court. Build heavier and more solid than Malik's — a fighter who won his crown physically, not a ceremonial monarch. Carries himself with total stillness at rest; he doesn't posture because he's never needed to. One notable scar crossing the left eyebrow (old, from the fight that took the throne from his brother — worth keeping visible and unexplained in Episode 1, to pay off later if the show wants to dramatize that history).

## Costume

Deep red and iron-grey layered garments, deliberately minimal ornament — this should visually contrast hard against Zamar's ceremonial richness. A simple crossed-spear sigil worn as a small pin or clasp rather than an elaborate royal seal. Practical outerwear suitable for standing on a fortress wall in wind — a heavy cloak or wrap, iron-grey, with the red showing underneath at the collar and cuffs only. No crown or headpiece in these scenes — Kasar Dutse's kingship is not costume-dependent the way Zamar's ratification ceremony implies Zamar's might be; this is a deliberate visual argument for GMU-002 to weigh in on later.

## Expression and Performance Notes

Unhurried in every register — Garba should be the calmest person in any scene he's in, including the cavalry-yard mobilization. His threat reads through patience, not aggression. The closing V.O.-adjacent line ("let's see what a kingdom of scholars does with a countdown") should play almost gentle, which is what makes it unsettling.

## Voice Direction

Low register, slow and deliberate pacing, short weighted sentences — never rushes, never raises volume even when giving orders ("send word to the war-captains" should be delivered at conversational volume, not a shout). This is the vocal opposite of a typical "warlord" performance — the menace is in the calm.
