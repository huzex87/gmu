# Character Design Sheet — Malik ibn Adamu

Appears in: Episode 01 — Act One, Act Two, Act Four.

## Physical Reference

Man, mid-twenties (26), Sahel-inspired West/Central African features, built like a working cavalry officer rather than a ceremonial prince — broader through the shoulders than Fatima, visible ease of movement, a couple of small training scars (knuckles, one faint line near the jaw) that read as earned rather than decorative. Close-cropped hair or short natural style, no headwear in casual/training scenes. Open, expressive face — his emotions show before he speaks, the visual opposite of Fatima's stillness.

## Costume

**Act One (cavalry grounds):** Practical training leathers — sleeveless or short-sleeved leather vest over a plain tunic, wrapped forearm guards, dust-colored fabric, nothing ceremonial. Should look genuinely worn, not costume-clean.

**Act One (Sultan's Chamber, Diwan Hall onward) / Act Two:** Zamar cavalry command uniform — dark tunic with gold braid at the shoulders and cuffs denoting rank, layered under the same unbleached mourning-white wrap Fatima wears from Act Two onward (the mourning cloth should be identical in texture/cut logic across all characters, just draped differently over each base costume).

**Act Two (war tent, per Character Bible costume note):** Red sash added once his faction begins pressing the acclamation option — should be subtle at first (a single sash, not head-to-toe red) so its escalation across later episodes has room to grow.

## Expression and Performance Notes

Default resting expression: warm, quick to smile, genuinely likable — this needs to read clearly in Act One's cavalry-grounds scene so his later refusal to bypass the Diwan (against Tahir's pressure) lands as a real cost rather than an easy choice. Under pressure his face goes still in a different way than Fatima's — hers is control, his is closer to shock or hurt, more legible. Watch for the "quiet, but final" line to Tahir — this should be the most controlled he gets in the whole episode.

## Voice Direction

Warmer, more casual cadence than Fatima's, faster default pace, genuine laugh (used once, in the cavalry-grounds banter). Drops the casualness entirely in the Tahir war-tent scene and the Sultan's Chamber scene — voice should tighten and slow without becoming as clipped as Fatima's; his control reads as effort, hers reads as habit.
