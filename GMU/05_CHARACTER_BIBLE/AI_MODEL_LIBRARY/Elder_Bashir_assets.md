# Character Design Sheet — Elder Bashir

Appears in: Episode 01 — Act Two, Act Three (unseen killer implied), Act Four (deceased).

## Physical Reference

Man, seventies, gentle-eyed, moves slowly and deliberately — should read as physically frail in a way that makes his murder land as an act against someone genuinely vulnerable, not a fair fight. White or grey beard, kept neat. Warm lines around the eyes from age and habitual gentleness rather than stress. Slightly stooped posture.

## Costume

Diwan formal robes — unornamented despite his seniority, a deliberate humility that should read as a values statement rather than low status (he outranks nearly everyone else in scholar's robes in the story). Muted, warm-toned fabric, no gold sash (contrast this deliberately against Fatima's, to signal he's past needing to display rank).

## Expression and Performance Notes

Warm and self-deprecating as a baseline, but let real weight show in the private scene with Fatima — the humor should not fully mask that he's frightened of what he's found. His death tableau (Act Four) needs careful, respectful staging: slumped at the desk, peaceful rather than violent-looking, so the horror comes from what it implies rather than graphic detail — consistent with the season's "politics before violence" restraint.

## Voice Direction

Warm, unhurried, faintly wry — pauses are part of his rhythm, not hesitation. The line "at my age those are usually the same sentence" should land as genuinely funny before the scene turns serious. Slight tremor is optional and should be used sparingly, only in the private ledger warning to Fatima, to signal fear rather than infirmity.
