# Character Design Sheet — Queen Mother Halima

Appears in: Episode 03 (introduction), Episode 06 (public dais scene), Episode 10 (closing mother-daughter scene).

## Physical Reference

Woman, mid-fifties, upright and composed bearing — the visual register of someone who has spent decades being carefully watched and has made peace with it. Warm facial features that soften considerably only in private scenes with Fatima or Malik. Should read as an older, more settled echo of Fatima's own restraint, so the family resemblance is visually legible without needing dialogue to establish it.

## Costume

Restrained indigo and muted gold — deliberately closer to Fatima's plain scholar's robes than to the Great Hall's ceremonial richness. Mourning-adjacent tones (deeper indigo, less gold trim than a reigning Sultana would wear) persist even months after Adamu III's death, visually signaling that she has not stopped mourning even while performing neutrality in public. For the Episode 6 dais scene, allow slightly more formal structure to the robe (a more defined silhouette, a simple gold circlet) since she is addressing the entire city — but keep ornamentation minimal relative to Fatima's eventual coronation dress in Episode 10, so Fatima's crowning still reads as the visual high point of the season.

## Expression and Performance Notes

Warm but exacting — she should never look indecisive, even when her dialogue is deliberately withholding a preference between her children. Her stillness is a choice, not an absence. In the Episode 6 courtyard scene, played with visible cost: this is a woman spending real emotional capital to hold a public position, not someone comfortable doing it. Episode 10's private scene should allow the most physical warmth she's shown all season — a hand on Fatima's face, unhurried eye contact — since it's the only scene where she isn't performing for an audience of any kind.

## Voice Direction

Measured, warm underneath a controlled surface — should sound like someone whose every word has been considered before it's spoken, without ever sounding cold. Reserve genuine vocal warmth (softer register, slower pacing) exclusively for private scenes with her children; public dialogue (Episode 6) should carry more formal weight and deliberate pacing, audibly costing her something to deliver.
