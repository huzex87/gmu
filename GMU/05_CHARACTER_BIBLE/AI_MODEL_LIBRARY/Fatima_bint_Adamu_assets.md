# Character Design Sheet — Fatima bint Adamu

Appears in: Episode 01 — Teaser (implied), Act One, Act Two, Act Three, Act Four (all scenes involving her).

## Physical Reference

Woman, late twenties (29), Sahel-inspired West/Central African features, tall and long-limbed with deliberate, unhurried posture — she never fidgets, which reads as either calm or calculation depending on who's watching. Dark eyes that hold a gaze rather than dropping it first. Hair worn in neat, tight braids gathered back under a simple indigo headwrap for formal scenes, looser and uncovered only in the most private moments (the antechamber scene with Bashir could go either way — recommend covered, since she's received Bashir in a quasi-official capacity). Minimal jewelry — a single signet ring (her own seal as a Diwan jurist, not a royal one) rather than royal ornamentation, reinforcing that she presents as a scholar first, princess second.

## Costume

**Primary (Acts One–Three, pre-mourning-white overlay):** Deep indigo scholar's robe, floor-length, structured shoulders, minimal embroidery — geometric gold thread trim at the cuffs and hem only, echoing the Zamar sigil (open book beneath a compass rose) without being ostentatious. Gold jurist's sash worn diagonally across the torso, the mark of her Diwan-earned rank.

**Mourning overlay (Act Two onward):** Unbleached mourning-white outer wrap layered over the indigo robe — the white is undyed, slightly rough-textured, deliberately unroyal, worn by everyone in the city regardless of rank during the mourning period. This should read as a visual leveler: for the length of Act Two and Three, Fatima looks more like everyone else in Birnin Zamar than she does like a princess.

**Reference silhouette:** Structured but not stiff — robes should move naturally when she walks fast (the Act One dash from the Scholars' Hall needs to read as urgent, not elegant).

## Expression and Performance Notes

Default resting expression: attentive, faintly appraising, rarely fully relaxed even in private moments. Smiles are small, brief, and usually a little dry rather than warm — reserve a genuinely warm, unguarded expression for the moment with Malik in the Sultan's Chamber (Act One) so it reads as a departure from her norm. Under pressure (the clergyman scene, the Amina negotiation) she gets stiller, not more animated — stillness is her tell for "this matters."

## Voice Direction

Measured pace, low-to-mid register, minimal vocal fry or uptalk — she should never sound like she's asking permission. Very slight increase in clipped precision when she's controlling anger (the Amina negotiation, the clergyman scene). Reserve genuine vocal warmth for scenes with Malik and Bashir only.
