# GMU-005 — Character Bible

Estimated full length: ~450 pages. This v0.2 draft covers the six principal Season One characters — enough to write against for the Series Bible and Episode One. Supporting and minor characters get added as episodes require them, per the "just enough, just in time" principle established in GMU-004.

## Principal Cast

### Fatima bint Adamu

**Title:** Princess of Zamar, elder child of Sultan Adamu III
**Age:** 29
**Affiliation:** Sultanate of Zamar
**Physical description:** Tall, deliberate in her movements, dresses plainly for a princess — favors the indigo robes of a Diwan scholar over royal ornament, a choice that reads to some as humility and to others as calculation.
**Biography:** Trained from childhood not as an heir but as a jurist, at her own insistence, over her father's early objections — Zamar has never had a female Sultan, and Adamu III assumed the throne would pass to Malik. Fatima earned a seat as the youngest associate scholar in the Diwan's history at twenty-two. She loves her brother genuinely, which is what makes the succession crisis a tragedy rather than a simple rivalry.
**Personality and voice:** Precise, patient, rarely raises her voice — she has learned that in rooms full of men waiting for her to be emotional, calm is a weapon. Dry humor she shows only to people she trusts.
**Want:** To be ratified Sultana on the strength of the law and her own competence, without having to become someone she doesn't respect to get there.
**Wound:** Grew up constantly proving she belonged in rooms built for men; still flinches, however briefly, at being underestimated.
**Lie she believes:** That if she plays entirely by the rules, the rules will actually protect her — a belief the season systematically dismantles.
**Arc across Season One:** Wins the throne by Episode 10, but only after compromising the very principle — legitimacy through law, not deals — that made her worth rooting for. Ends the season having become, in one specific way, more like the system she meant to reform.
**Costume/rank markers:** Indigo scholar's robe over the gold sash of a Diwan-ratified jurist; earns the right to royal gold trim only after ratification.

### Malik ibn Adamu

**Title:** Prince of Zamar, younger child of Sultan Adamu III
**Age:** 26
**Affiliation:** Sultanate of Zamar (military faction)
**Physical description:** Built like the cavalry officer he trained as; wears his rank openly, gold braid on a soldier's cut rather than royal robes.
**Biography:** Always assumed he would inherit — not out of arrogance but because it was simply what everyone, including his father, told him growing up. Commissioned into the Zamar cavalry at eighteen and genuinely earned the loyalty he now leans on; this is not a hollow prince playing soldier.
**Personality and voice:** Direct, warm with people he trusts, uncomfortable with the political maneuvering the crisis forces on him. Not stupid — just conflict-averse in a way that his advisors mistake for weakness and exploit.
**Want:** The throne he was raised to expect, and to prove he can hold it without his sister's help.
**Wound:** Suspects, quietly, that Fatima is better suited to rule than he is, and hates himself for how much that thought costs him.
**Lie he believes:** That loyalty from soldiers is the same thing as legitimacy — a belief Kasar Dutse and his own advisors both exploit.
**Arc across Season One:** Is pushed by his faction toward declaring himself Sultan by military acclamation, bypassing the Diwan entirely (Episode 4) — the season's clearest test of Creative Principle "politics before violence." By Episode 9 he chooses, at real cost to himself, not to break the constitutional process even though it would hand him the crown — the most morally admirable act in the season, and one that costs him the throne.
**Costume/rank markers:** Zamar cavalry command insignia; red sash added once his faction moves toward open acclamation.

### King Garba Ironhand

**Title:** King of Kasar Dutse
**Age:** mid-50s
**Affiliation:** Kingdom of Kasar Dutse
**Physical description:** Scarred, grey-bearded, moves and speaks like a man who has never needed to raise his voice to be obeyed.
**Biography:** Took the throne of Kasar Dutse in his youth the way the kingdom requires — by winning the war-captains' loyalty, including against his own elder brother. Has spent thirty years building Kasar Dutse's leverage over the mountain passes and waiting, patiently, for exactly the kind of instability Adamu III's death has created.
**Personality and voice:** Speaks in short, weighted sentences; genuinely believes in Kasar Dutse's honor code even when he's manipulating people who don't share it — he is not a cynic, he is a true believer in a harder set of rules.
**Want:** Permanent, treaty-guaranteed control of the mountain passes — the throne of Zamar itself holds no appeal for him.
**Wound:** Won his crown by defeating his own brother; has never fully made peace with what that cost him, and sees echoes of it in Fatima and Malik's rivalry.
**Lie he believes:** That taking the passes by leverage rather than open war makes him the reasonable party in the conflict, not an aggressor.
**Arc across Season One:** Escalates from watchful opportunist to open (if ambiguously justified) military action by Episode 8, ending the season holding disputed ground and a dangerous piece of leverage — evidence about Adamu III's death — that positions him as a driver of Season Two.
**Costume/rank markers:** Deep red and iron-grey, minimal ornament, the crossed-spear sigil worn as a simple pin rather than elaborate regalia — deliberately understated next to Zamar's ceremonial richness.

### Speaker Amina Bello Kwarafi

**Title:** Speaker of the Merchant-Council, River Confederacy of Kwara
**Age:** mid-40s
**Affiliation:** River Confederacy of Kwara
**Physical description:** Impeccably, expensively understated; dresses to be underestimated by outsiders and instantly recognized by anyone who matters.
**Biography:** Rose through Kwarafi's merchant houses on financial instinct rather than birth, becoming Speaker by out-maneuvering three rival houses without a single act of open conflict. Kwara's entire strategy — profit from stability or chaos equally, be no one's enemy and no one's friend — is one she personally perfected.
**Personality and voice:** Charming in a way that never quite lets you forget it's a tool; extremely precise about money, deliberately vague about everything else.
**Want:** Favorable trade terms locked in with whoever wins the succession — she has no stake in who sits on Zamar's throne, only in what it costs her to do business with them.
**Wound:** Kwara was nearly destroyed by a trade war a generation before she was born; her entire worldview is built on making sure the Confederacy never again has that little leverage over its own survival.
**Lie she believes:** That she can stay above the succession crisis indefinitely by financing both sides — Episode 5 forces her to discover that neutrality has a shelf life once one side realizes what she's doing.
**Arc across Season One:** Goes from quiet kingmaker-for-profit to a woman who has to actually choose a side by the finale, having built real (if transactional) trust with Fatima along the way.
**Costume/rank markers:** Emerald and white, minimal royal signaling — Kwara deliberately dresses down compared to Zamar and Kasar Dutse to reinforce its "we're just merchants" posture.

### Yusuf al-Kanem

**Title:** Royal Advisor and Spymaster to the Zamar throne
**Age:** 50s
**Affiliation:** Nominally Zamar; true loyalty revealed late in the season
**Physical description:** Unremarkable by design — the kind of face that's hard to describe five minutes after meeting him, which is exactly the point.
**Biography:** Served Adamu III faithfully (as far as anyone can tell) for two decades, running Zamar's intelligence network. Trusted by both Fatima and Malik individually, which is itself a warning sign neither of them registers until too late.
**Personality and voice:** Soft-spoken, endlessly reasonable-sounding, the kind of advisor who makes every bad idea sound like the responsible choice.
**Want:** To ensure that whoever ultimately wins Zamar's throne does so only after being fully tested and shaped by the cost of winning — chaos was never his goal, it was his method, deployed on behalf of an unnamed organization ("the fourth power," per GMU-003 §3.5) that has operated inside multiple courts across the Crescent for longer than Zamar's monarchy has existed.
**Wound/Lie:** Genuinely believes patience and long-view manipulation are a kind of mercy compared to open war — he tells Fatima directly (Episode 9) that he "removed the reasons" the siblings had to pretend their rivalry wasn't real, framing himself as a revealer of truth rather than its author. This is both self-serving and, uncomfortably, not entirely wrong, which is the point.
**Arc across Season One:** Trusted confidant to both siblings through Episode 8, planting the Kasar Dutse coin and Kwara silver trail (via Abubakar) to keep Fatima's investigation pointed outward. Exposed in Episode 9 at the Old Customs House confrontation: admits to ordering Bashir's murder (executed by Abubakar) but denies killing Sultan Adamu III, whose death he confirms was natural, though he ensured no one was prepared for it. Imprisoned rather than executed or freed at the close of Episode 10 — held in a cell beneath the palace as a living, unresolved Season Two thread.
**Costume/rank markers:** Zamar court dress, deliberately unornamented. Post-reveal (Episodes 9–10), his true signet — first glimpsed in Episode 3, distinct from all three known kingdoms' marks — becomes a key visual identifier; this should be designed once and used consistently across GMU-002 (Brand Bible) and any future Season Two material featuring the fourth power.

### Danladi

**Title:** Cavalry Lieutenant, Zamar army (Malik's faction)
**Age:** 24
**Affiliation:** Sultanate of Zamar (military faction) — the season's ground-level POV
**Physical description:** Younger and rougher-edged than the officers around him; wears his rank like it still surprises him.
**Biography:** Commoner-born, promoted on merit under Malik's command — owes Malik his career and his loyalty, uncomplicated by the politics consuming the royal family. Gives the audience a way into the succession crisis that isn't a palace or a council chamber.
**Personality and voice:** Plainspoken, loyal to a fault, increasingly unsettled as he's asked to do things in Malik's name that Malik himself would never have asked a year ago.
**Want:** To protect Malik and to believe the man he serves is still the man he signed up to follow.
**Wound:** Comes from a border village that has already felt the cost of Kasar Dutse's pass tolls — the political conflict is existential for people like him in a way it isn't for the royals.
**Lie he believes:** That loyalty means never questioning orders — Episode 8 (Tsauri Pass) breaks this.
**Arc across Season One:** The audience's barometer for how far Malik's faction has drifted from its own honor code; his growing doubt in Episodes 6–8 mirrors Malik's own.

## Supporting Cast (added as episodes require them)

### Elder Bashir

**Title:** Senior member of the Diwan al-Ilm, second only to Kamal in seniority
**Age:** 70s
**Affiliation:** Sultanate of Zamar (Diwan)
**Physical description:** Gentle-eyed, moves slowly, pours his own tea rather than let a servant do it — a small habit that reads as humility to everyone who's ever met him.
**Biography:** The late Sultan Adamu III's most trusted counselor on the Diwan; introduced in Episode 1, Act Two, meeting privately with Fatima. Notices, before anyone else does, that Yusuf al-Kanem has twice stalled his request to review the Sultan's final ledgers.
**Personality and voice:** Warm, self-deprecating, precise underneath the warmth — the kind of old man whose casual remarks turn out, on reflection, to have been carefully chosen.
**Want:** To understand what actually happened to Adamu III before the Diwan ratifies anyone.
**Wound/Lie:** Believes his seniority and reputation make him safe to ask uncomfortable questions openly. This gets him killed.
**Arc across Season One:** Murdered at the end of Episode 2 — the season's inciting mystery. His Episode 1 warning to Fatima ("be careful who you trust with what you don't yet know") becomes the audience's first real clue, in hindsight, toward Yusuf.
**Costume/rank markers:** Diwan formal robes, unornamented, deliberately understated for his seniority.

### General Tahir

**Title:** Senior General, Zamar military
**Age:** 60s
**Affiliation:** Sultanate of Zamar (Malik's faction)
**Physical description:** Hard-featured, economical in movement and speech.
**Biography:** The most senior general backing Malik's claim; introduced in Episode 1, Act Two, pressing Malik toward military acclamation over Diwan process. Not a villain — a pragmatist who genuinely believes Fatima's slower path risks Kasar Dutse reaching the throne question before Zamar resolves it internally.
**Personality and voice:** Blunt, respectful of Malik without being deferential — the kind of general who tells a prince the truth precisely because he respects him.
**Want:** Zamar secured against Kasar Dutse, by whatever legitimate-enough means gets there fastest.
**Wound/Lie:** Believes speed and law are opposites — Malik's Episode 1 refusal to bypass the Diwan is his first real friction with Tahir, a tension that should recur through the acclamation pressure beat in Episode 4.
**Arc across Season One:** Escalating advocate for the acclamation option; his reaction to Malik's Episode 9 choice (per GMU-003) should be a key beat once that episode is drafted.

### Elder Kamal

**Title:** Senior scholar of the Diwan al-Ilm, presiding elder
**Age:** 60s
**Affiliation:** Sultanate of Zamar (Diwan)
**Physical description:** Composed, dry-humored, the visual and temperamental image of institutional authority — the kind of face that reads as "the rules" before he says a word.
**Biography:** Presides over the Diwan al-Ilm; sparring partner and quiet admirer of Fatima's competence from her earliest days challenging the Diwan's assumptions (Episode 1, Act One). Formally announces the forty-day ratification law. Discovers Bashir's body in Act Four, alongside Fatima and Malik — the loss visibly costs him his usual composure.
**Personality and voice:** Dry, exacting, tests people by disagreeing with them — his Act One sparring with Fatima ("you'd have us negotiate with merchants the way we negotiate with children") is provocation, not genuine objection, and he telegraphs this with a suppressed almost-smile.
**Want:** A ratification process the whole Crescent will respect as legitimate, regardless of which sibling wins.
**Wound/Lie:** Believes procedure alone can absorb any succession crisis — Bashir's murder (Act Four) is the first crack in that belief, visible in his loss of composure ("nothing leaves this room until I say so. Nothing.").
**Arc across Season One:** The Diwan's institutional conscience; his reaction to the murder should track the season's central question of whether law can survive contact with real danger.
**Costume/rank markers:** Diwan formal robes, senior rank markers more visible than Bashir's understated version — Kamal performs institutional authority where Bashir declined to.

### Queen Mother Halima

**Title:** Queen Mother of Zamar, widow of Sultan Adamu III
**Age:** mid-50s
**Affiliation:** Sultanate of Zamar (formally neutral)
**Physical description:** Composed and unhurried in every movement, dresses in restrained mourning-adjacent tones even months after Adamu III's death — deliberately understated next to the ceremonial richness of the rest of the court, closer in spirit to Fatima's plain scholar's dress than to royal ornament.
**Biography:** Married Adamu III in his youth and watched him build Zamar's forty-year peace from close enough to understand exactly what it cost him. Has stayed publicly neutral through the entire succession crisis, a position she maintains not from indecision but from a considered belief that a Queen Mother's preference should never outweigh the Diwan's law. Introduced in Episode 3, when Fatima first comes to her in confidence.
**Personality and voice:** Warm but unsentimental, speaks in the register of someone who has spent decades being carefully listened to and has learned to make every word count. Capable of real tenderness with her children in private that she never shows in public.
**Want:** To see both her children survive this crisis as people, regardless of which one survives it as Sultan.
**Wound:** Has already watched one marriage cost her everything she might have wanted for herself personally, in service of Zamar's stability; sees the same trade being asked of both her children now and cannot spare either of them from it.
**Lie she believes:** That staying neutral protects both her children equally — Episode 6 forces her to discover that neutrality, like Amina's, has a form that still requires a costly public choice, even when it isn't a choice between them.
**Arc across Season One:** Introduced as Fatima's confidante (Episode 3); forced into a public appearance by Covenant pressure (Episode 6), where she extracts a mutual vow from both siblings rather than naming a preference; closes the season with a quiet, honest mother-daughter scene the night of Fatima's coronation (Episode 10).
**Costume/rank markers:** Restrained indigo and muted gold, minimal royal ornament — visually closer to Fatima's scholar dress than to ceremonial court wear, reinforcing her chosen neutrality.

### Commander Rafiu

**Title:** Garrison Commander, Zamar military (eastern approach to Birnin Zamar)
**Age:** late 50s
**Affiliation:** Sultanate of Zamar (military, formally under Malik's command structure)
**Physical description:** Older and more weathered than the officers around Malik and Tahir, moves like a man who has spent a career in the field rather than at court; economical, unhurried.
**Biography:** Commands sixteen hundred men holding the eastern approach to the capital. Deliberately did not sign General Tahir's acclamation petition in Episode 3 — not out of doubt in Malik, but from a personal principle that loyalty is earned in person, not collected on parchment. His son serves in the undersupplied border garrison at the pass, which becomes the leverage point (and genuine grievance) that shapes his Episode 4 exchange with Malik.
**Personality and voice:** Plainspoken, unimpressed by rank for its own sake, direct in a way that reads as insubordinate to some officers and refreshingly honest to Malik specifically.
**Want:** A Zamar that actually takes care of the soldiers holding its hardest, least glamorous postings — not abstract loyalty tests.
**Wound:** Watching his own son go underpaid and underequipped at the border while the capital garrisons are prioritized for a possible acclamation crisis he personally refuses to participate in.
**Lie he believes:** That loyalty bought with back-pay and better supply is meaningfully different from loyalty bought with a signed petition — Malik's Episode 4 decision forces both of them to sit with how thin that distinction actually is.
**Arc across Season One:** Introduced Episode 4; his back-pay request becomes Malik's first real compromise of the season. Reappears Episode 7 (delivering casualty reports from the pass seizure) and Episode 8 (formally authorizing Danladi's village liaison role) — a quiet mentor figure to Danladi across the back half of the season.
**Costume/rank markers:** Standard Zamar cavalry command insignia, visibly plainer and more worn than Tahir's — a deliberate contrast between career field officer and polished senior general.

### High Cleric Nafisa

**Title:** High Cleric of the Faith of the Covenant, Great Sanctuary of Birnin Zamar
**Age:** 60s
**Affiliation:** The Faith of the Covenant (Islam, per GMU-004 §1) — formally independent of all three political powers
**Physical description:** Austere, upright bearing, dressed in the unornamented formal robes of senior Ulama rather than any royal or noble house's colors — visually and deliberately distinct from every other authority figure in the series.
**Biography:** Leads the Covenant's clergy in Birnin Zamar. Has stayed formally silent on the succession itself throughout the season, as doctrine and custom require, but breaks that silence in Episode 6 to publicly condition the Covenant's blessing on legitimacy being won through law rather than force — a direct, public intervention with real consequences for every faction, including the two siblings she has otherwise stayed carefully impartial toward.
**Personality and voice:** Measured, precise, unafraid of confrontation when principle requires it — the season's clearest voice for "knowledge is power, literally" from outside the palace's political factions entirely.
**Want:** A ratification the whole Crescent will recognize as legitimate specifically because it was won honestly, not merely because it was won.
**Wound/Lie:** Believes the Covenant's moral authority operates independently of the political currents around it — her Episode 6 encounter with Yusuf, and his unsettling offer to be a "discreet channel" between the clergy and either faction, is the first crack in that belief, though she does not yet have language for what unsettled her about it.
**Arc across Season One:** Introduced Episode 6, delivering the public legitimacy ultimatum that raises the season's central theme to open text; her unease after the Yusuf exchange becomes one of three independent threads (alongside Fatima's suspicion and Amina's investigation) converging on his exposure in Episode 9, where she presides over the ratification vote alongside Kamal.
**Costume/rank markers:** Unornamented Ulama formal dress, deliberately outside the indigo/gold, red/grey, emerald/white kingdom-color system — the Covenant's visual identity should read as belonging to the faith itself, not to any one kingdom, even though it is centered in Zamar.

### Abubakar

**Title:** Palace Steward (former), Zamar royal household
**Age:** 40s
**Affiliation:** Nominally Zamar household staff; secretly in Yusuf al-Kanem's service
**Physical description:** Older, unassuming, the kind of household presence a palace runs on without anyone quite noticing him individually — deliberately designed to be as forgettable in his register as Yusuf is in his.
**Biography:** A mid-level steward with legitimate, unquestioned access to the palace's private wings, including the service stair connecting to Elder Bashir's study. Recruited by Yusuf, likely years before Season One begins, as the hands-on operative for tasks Yusuf needed executed but never wanted to be seen executing himself. Murders Elder Bashir on Yusuf's instruction (between Episodes 1 and 2) when Bashir's audits come too close to exposing Yusuf's ledger trail; plants the Kasar Dutse coin and later routes suspicion toward the Kwara silver shipment to keep Fatima's investigation pointed outward.
**Personality and voice:** Deferential, forgettable, competent at his household duties — should never register as sinister on screen until Episode 9 confirms what he's done; his fear in the Episode 9 confrontation ("they're close, Yusuf") should read as the first genuinely human, unguarded moment he's given in the whole season.
**Want:** Originally, presumably, whatever payment or protection Yusuf offered him; by Episode 9, simple survival, now that the operation is unraveling.
**Wound/Lie:** Believed proximity to Yusuf's protection made him safe from consequences — Episode 9 proves otherwise.
**Arc across Season One:** Present but unnamed/unidentified through Episodes 1–6 (background household staff); identified by name and implicated via the household staff roster in Episode 7; confronted and taken into custody alongside Yusuf in Episode 9. Per Episode 10, faces Zamar's law in full, distinct from Yusuf's more ambiguous imprisonment, since he held the blade.
**Costume/rank markers:** Plain Zamar household staff dress, no rank markers at all — should be visually indistinguishable from any other palace steward until the plot requires him to be identified.
