# GMU-307 — Costume Bible

Fashion by region, rank, and profession. This document expands GMU-007 §4's costume rendering notes (rank-tier guidance already used across every character sheet) into a full reference — the *why* behind those rules, and enough per-kingdom detail to design a new character's costume without reverse-engineering it from existing sheets.

## 1. Zamar — Law-and-Learning Dress

**Governing principle:** Costume communicates scholarly and legal standing more than wealth. A senior jurist's robes should read as more authoritative than a wealthy merchant's finery, even if the merchant's fabric costs more — this is a deliberate reversal of a "richest = most powerful" visual shorthand, consistent with GMU-304 §1's social-values note that learning outranks money in Zamar.

**Royalty (Fatima, Malik):** Full indigo/gold saturation, rank-appropriate ornament, richest fabric detail — per GMU-007 §4's royalty tier. Fatima's costume should carry visible scholarly markers (a jurist's stole or sash element) alongside royal ones, reflecting her Diwan-facing legitimacy strategy across Season One.

**Clergy/senior officials (Kamal, Bashir, Nafisa):** Formal robes over ceremonial richness — restrained indigo/gold or, for Sanctuary clergy like Nafisa, the faith-neutral palette (Section 4 below), never Zamar's court colors, per GMU-007's existing guardrail against defaulting her to indigo/gold.

**Field/working rank:** Muted, practical, weathered versions of court colors — visible repairs and sun-fading, per GMU-412's established note, reflecting Zamar citizens' functional relationship to the kingdom's colors rather than a costume-department palette.

**Household staff:** Minimal-to-no rank markers, deliberately unremarkable per GMU-414 — Zamar's household service dress should never compete visually with the family it serves.

## 2. Kasar Dutse — Martial Dress

**Governing principle:** Costume communicates combat record and war-band loyalty. Ornament is earned, not inherited by default — a young untested noble should visually read as less adorned than a "Blooded" fighter (GMU-308 §2) of common birth, reversing Zamar's scholarly-credential logic into a martial one.

**Royalty (Garba):** Deep red/iron-grey saturation, but restrained rather than ornate — per GMU-007's existing guardrail against "warlord" caricature (GMU-403), Garba's design should read dignified, not menacing. His costume should carry visible signs of a working war-leader (practical, worn) rather than a purely ceremonial monarch.

**War-captains and senior fighters:** Layered leather and iron plate (per GMU-308 §2), marked with band-specific insignia rather than a single uniform standard — Kasar Dutse's military culture (personal loyalty over centralized command, GMU-309 §2) should show up visually as varied war-band markings rather than uniform kingdom dress.

**Common fighters/field rank:** Practical, unornamented, iron-grey and deep-red accents on otherwise plain gear — visibly built for mountain mobility over display.

**Civilian dress:** Even outside the war-bands, iron-grey and deep-red remain common in everyday clothing (per GMU-304 §2), reflecting how thoroughly the kingdom's martial identity extends into civilian life — this is a genuine point of difference from Zamar, where court colors are largely confined to court contexts.

## 3. Kwara — Mercantile Dress

**Governing principle:** Costume communicates reliability and trading-house standing, not raw wealth display — a Kwara merchant's dress should look competent and put-together rather than ostentatious, consistent with GMU-304 §3's note that reputation for fair dealing is the kingdom's real social currency.

**Speaker/Merchant-Council leadership (Amina):** Emerald/white with silver accents — per GMU-002 §7/§8's established Kwara direction, charming and composed rather than grand. Amina's costume should never read as more elaborate than the situation calls for; her power is procedural (Merchant-Council votes, GMU-309 §3), not monarchical, and her dress should reflect that restraint.

**Trading-house principals:** Emerald and white with silver accents scaled to house standing on the Merchant-Council — visible quality of fabric and tailoring (signaling reliability) rather than heavy ornament.

**Mercenary fleet crews (per GMU-308 §3, already established):** Deliberately non-uniform — hired, not raised. Emerald/white contract colors appear only where a given crew chooses to display them, never as a mandated standard. This inconsistency is intentional and should never be "cleaned up" into a uniform Kwara military look.

**Everyday trade-district dress:** Emerald and white dominate, with silver accents reserved for higher Merchant-Council standing (per GMU-304 §3) — practical, transaction-ready clothing suited to a working port economy.

## 4. The Faith of the Covenant — Cross-Kingdom Clergy Dress

Per GMU-305 (Religion Bible) and GMU-007's existing guardrail, clergy dress is visually independent of all three kingdoms' color systems — neutral stone-adjacent tones (sand, pale grey, warm white) echoing the Sanctuary's own architecture (GMU-306 §4), regardless of which kingdom a given cleric or worshipper is from. High Cleric Nafisa's costume (GMU-413) is the reference standard. Since GMU-305 §6 establishes that Kasar Dutse and Kwara practice the same faith through their own cultural lens, clergy in those kingdoms should still favor this neutral faith palette over their kingdom's court colors, even if everyday worshippers' devotional dress incorporates local materials or minor local styling.

## 5. Rank-Tier Summary (formalizing GMU-007 §4)

| Tier | Zamar | Kasar Dutse | Kwara |
|---|---|---|---|
| Royalty/head of state | Full court color, scholarly markers | Restrained martial saturation, dignified not menacing | Emerald/white, composed not grand |
| Senior officials/clergy | Formal robes, restrained | War-band insignia, layered armor | House-standing signaled by tailoring quality |
| Field/working rank | Muted, weathered court colors | Practical unornamented gear | Practical trade-ready dress |
| Household/background | Deliberately unremarkable | N/A (all field rank is martial) | Deliberately unremarkable |
