# GMU-312 — GMU Ceremonial Court Language

A constructed ceremonial language — referred to in-world simply as **the Old Tongue** — used across all three kingdoms for coronation, royal ceremony, military commands, ancient inscriptions, royal seals, blessings, battle cries, formal court greetings, and legal documents of the highest formality. It is deliberately not a stand-in for Hausa, Arabic, or English, and should never be used for ordinary dialogue — characters speak their everyday language (implicitly English-rendered for the audience, per screenplay convention) and drop into the Old Tongue only for genuinely ceremonial moments, the way a real coronation might briefly use Latin or a liturgical language within an otherwise vernacular ceremony.

**Editorial guardrail:** The Old Tongue predates the three kingdoms' current political division — this is why all three powers, despite their very different cultures, still share it for the highest ceremonial purposes. It should never be portrayed as belonging to any single kingdom or to the Faith of the Covenant specifically; using it is itself a marker of significance regardless of which kingdom or institution is speaking.

## 1. Phonetic and Naming Conventions

The Old Tongue uses consonant-vowel-consonant rhythm, favoring hard stops (*k*, *t*, *d*) and long vowels in ceremonial contexts, giving it a deliberately weightier, more formal sound than the everyday speech patterns implied elsewhere in the series. Words tend toward two or three syllables; longer ceremonial phrases are built by stringing short root words together rather than complex single-word constructions.

**Core root words (building blocks for names and phrases):**

| Root | Meaning |
|---|---|
| *Kael-* | crown / rule |
| *Dun-* | stone / foundation |
| *Sha-* | light / clarity |
| *Mor-* | blood / lineage |
| *Ith-* | word / oath |
| *Vael-* | sky / height |
| *Ren-* | earth / ground |
| *Zol-* | flame / fire |
| *Anu-* | first / origin |
| *Tor-* | strength / endurance |

Compound words are formed by combining roots with a connecting vowel (*-a-*, *-i-*, *-o-*) and an ending particle marking function: *-eth* (noun, formal/sacred), *-en* (verb/action), *-ar* (possessive/of).

## 2. Sample Ceremonial Vocabulary

| Old Tongue | Literal roots | Meaning / Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Kaeleth** | Kael + eth | "The Crown" (sacred/formal sense, not the physical object) |
| **Ithoren** | Ith + oren | "Sworn word" — an oath of the highest formality, used in ratification and succession contexts |
| **Morareth** | Mor + ar + eth | "Of the bloodline" — used in genealogical/succession declarations |
| **Shavael** | Sha + vael | "Light of the sky" — an invocation used in blessings, faith-neutral in the sense that it predates the Faith of the Covenant's arrival but is not in tension with it |
| **Toranu** | Tor + anu | "First strength" / "enduring from the beginning" — used in military and founding contexts |
| **Dunareth** | Dun + ar + eth | "Of the foundation-stone" — used in architectural dedications and founding inscriptions |
| **Zolkaen** | Zol + kaen | "Flame-bearer" — an old title, now largely ceremonial, occasionally invoked for a monarch taking the throne in contested circumstances |

## 3. Ceremonial Phrases (ready for screenplay/production use)

**Coronation blessing (usable in Chapter 19–20's coronation sequence, spoken by the officiating cleric):**
> *"Kaeleth ithoren, Shavael morar. Toranu, anu, dunareth."*
> ("The Crown is sworn, by the light of the sky upon this bloodline. Enduring, as from the beginning, upon this foundation-stone.")

**Battle cry / rally call (usable in border-skirmish chapters):**
> *"Toranu! Zolkaen!"*
> ("Enduring strength! Flame-bearer!" — a call invoking both endurance and the old flame-bearer title, used to rally rather than to name a specific person.)

**Formal court greeting (highest formality, used only for state occasions):**
> *"Ithoren ar Kaeleth."*
> ("By the sworn word of the Crown.")

**Seal/inscription formula (for royal seals, founding stones, or ancient inscriptions referenced in future Master Encyclopedia entries):**
> *"[Name]-ar Dunareth, Anu."*
> ("[Name], of the foundation-stone, from the beginning.")

## 4. Usage Notes for Future Screenplays

The Old Tongue should appear sparingly — no more than a handful of lines per season — precisely so it retains weight when it does appear. It is understood in-world by clergy, senior nobility, and scholars across all three kingdoms (part of why it works as a shared ceremonial layer despite the kingdoms' political division), but not by ordinary citizens, meaning a character invoking it in front of a general crowd is making a deliberate rhetorical choice to invoke old legitimacy rather than simply communicating.
