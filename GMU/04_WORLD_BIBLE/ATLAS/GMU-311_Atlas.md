# GMU-311 — Atlas

A written geography reference for the continent of Afrasah and the Sahel Crescent — mountains, rivers, roads, trade routes, kingdom borders, and named villages. This is a text-based atlas: it establishes the geographic facts and relative positioning every other document should stay consistent with. As of 2026-07-11 it also has a first illustrated map (Section 7) — future, higher-fidelity map art (AI-generated via GMU-006's pipeline, or hand-drawn) should still be produced against the geography fixed here, not the other way around, so the map matches the story rather than the story being reverse-engineered from a map drawn without this reference.

## 1. Continental Overview

The story takes place on the continent of **Afrasah**, within a region its peoples call **the Sahel Crescent** — a band of savanna, river delta, and highland stretching between a northern desert and a southern coastline (GMU-004 §1). The Crescent's shape is literal: a curved belt of habitable, tradeable land bounded by harsher terrain on both sides, which is precisely why all three powers' territory sits within it rather than spreading further north or south.

## 2. The Three Powers' Territory (relative geography)

**The Sultanate of Zamar** occupies the Crescent's central belt — savanna and river-adjacent farmland surrounding its capital, **Birnin Zamar**. Zamar sits geographically between Kasar Dutse's highlands (north) and Kwara's river delta (south), which is not incidental: Zamar's entire economic and political position depends on being the necessary middle ground both other powers' trade has to pass through or near (GMU-310 §2).

**The Kingdom of Kasar Dutse** occupies the Crescent's northern highland edge, where the savanna rises into the mountain range containing **the pass** — the single viable route connecting the northern desert trade routes to the southern delta (GMU-004 §2.2). **Tsauri Keep**, the capital, is built directly into this pass rather than in open highland territory, making the kingdom's capital and its most valuable strategic asset the same physical location.

**The River Confederacy of Kwara** occupies the Crescent's southern edge, where the region's principal river meets the coast in a wide delta. **The Port of Kwarafi** sits at the delta's mouth, giving Kwara control of the point where river trade becomes seaborne trade — the reason Kwara's mercenary fleet (GMU-308 §3) is naval/marine rather than land-based.

## 3. The Pass (Kasar Dutse's central geographic asset)

The pass is narrow enough that a small, well-positioned force can hold it against a much larger one — the geographic fact underlying Kasar Dutse's entire military doctrine (GMU-308 §2) and its ability to tax trade disproportionate to the kingdom's actual population or wealth. There is no viable land alternative connecting the northern desert routes to the southern delta; goods that don't pass through Kasar Dutse's toll either travel a much longer, more dangerous route around the highlands, or don't move north-south at all. This single geographic fact is the reason the Zamar–Kasar Dutse relationship has remained "taxed trade — and tension" for two centuries (GMU-004 §3) rather than resolving into either war or full cooperation: neither side can afford to actually break the arrangement.

## 4. Rivers and the Delta

The river that feeds Kwara's delta rises somewhere north of Zamar's territory (exact headwaters not yet plot-relevant, left undefined per this document's "just enough" scoping) and flows south past Birnin Zamar before fanning into the delta at Kwarafi. This river is Zamar's primary internal transport route as well as the backbone of Kwara's canal-based trade infrastructure (GMU-306 §3) — the same waterway serves both kingdoms' very different economic models, which is part of why Kwara has every incentive to keep good relations with whoever controls Zamar's upstream territory.

## 5. Roads and Trade Routes

**The Northern Route:** Desert trade → the pass (Kasar Dutse toll) → Zamar's northern approach to Birnin Zamar. The route most directly affected by Kasar Dutse's military posture, and the one Garba's pressure on the border threatens to disrupt across Season One.

**The River Route:** Birnin Zamar → downstream along the river → Kwarafi's delta and onward to sea. The route Amina's financial network operates along, and the one least affected by Kasar Dutse's military maneuvering, which is part of why Kwara can stay comparatively insulated from the northern crisis while still profiting from it.

**The Great Market Convergence:** Both routes' goods ultimately pass through or near the Great Market of Birnin Zamar (GMU-004 §5), reinforcing why Zamar's capital — despite the kingdom's military weakness (GMU-308 §1) — remains the Crescent's genuine economic center.

## 6. Named Villages and Settlements

- **Kantin Rafi** (GMU-509) — a border village near the Zamar/Kasar Dutse frontier, humble vernacular architecture (GMU-306 §1), directly exposed to the tension dramatized in Season One's border episodes.
- Additional villages along both the Northern Route and the River Route exist but are not yet individually named or plot-relevant — add them to the Master Encyclopedia (GMU-320 schema, Places range) only as specific episodes require a named location.
