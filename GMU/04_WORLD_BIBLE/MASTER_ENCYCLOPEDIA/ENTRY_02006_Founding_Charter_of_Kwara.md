# The Founding Charter of Kwara

## Summary

The founding document of the River Confederacy, drafted circa 365 CU after a ruinous trade war nearly destroyed the delta city-states individually — the written agreement that established the rotating Merchant-Council system Kwara still governs by (GMU-309 §3).

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Date drafted | ~365 CU (GMU-004 §3) |
| Custodian | The Merchant-Council of Kwara (ENTRY-06002), held at Kwarafi Merchant House (ENTRY-00008) |
| Core provision | Establishes rotating council representation and prohibits city-states from waging war on one another |

## Description

Unlike Zamar's Seal (ENTRY-02002), which authenticates an ongoing legal apparatus, the Founding Charter is closer to a peace treaty among former rivals — its central, load-bearing clause is the mutual non-aggression provision that has held for over sixty years since. Kwara's merchants reference it less as sacred text and more as a functioning contract, consistent with the Confederacy's pragmatic, commerce-oriented culture (GMU-304 §3, GMU-305 §4) — a telling contrast with how reverently Zamar treats its own founding-era documents.
