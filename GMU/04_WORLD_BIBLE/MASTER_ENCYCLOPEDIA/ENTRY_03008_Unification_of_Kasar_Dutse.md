# The Unification of Kasar Dutse

## Summary

The first "Ironhand" king's unification of the highland clans circa 310 CU, taking the mountain passes by force and beginning two centuries of taxed trade — and tension — with Zamar.

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Date (CU) | ~310 CU (GMU-004 §3) |
| Location | Tsauri Keep and the surrounding highland pass (ENTRY-00005) |
| Key figure | The first "Ironhand" king (ancestor of King Garba Ironhand, ENTRY-01003) |
| Immediate consequence | Establishes Kasar Dutse's control of the only viable pass between the northern desert and southern delta |

## Description

Before unification, Kasar Dutse was "a collection of highland clans" (GMU-004 §3) rather than a single kingdom — the first Ironhand king's achievement was not merely conquest but the creation of the war-captain succession system itself (GMU-309 §2), which has governed the kingdom ever since. This event is the origin point of Kasar Dutse's entire military identity (GMU-308 §2): a kingdom that exists because it took and held a chokepoint by force, and whose politics have run on demonstrated combat strength ever since. It also marks the start of the pass-toll economy (GMU-310 §2) that funds the kingdom to this day.
