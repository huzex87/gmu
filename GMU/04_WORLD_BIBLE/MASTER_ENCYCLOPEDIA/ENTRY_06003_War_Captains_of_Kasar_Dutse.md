# The War-Captains of Kasar Dutse

## Summary

The informal governing institution of Kasar Dutse — collectively, the war-band commanders whose loyalty determines who holds the throne, per the kingdom's unwritten succession law.

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Function | Collective determination of throne legitimacy — "the throne goes to whichever claimant the war-captains will follow" (GMU-004 §2.2) |
| Membership basis | Combat record and personal loyalty of fighters, not birthright alone (GMU-309 §2) |
| Current standing | King Garba Ironhand (ENTRY-01003) holds sustained confidence after thirty years' rule |
| Structure | No formal council body — an informal war council serving as the King's inner circle (GMU-309 §2) |

## Description

Functions as Kasar Dutse's real constitutional order despite having no written charter, consistent with the kingdom's broader preference for personal loyalty and oral tradition over codified law (GMU-304 §2's griot tradition). This is the direct structural parallel to Zamar's Diwan al-Ilm (ENTRY-06001) — both are the real seat of legitimacy-granting power in their respective kingdoms, just organized on opposite principles (institutional deliberation vs. personal loyalty).
