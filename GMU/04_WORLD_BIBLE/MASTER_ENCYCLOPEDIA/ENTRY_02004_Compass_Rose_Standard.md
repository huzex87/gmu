# The Compass Rose Standard of Zamar

## Summary

Zamar's state and battle standard — the compass rose from the Sultanate's seal (ENTRY-02002), rendered in indigo and gold on a field carried by the Covenant Guard at formal state occasions and, historically, into whatever rare field engagements Zamar's small standing force has fought.

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Design | Gold compass rose on an indigo field, matching the state seal's motif (GMU-004 §2.1) |
| Carried by | The Covenant Guard's standard-bearer, a Watch-Captain-or-above appointment (GMU-308 §1) |
| Used for | State processions, palace ceremonies, and (rarely) escort into contested territory |

## Description

Where the Seal of Zamar (ENTRY-02002) authenticates documents, the Compass Rose Standard authenticates presence — it marks a location or procession as under the Sultanate's direct authority. Because the Covenant Guard is a small, defensive force built for palace and city duty rather than field campaigning (GMU-308 §1), the standard appears on-screen far more often in ceremonial contexts (coronations, the ratification vote) than in any military one, reinforcing Zamar's self-image as a power that governs by presence and legitimacy rather than force projection.
