# The Silver River-Fish

## Summary

The river-delta fish species that gives Kwara its heraldic symbol — "a silver river-fish on emerald and white" (GMU-004 §2.3) — and its most characteristic street food.

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Habitat | The river delta surrounding Port of Kwarafi |
| Heraldic use | Kwara's kingdom symbol, rendered silver on emerald and white (GMU-004 §2.3) |
| Culinary use | Kwara's signature street/dock food — grilled and fried, per GMU-315 §3 |
| Trade role | Central to Kwara's fishing economy, distinct from its better-known merchant/banking identity |

## Description

Unlike Zamar's compass-rose-and-book symbol or Kasar Dutse's crossed spears — both abstract or man-made — Kwara's chosen symbol is a living creature drawn directly from the delta the Confederacy is built on, reinforcing the kingdom's self-image as inseparable from its river geography (GMU-311 §2) rather than from any single ruling institution. Its everyday culinary presence (GMU-315 §3) means the symbol appearing on a Kwara banner and the same fish appearing on a dockside grill are, in-world, the same unremarkable fact of delta life elevated to heraldry.
