# The Great Market of Birnin Zamar

## Summary

The Crescent's largest trade hub, technically neutral ground even during conflict by centuries-old custom (GMU-004 §5) — the economic and culinary crossroads where all three powers trade side by side.

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Location | Birnin Zamar (ENTRY-00001) |
| Status | Neutral ground by custom, not law |
| Function | Clearing-house for the Northern Route (Kasar Dutse pass trade) and River Route (Kwara delta trade) |
| Known For | Cross-kingdom street food, currency exchange between all three powers' coinage |

## Description

Open-air, awninged, deliberately less monumental than the Royal Palace (ENTRY-00002) — the market's power comes from its neutrality and its crowds, not its architecture (GMU-306 §1). Functions as the endpoint for both major trade routes described in the Atlas (GMU-311 §5) and the place where Zamar's, Kasar Dutse's, and Kwara's currencies are informally exchanged (GMU-310 §1). Zamar's most culinarily diverse district (GMU-315 §1), reflecting merchants from all three kingdoms trading in close proximity.

## History

Established as neutral ground by custom rather than formal treaty, predating any documented agreement between the three powers — its neutrality has held for two centuries not because it was legally guaranteed but because disrupting it would damage all three powers' interests simultaneously (GMU-004 §5).
