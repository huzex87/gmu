# Kwarafi Merchant House

## Summary

Speaker Amina Bello Kwarafi's seat of power within the Port of Kwarafi, and her temporary embassy in Birnin Zamar during the coronation — the specific building within the Port that houses Kwara's governing function.

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Location | Port of Kwarafi (ENTRY-00006); a smaller embassy variant in Birnin Zamar |
| Function | Seat of the Merchant-Council's presiding Speaker; counting hall and private office |
| Architectural style | Light timber and pale stone, open canal-facing walls (GMU-306 §3) |
| Concept brief | `19_CONCEPT_ART/GMU-507_Kwarafi_Merchant_House.md` |

## Description

The Counting Hall is Kwara's functional answer to a throne room or war tent — no singular seat of power, just a raised presiding desk surrounded by constantly active clerks, reflecting the Merchant-Council's rotating, transactional governance (GMU-309 §3, ENTRY-06002). The Birnin Zamar embassy variant is a deliberate color intrusion — emerald and white against Zamar's indigo and gold — visually marking Kwara's temporary but real presence inside the capital during the coronation.
