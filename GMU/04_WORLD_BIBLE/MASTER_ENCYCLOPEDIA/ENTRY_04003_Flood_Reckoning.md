# The Flood Reckoning

## Summary

Kwara's early-Morien civic observance marking the start of the Rains season and the delta's shift into its most active trading period — a commercial festival, not a religious one, consistent with the Confederacy's pragmatic public culture.

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Observed | Early Morien, start of the Rains season (GMU-313 §1, §3) |
| Location | Port of Kwarafi and the river delta (ENTRY-00006) |
| Character | Civic/commercial, not religious (GMU-313 §3, GMU-304 §3) |

## Description

The Flood Reckoning is Kwara's clearest festival expression of its overall character (GMU-004 §2.3): where Zamar marks a founding and Kasar Dutse marks a military threshold, Kwara marks a trading-season threshold — the point each year when high water fills the Kwarafi canals and the delta's commercial capacity peaks (GMU-310 §2). Merchant houses traditionally settle outstanding credit-network accounts (GMU-310 §1) around this observance, giving it a practical "reckoning" function beyond its name — a rare Crescent festival that is, at its core, an accounting occasion.
