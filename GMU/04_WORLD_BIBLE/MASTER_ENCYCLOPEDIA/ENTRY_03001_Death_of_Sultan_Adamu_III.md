# The Death of Sultan Adamu III

## Summary

The sudden death of Zamar's Sultan without a Diwan-ratified heir, starting the forty-day ratification clock and triggering Season One's entire succession crisis.

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Date (CU) | 427 CU (GMU-004 §3) |
| Location | Royal Palace of Zamar (ENTRY-00002) |
| Chapters | 01–02 (Episode 1 equivalent) |
| Immediate consequence | The forty-day ratification clock (GMU-004 §4) begins; Fatima and Malik become rival claimants |

## Description

Confirmed in Episode 9/Chapter 17-18 by Yusuf al-Kanem (ENTRY-01005) to have been a natural death — Yusuf denies causing it directly, though he ensured no one was prepared for its political consequences. The death's timing, not its cause, is what makes it destabilizing: no heir had yet been ratified by the Diwan al-Ilm (ENTRY-06001), triggering the untested forty-day clause (GMU-004 §4) for the first time in the institution's two-century history.
