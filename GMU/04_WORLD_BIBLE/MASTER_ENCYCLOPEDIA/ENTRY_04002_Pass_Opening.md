# The Pass Opening

## Summary

Kasar Dutse's early-Zolmar observance marking the start of the Dry season's easier mountain-pass crossing conditions and, historically, the traditional start of the campaign season — a festival with unmistakable military undertones.

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Observed | Early Zolmar, start of the Dry season (GMU-313 §1, §3) |
| Location | Tsauri Keep and the mountain pass (ENTRY-00005) |
| Character | Kasar Dutse-specific; marks both a practical travel-conditions shift and the conventional opening of the campaign season (GMU-308 §2) |

## Description

Unlike Zamar's Founding Day (ENTRY-04001), which commemorates a single founding act, the Pass Opening marks a recurring seasonal threshold — the point each year when the pass becomes easiest to cross and, not coincidentally, when Kasar Dutse's war-bands traditionally begin campaign movements (GMU-308 §2, GMU-313 §4). This dual civic/martial character is precisely why King Garba's Season One border pressure, which begins near the Sultan's death rather than at the conventional Pass Opening, reads as an urgent departure from tradition rather than routine seasonal activity (GMU-313 §4).
