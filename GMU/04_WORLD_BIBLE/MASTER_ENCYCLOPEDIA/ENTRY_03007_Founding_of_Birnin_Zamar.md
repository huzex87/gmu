# The Founding of Birnin Zamar

## Summary

Sultan Adamu I's founding of Birnin Zamar and, with it, the first Diwan al-Ilm — the single act that established the ratification rule still governing Zamar's succession over two centuries later, and the origin point of the CU (Years of the Covenant) calendar itself.

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Date (CU) | 211 CU — Year 1 of the Covenant calendar begins here (GMU-313 §1) |
| Location | Birnin Zamar (ENTRY-00001) |
| Key figure | Sultan Adamu I |
| Immediate consequence | Establishes the Diwan al-Ilm's ratification authority over all future Zamar successions |

## Description

The founding is deliberately sparse in canon by design (GMU-004 §3's own editorial note) — its dramatic weight comes not from spectacle but from the single governing precedent it sets: no ruler after Adamu I holds the Zamar throne without the Diwan's ratification. Every subsequent event in Zamar's political history, including Season One's entire succession crisis, is a consequence of this one founding decision. The CU calendar itself (GMU-313 §1) counts forward from this event, making it the fixed zero-point against which every other date in the World Bible timeline is measured.
