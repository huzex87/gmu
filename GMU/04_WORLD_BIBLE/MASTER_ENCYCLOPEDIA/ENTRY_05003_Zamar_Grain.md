# Zamar Grain (Millet and Sorghum)

## Summary

The staple grain crops of Zamar's farmland — millet and sorghum — forming the base of the kingdom's cuisine across every social tier, from farmer porridge to elevated royal dishes.

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Grown | Farmland surrounding Birnin Zamar (GMU-311 §2) |
| Culinary role | Base of Zamar cuisine at every tier — royal, street, and farmer food alike (GMU-315 §1) |
| Economic role | Domestic staple rather than a major export (contrast with leather, books, textiles, gold, horse equipment per ENTRY-00001) |

## Description

Grown in the savanna farmland Zamar sits within (GMU-311 §2), consistent with the real Sahel's actual grain-agriculture tradition this world draws inspiration from. Unlike Zamar's recorded exports (ENTRY-00001), millet and sorghum stay largely domestic — feeding the capital and the Great Market's street food rather than moving along the Northern or River trade routes, reinforcing that Zamar's economic strength (GMU-310 §2) comes from trade and scholarship rather than agricultural surplus.
