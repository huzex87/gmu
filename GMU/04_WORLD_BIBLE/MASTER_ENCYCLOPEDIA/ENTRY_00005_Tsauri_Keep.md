# Tsauri Keep

## Summary

Capital fortress of the Kingdom of Kasar Dutse, built directly into the highland pass that gives the kingdom its name (GMU-004 §2.2).

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Location | The mountain pass, Kasar Dutse's northern highland territory |
| Function | Royal seat and the kingdom's primary military chokepoint, combined |
| Architectural style | Fortified black/grey stone (GMU-306 §2) |
| Known For | Controlling the only viable trade route between the northern desert and southern delta |

## Description

Unlike the Royal Palace of Zamar (ENTRY-00002) or the Kwarafi Merchant House (ENTRY-00008), Tsauri Keep is not merely a seat of governance — it is the physical chokepoint itself (GMU-311 §3), meaning the kingdom's capital and its most valuable strategic and economic asset are the same location. Built for defensibility over grandeur: thick walls, narrow windows, functional stone furnishing (GMU-306 §2). Home to King Garba Ironhand and, in practice, to whichever war-captains currently hold his confidence (GMU-309 §2).

## History

Built during the unification of Kasar Dutse's highland clans under the first "Ironhand" king (~310 CU, GMU-004 §3), who took the pass by force and began two centuries of taxed trade — and tension — with Zamar.
