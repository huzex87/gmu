# The Founding of the Confederacy of Kwara

## Summary

The river city-states' formation of the Confederacy of Kwara circa 365 CU, after a ruinous trade war nearly destroyed all of them individually — they have never fought each other since.

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Date (CU) | ~365 CU (GMU-004 §3) |
| Location | The river delta, centered on what became Kwarafi (ENTRY-00006) |
| Key document | The Founding Charter of Kwara — ENTRY-02006 |
| Immediate consequence | Establishes the rotating Merchant-Council system and a permanent non-aggression pact among the city-states |

## Description

Unlike Zamar's founding (a single ruler's institution-building act) or Kasar Dutse's (a conquest), Kwara's founding is a founding-through-exhaustion — a near-catastrophic trade war taught the delta city-states that they profited more from cooperation than rivalry. This origin directly shapes Kwara's entire later political character (GMU-004 §2.3): a confederacy with "no interest in ruling anyone," built by merchants who had already learned the cost of open conflict the hard way. The credit-and-promissory-note financial system (GMU-310 §1) that lets Speaker Amina quietly finance both Zamar claimants in Season One traces directly back to the cooperative institutions built out of this founding crisis.
