# The Diwan's Ledger of Ratification

## Summary

The Diwan al-Ilm's unbroken written record of every Sultan ratified since Sultan Adamu I founded the institution in 211 CU — the physical proof, referenced during Season One's crisis, that the forty-day clause has never previously been tested.

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Begun | 211 CU, with the first Diwan al-Ilm (GMU-004 §3) |
| Custodian | The Diwan al-Ilm (ENTRY-06001), housed at the University of the Covenant |
| Contents | The date, claimant, and ratifying vote count for every Zamar succession since the founding |

## Description

The Ledger matters dramatically because of what it does not contain: a precedent for a contested or lapsed ratification. Elders citing the Ledger during the Season One ratification vote (Episode 9/Chapters 17-18) are not consulting abstract law but pointing to two centuries of unbroken practice — which is precisely why Yusuf's manipulation of the succession crisis (ENTRY-02003) represents such a genuine institutional rupture, not merely a political inconvenience.
