# Kasar Dutse Mountain Horse

## Summary

The mountain-bred horse breed underpinning Kasar Dutse's cavalry doctrine and one of Zamar's recorded imports ("horse equipment," per the Birnin Zamar encyclopedia entry) — bred specifically for highland terrain other breeds struggle with.

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Origin | Kasar Dutse's highland territory |
| Bred for | Surefootedness on mountain-pass terrain, endurance over raw speed |
| Primary use | Kasar Dutse cavalry (GMU-308 §2), traded to Zamar as livestock and equipment |
| Trade relevance | Underpins the "horse equipment" line item in Zamar's recorded exports (ENTRY-00001), since Zamar's own cavalry equips against Kasar Dutse-bred stock |

## Description

Smaller and stockier than a plains-bred horse, prized for the same surefootedness that makes the pass itself defensible (GMU-311 §3) — a mount bred for exactly the terrain its own kingdom uses as a military chokepoint. Kasar Dutse's herding of this breed is a genuine economic asset independent of the pass toll (GMU-310 §2), and one of the few Kasar Dutse exports that moves south into Zamar and Kwara territory without passing through the pass toll structure itself, since livestock trade predates and sits outside the formal toll system.
