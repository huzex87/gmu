# Port of Kwarafi

## Summary

Seat of the River Confederacy of Kwara, at the mouth of the delta where river trade becomes seaborne trade (GMU-004 §2.3, GMU-311 §2).

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Location | The delta's mouth, southern edge of the Sahel Crescent |
| Function | Seat of the Merchant-Council and current Speaker Amina Bello Kwarafi |
| Architectural style | Open canal-facing timber and pale stone (GMU-306 §3) |
| Known For | The Kwarafi Merchant House, floating markets, Kwara's mercenary fleet moorings |

## Description

Built around movement rather than defense — open canals, floating markets, light timber construction (GMU-306 §3, GMU-311 §2). Contains the Kwarafi Merchant House (ENTRY-00008), seat of the current Speaker, but no equivalent to a Palace or Keep — Kwara's governance is deliberately decentralized across its Merchant-Council (GMU-309 §3), and its architecture reflects that lack of a singular seat of power.

## History

Grew from the river city-states' formation of the Confederacy of Kwara (~365 CU, GMU-004 §3), following a ruinous trade war the city-states nearly didn't survive individually — Kwarafi's rise as the Confederacy's practical seat followed from its position at the delta's mouth, giving it control of the transition point between river and sea trade.
