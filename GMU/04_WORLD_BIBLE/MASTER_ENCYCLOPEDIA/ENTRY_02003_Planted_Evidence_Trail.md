# The Planted Evidence Trail (Kasar Dutse Coin and Kwara Silver)

## Summary

A Kasar Dutse coin and a Kwara silver shipment, planted and routed respectively by Abubakar on Yusuf al-Kanem's instruction, used across Season One to keep Fatima's murder investigation pointed outward at Kasar Dutse and Kwara rather than inward at the Zamar court.

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Planted by | Abubakar (ENTRY-01013), on Yusuf al-Kanem's (ENTRY-01005) instruction |
| Purpose | Misdirect Fatima's investigation into Elder Bashir's murder |
| Kasar Dutse coin | Planted at the scene of Bashir's murder |
| Kwara silver | A shipment routed to implicate Kwara's financial involvement |
| Resolved | Episode 9 / Chapter 17-18, upon Yusuf's exposure |

## Description

Consistent with GMU-310 §1's note that each kingdom mints or recognizes its own coinage, a Kasar Dutse coin found at a Zamar crime scene reads as damning circumstantial evidence to any investigator unaware of Yusuf's manipulation — exactly the effect intended. The device is a clean illustration of Season One's "politics before violence" creative principle (GMU-001 §6): the fourth power's operation works entirely through misdirection and institutional trust, never through open confrontation, until Episode 9 forces it into the open.
