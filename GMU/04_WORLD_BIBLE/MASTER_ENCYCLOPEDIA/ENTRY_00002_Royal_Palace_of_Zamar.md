# Royal Palace of Zamar

## Summary

Seat of the Sultanate's government within Birnin Zamar (ENTRY-00001), and the physical setting for most of Season One's Zamar-side political scenes, including the Throne Room (GMU-505).

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Location | Central Birnin Zamar |
| Built under | Sultan Adamu I, founding era (211 CU) |
| Architectural style | Layered courtyards, indigo/gold lattice ornamentation (GMU-306 §1) |
| Known For | Throne Room, private royal chambers, the setting of Adamu III's death |

## Description

Formal, axially symmetric complex moving from public courtyards to increasingly private chambers — a spatial expression of Zamar's layered, procedural approach to power (GMU-309 §1). Built in the earth-toned, indigo-and-gold lattice style established across GMU-002 §6 and GMU-306 §1, deliberately more monumental than the Great Market (ENTRY-00004) but without Kasar Dutse's fortification or Kwara's canal-facing openness.

## History

Built as part of Sultan Adamu I's founding of Birnin Zamar in 211 CU (GMU-004 §3), alongside the establishment of the first Diwan al-Ilm. Has housed every subsequent Sultan without interruption, making it, alongside the Diwan itself, one of the Sultanate's two continuous institutions across two centuries.
