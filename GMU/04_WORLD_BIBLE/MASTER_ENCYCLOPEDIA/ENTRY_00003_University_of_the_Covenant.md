# University of the Covenant

## Summary

Zamar's seat of learning, training the jurists who eventually sit on the Diwan al-Ilm (GMU-004 §5).

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Location | Birnin Zamar (ENTRY-00001) |
| Function | Legal/religious scholarly education, feeder institution to the Diwan al-Ilm |
| Architectural style | Scholarly-restrained variant of Zamar's lattice architecture (GMU-306 §1) |
| Known For | Training ground for every Diwan member; open study courtyards |

## Description

Architecturally distinct from the Palace (ENTRY-00002) despite sharing Zamar's core visual language — more window, more open study courtyard, less gold inlay, reflecting learning's practical rather than ostentatious role in Zamar's value system (GMU-306 §1, GMU-304 §1). Functions as the pipeline institution for the Diwan al-Ilm (ENTRY-06001): senior Ulama serving on the Diwan are, without exception, University-trained.

## History

Established alongside the Diwan al-Ilm's founding practice in the early Covenant era, though its exact founding date relative to 211 CU is not yet fixed in canon — treat as contemporaneous with the Diwan's founding until a specific episode requires a precise date.
