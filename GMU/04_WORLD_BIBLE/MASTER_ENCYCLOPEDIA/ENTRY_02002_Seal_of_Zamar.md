# The Seal of Zamar

## Summary

The Sultanate's state seal — an open book beneath a compass rose, rendered in indigo and gold (GMU-004 §2.1) — affixed to Diwan-ratified legal documents, including the ratification declaration that closes Season One's succession crisis.

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Design | Open book beneath a compass rose, indigo and gold |
| Used for | Ratification documents, formal Diwan rulings, state correspondence |
| Custodian | Historically the sitting Sultan; during an interregnum, the Diwan al-Ilm (ENTRY-06001) holds custodial authority |

## Description

The seal's open-book motif directly encodes Zamar's self-image as a kingdom ruled by law and scholarship rather than force (GMU-004 §2.1) — the same symbolic logic that makes the Diwan's ratification process (GMU-309 §1) the season's central mechanism. Affixing the seal to a ratification document is the formal, physical act that makes a Sultan's legitimacy real in the eyes of Zamar's institutions, distinct from any coronation ceremony's symbolic weight.
