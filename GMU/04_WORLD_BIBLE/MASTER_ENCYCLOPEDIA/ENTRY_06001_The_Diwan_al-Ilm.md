# The Diwan al-Ilm

## Summary

Zamar's ratifying council of senior Ulama — the body whose approval every Sultan requires, and the institution the entire Season One conflict turns on (GMU-004 §2.1).

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Founded | 211 CU, alongside Birnin Zamar's founding (GMU-004 §3) |
| Composition | Senior Ulama drawn primarily from the University of the Covenant (ENTRY-00003) and the Great Sanctuary's clergy hierarchy (GMU-309 §1) |
| Real-world basis | Modeled on the historical Islamic governance concept of Ahl al-Hall wal-Aqd (GMU-305 §4) |
| Core power | Ratification of a new Sultan within forty days of the previous Sultan's death, or the throne is considered vacant (GMU-004 §4) |

## Description

Not a rubber-stamp body — the Diwan examines a claimant's fitness to rule (character, conduct, capacity) rather than simple bloodline priority (GMU-309 §1). Between successions, the Diwan itself functions as Zamar's acting authority, which is why King Garba and Speaker Amina are both, in practice, negotiating with or around the Diwan during Season One's interregnum rather than with any single named official.

## History

Founded alongside Sultan Adamu I's establishment of Birnin Zamar in 211 CU (GMU-004 §3), and has ratified every Zamar Sultan since without interruption until the current crisis — Sultan Adamu III's sudden death with no ratified heir (GMU-004 §4) is the first genuine test of the forty-day clause in the institution's two-century history.
