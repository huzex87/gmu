## Description

Zamar's capital and the Crescent's genuine economic center, despite the kingdom's military weakness relative to Kasar Dutse (GMU-308 §1) — built in the earth-toned, indigo-and-gold lattice architectural style formalized in GMU-306 §1, moving from the Royal Palace's formal ceremonial core outward through the University's scholarly district to the Great Market's comparatively humble, crowded commercial sprawl. Its position as the convergence point for both the Northern trade route (through Kasar Dutse's pass) and the River Route (through Kwara's delta) is what makes it economically indispensable to all three powers regardless of who currently holds political advantage (GMU-310 §2, GMU-311 §5).

## History

Founded in 211 CU by Sultan Adamu I, who established both the city and, alongside it, the first Diwan al-Ilm (ENTRY-06001) and its ratification rule — the same rule whose forty-day clause Season One's central conflict now tests for the first time in the institution's two-century history (GMU-004 §4). The city's growth since founding has tracked its role as the Crescent's trade clearing-house (GMU-310 §2) rather than any territorial expansion — Zamar has never needed to conquer new land to remain the region's economic center, only to keep the Great Market's neutral status intact.
