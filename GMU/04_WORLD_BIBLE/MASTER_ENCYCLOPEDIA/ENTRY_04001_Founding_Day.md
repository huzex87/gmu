# Founding Day

## Summary

Zamar's civil observance commemorating Sultan Adamu I's founding of Birnin Zamar and the first Diwan al-Ilm in 211 CU — the Crescent's oldest continuously observed civil festival.

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Observed | Early Zolmar (start of the civil year, GMU-313 §1) |
| Commemorates | Sultan Adamu I's founding of Birnin Zamar and the first Diwan al-Ilm, 211 CU |
| Observance level | Most formal in Zamar; acknowledged but not celebrated with equivalent weight in Kasar Dutse or Kwara (GMU-313 §3) |

## Description

The oldest of the Crescent's named civil festivals, tied directly to the founding events recorded in GMU-004 §3's timeline. Its comparatively muted observance outside Zamar reflects the Crescent's genuinely decentralized political history — Founding Day is Zamar's origin story specifically, not a shared Crescent-wide founding myth, distinct from the way the Faith of the Covenant (GMU-305) is genuinely shared across all three kingdoms.
