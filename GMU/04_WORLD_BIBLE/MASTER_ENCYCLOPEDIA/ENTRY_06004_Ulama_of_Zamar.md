# The Ulama of Zamar

## Summary

Zamar's full body of Islamic scholars, jurists, and clergy — the broader class from which the Diwan al-Ilm's ratifying council (ENTRY-06001) is drawn. The Diwan is the Ulama's most visible institutional expression, not a separate body.

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Basis of authority | Years of study and the granting of teaching licenses (ijazas) — real Islamic tradition, not appointment or bloodline (GMU-305 §3) |
| Hierarchy | High Cleric (most senior, presides at the Great Sanctuary) → Elders of the Diwan al-Ilm (senior Ulama who sit on the ratification council) → Junior clergy |
| Seat of training | The University of the Covenant (ENTRY-00003) |
| Notable members | High Cleric Nafisa (ENTRY-01012), Elders Kamal (ENTRY-01009) and Bashir (ENTRY-01007) |

## Description

The distinction between the Ulama and the Diwan al-Ilm matters institutionally: the Ulama is Zamar's entire scholarly-religious class, while the Diwan is specifically the subset of senior Ulama empowered to ratify a Sultan (GMU-309 §1) — a real-world-grounded structure modeled on Ahl al-Hall wal-Aqd, "those who loose and bind" (GMU-004 §1). Not every member of the Ulama sits on the Diwan; junior clergy and teaching scholars build standing for years, sometimes decades, before reaching that tier. This hierarchy gives Zamar's clergy the same precedent-based, study-driven character as its broader legal culture (GMU-304 §1) — authority earned through demonstrated scholarship, in explicit contrast to Kasar Dutse's combat-earned war-captain rank (GMU-309 §2) and Kwara's contract-and-vote-based Merchant-Council seats (GMU-309 §3).
