# The Coronation of Fatima

## Summary

Season One's finale event — Fatima bint Adamu is crowned Zamar's first female ruler, at real and lasting personal cost, closing the succession crisis that began with her father's death.

## Key Facts

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| Crowned | Fatima bint Adamu (ENTRY-01001) |
| Chapters | 19–20 (Episode 10 equivalent, season finale) |
| Ceremonial language used | The Old Tongue's coronation blessing (GMU-312 §3) |
| Immediate aftermath | A quiet, honest mother-daughter scene with Queen Mother Halima (ENTRY-01010) the same night |

## Description

Fatima wins the throne only after compromising the very principle — legitimacy through law, not deals — that made her worth rooting for across the season (GMU-005 §Fatima bint Adamu), consistent with GMU-001 §6's creative principle "every victory has a cost." The ceremony is the first on-screen use of the Old Tongue's ready-made coronation blessing ("Kaeleth ithoren, Shavael morar. Toranu, anu, dunareth," GMU-312 §3). Malik's Episode 9/Chapter 17-18 choice not to break the constitutional process, at cost to himself, is the direct precondition for this event being possible at all.
