# GMU-308 — Military Bible

Armies, doctrine, weapons, ranks, and campaign history for all three powers. Written to formalize what the screenplays and GMU-002 §8 (Weapons/Armor) already establish or imply, not to invent new plot-relevant military facts — any future episode needing a specific battle or unit beyond what's here should extend this document rather than contradict it.

## 1. The Sultanate of Zamar — The Covenant Guard

Per GMU-004 §2.1, Zamar is deliberately "the weakest of the three powers militarily" — its security rests on law and the Diwan al-Ilm's legitimacy, not on force. This is a core, load-bearing worldbuilding fact: Zamar's military exists to keep order and defend the capital, not to project power abroad.

**Structure:** The **Covenant Guard**, a comparatively small standing force headquartered at the Palace and the University of the Covenant. Recruited from Zamar's citizenry (not a warrior caste), many officers are University-trained in law as well as arms — reflecting Zamar's self-image as a place where authority is reasoned, not simply imposed.

**Doctrine:** Defensive and ceremonial first, combat second. The Covenant Guard's primary real function across the series is crowd control, palace security, and escort duty (e.g., presence at the Great Sanctuary during Episode 6/Chapter 11-12's public address, and at the ratification vote in Episode 9/Chapters 17-18). It is not built or expected to win a field battle against Kasar Dutse's cavalry — this asymmetry is precisely what makes Garba's pressure on the border credible and dangerous throughout Season One.

**Ranks (low to high):** Guardsman → Watch-Captain → Gate-Commander → Commander of the Covenant Guard (a Diwan-adjacent appointment, answerable to the sitting Sultan and, in practice, to the Diwan during an interregnum).

**Equipment:** Practical, undecorated indigo-and-gold-trimmed service dress over light armor — spears and short swords for crowd/palace work, nothing suited to sustained field campaigns. Ceremonial ranks (ceremonial guard flanking the throne room, per GMU-002 §8) carry more ornamented versions of the same base kit for state occasions.

## 2. The Kingdom of Kasar Dutse — The War-Captains' Cavalry

Kasar Dutse's entire political system (GMU-004 §2.2) runs on military strength: "the throne goes to whichever claimant the war-captains will follow." Its army is not a tool of the state — it effectively *is* the state.

**Structure:** Organized around **war-bands**, each answering to a **war-captain** — a Kasar Dutse noble title earned through combat record and the personal loyalty of the fighters under him, not inherited by birthright alone (though sons of war-captains have a practical head start). King Garba Ironhand's authority is real but conditional: he rules because enough war-captains currently back him, a fact the Political Bible (GMU-309) formalizes as the kingdom's actual succession law.

**Doctrine:** Mountain and mounted warfare. Kasar Dutse controls the only viable pass connecting the northern desert routes to the southern river delta (GMU-004 §2.2), and its doctrine is built entirely around that terrain — fast cavalry for the open approaches, disciplined infantry holding fortified chokepoints for the pass itself. Kasar Dutse does not build a navy or a bureaucratic guard force; its entire military identity is offense-capable land power, which is exactly what makes it the credible outside threat throughout Season One's succession crisis.

**Ranks (low to high):** Spear (rank-and-file fighter) → Blooded (a fighter who has survived and been marked by real combat — a social as well as military status) → War-Captain (band commander) → the King, first among war-captains rather than above them in any absolute sense.

**Equipment:** Iron-grey and deep-red heraldry (crossed spears over a stone peak, per GMU-004 §2.2's symbol). Practical, unornamented armor built for mountain mobility — layered leather and iron plate rather than Zamar's ceremonial dress or Kwara's naval gear. Weapons favor the spear (the kingdom's own core symbol) and curved blades suited to mounted combat.

**Campaign history (brief, to be expanded only as episodes require):** Kasar Dutse and Zamar have never fought an open war in living memory — their relationship has instead been two centuries of taxed trade and unresolved tension (GMU-004 §3, ~310 CU unification onward), which is exactly why King Garba's current pressure on the border reads as a genuine escalation rather than a continuation of an existing conflict.

## 3. The River Confederacy of Kwara — The Mercenary Fleet

Per GMU-004 §2.3, Kwara "has no army worth the name" by design — its military capability is a hired fleet, not a raised one, consistent with a confederacy whose entire strategy is never being anyone's enemy and never quite being anyone's friend.

**Structure:** No standing royal army. Instead, the Merchant-Council retains a rotating roster of mercenary naval and marine crews under contract, expandable or reducible based on need and price. This is a deliberate structural difference from Zamar and Kasar Dutse: Kwara's "military" is a line item, not an institution with its own internal politics.

**Doctrine:** Naval interdiction and marine boarding actions to protect river-delta trade routes from piracy and to enforce contract terms on client vessels — not field conquest. Kwara's fleet exists to keep goods moving, not to hold territory.

**Ranks:** Informal and contract-based rather than a fixed hierarchy — a hired crew answers to its own captain, who answers to whichever Merchant-Council representative holds that contract. There is no "Kwara officer corps" in the way Zamar or Kasar Dutse would recognize the term.

**Equipment (per GMU-002 §8, already established):** Naval/marine mercenary gear, deliberately non-uniform since crews are hired, not raised — emerald and white accents where a crew chooses to display Kwara contract colors, but no mandated uniform standard. This visual inconsistency is intentional and should be preserved in any future concept art or shot generation — a uniform Kwara "army look" would contradict the kingdom's entire military philosophy.

## 4. Cross-Kingdom Comparison (for quick reference)

| | Zamar | Kasar Dutse | Kwara |
|---|---|---|---|
| Basis | Law/legitimacy | Personal loyalty to war-captains | Contract/price |
| Primary force type | Small standing guard | Mounted cavalry + fortified infantry | Hired naval/marine crews |
| Terrain specialty | Urban/palace | Mountain pass | River delta |
| Command legitimacy | Diwan-appointed | War-captains' consent | Merchant-Council contract |
| Combat readiness (vs. field war) | Low | High | Situational (naval only) |
