# GMU-317 — Transportation Bible

How people and goods actually move through the Sahel Crescent — consolidated in one place for the first time. Every fact below already existed somewhere in the Military Bible, Economic Bible, Atlas, screenplays, or concept art briefs; this document gathers it into a single reference and fills the two gaps that scattered coverage had left open: the northern desert caravan leg of the Northern Route, and ordinary land transport for common people and everyday goods. Per GMU-001's "just enough, just in time" principle, transportation choices below reinforce each power's established identity (GMU-002, GMU-004 §2) rather than introducing new worldbuilding for its own sake.

## 1. Guiding Principle

Exactly like currency, architecture, and military doctrine, transportation is a mirror of the three powers' identities: Zamar moves people and paperwork with ceremonial control, Kasar Dutse moves goods and soldiers with terrain-adapted discipline, and Kwara moves everything else, for a fee, faster and less formally than either. No power has transportation infrastructure that contradicts its established character elsewhere in the bible — see Section 6 for how this rule shaped the two new additions in this document.

## 2. Zamar — Cavalry, Palanquin, and River Traffic

**Cavalry:** Zamar's dark-tunic, gold-braid cavalry (Malik's, Tahir's, Rafiu's commands) is fully documented in the Military Bible (GMU-308 §1) — horses here are plains-bred rather than the mountain breed Kasar Dutse fields (Section 3), suited to open savanna and ceremonial parade use rather than pass-terrain endurance.

**Within Birnin Zamar:** The city's 18 districts (GMU-501, GMU-311) are dense enough that most short-distance movement, including by nobility, is on foot or by palanquin rather than mounted — see Section 5 for the palanquin as a cross-kingdom elite convention.

**River traffic:** The river that feeds Kwara's delta passes directly by Birnin Zamar (GMU-311 §4) and serves as the city's own internal transport backbone as well as the Kwara-bound trade artery — fishing boats work the River Port pre-dawn (GMU-501), and the same stretch of river carries local passenger and goods traffic within the city that never leaves Zamar territory at all, distinct from the long-haul River Route to Kwarafi (Section 4).

## 3. Kasar Dutse — Mountain Cavalry and Pass Logistics

**Cavalry:** Kasar Dutse's mounted-and-fortified doctrine (GMU-308 §2) runs on the Kasar Dutse Mountain Horse (Master Encyclopedia ENTRY-05001) — smaller and stockier than a plains breed, bred specifically for surefootedness on the pass's terrain rather than raw speed. This is the kingdom's only cavalry stock; it does not field, breed, or use any other mount for military purposes.

**Pack transport through the pass:** Goods crossing the pass itself — regardless of what carried them on the desert side (Section 6) — transfer to mountain-bred pack mules and the same stocky mountain-horse stock for the crossing, since the pass's terrain is exactly what the desert-adapted caravan stock arriving from the north cannot safely handle. This transfer point is itself a minor but real economic detail: Kasar Dutse's toll (GMU-310 §2) is collected precisely where this changeover happens, meaning the kingdom taxes the one stretch of the Northern Route no other transport option can bypass.

**No naval capability, by design:** Kasar Dutse fields no boats or ships of any kind (GMU-308 §2) — its military and economic identity is built entirely around land power and the pass chokepoint, consistent with the kingdom's "does not build a navy or a bureaucratic guard force" characterization.

## 4. Kwara — River Barges, Canal Boats, and the Mercenary Fleet

**Canal and delta transport:** Kwara's open, canal-facing architecture (GMU-507) exists because canal boats and barges are the Confederacy's primary transport mode for both goods and people — the Kwarafi Merchant House's Counting Hall and Trade Quarter briefs both assume constant boat traffic as part of the location's normal visual texture, not a special event.

**River barges (formal/ceremonial use):** Reserved for higher-stakes or ceremonial movement rather than routine transport — Speaker Amina's arrival at the Palace of Zamar (Chapter 2) and Fatima and Kamal's journey to Kwarafi (Chapter 7) both use a river barge specifically, distinct from the everyday canal-boat traffic of Section 4's first point. A river-barge landing is a fixed feature of the Palace's Main Gate (GMU-502).

**The mercenary naval/marine fleet:** Documented in full in the Military Bible (GMU-308 §3) and its funding mechanics in the Economic Bible (GMU-310 §5) — hired crews under Merchant-Council contract, non-uniform by deliberate design, tasked with interdiction and escort along river-delta trade routes rather than field conquest. This is Kwara's only standing military-adjacent transport asset.

**The Port of Kwarafi:** Where river trade becomes seaborne trade (GMU-311 §1) — the physical point that makes Kwara's fleet naval/marine rather than land-based in the first place.

## 5. Cross-Kingdom Elite Conveyance — The Palanquin

The palanquin is not tied to any single kingdom's identity — it's the general convention for short-distance ceremonial movement by high-ranking figures of any power, used where walking would undercut the moment's formality but a full mount or vehicle would be impractical for the distance involved (e.g., from a river-barge landing to a palace's reception hall). Established on-screen by Speaker Amina's entrance in Chapter 2. Treat it as available to any principal character in a formal-arrival context, not exclusive to Kwara despite Amina being the character who first used one.

## 6. The Northern Route's Desert Leg — Caravan Camels (new)

The Atlas (GMU-311 §5) and Economic Bible (GMU-310 §2) both reference "desert trade" and "the northern desert routes" feeding into the pass, but neither previously specified what actually carries goods across that stretch — this was a real gap, since neither Zamar's plains horses nor Kasar Dutse's mountain-bred stock (Section 3) is suited to desert terrain, and it would have been inconsistent to imply either kingdom's own mount handles that leg.

**Resolution:** the desert leg north of the pass is worked by independent caravan traders using desert-adapted camel stock — foreign to all three Crescent powers, arriving from beyond the map's currently-defined northern edge (per GMU-311's "just enough" scoping, the exact desert polities beyond the Crescent remain undefined until a story reason requires them). These caravans sell or transfer their goods at the pass's northern approach, where Kasar Dutse's own mountain pack animals take over for the crossing itself (Section 3) — camels are not bred for the pass's terrain and don't cross it. This also means camel caravans themselves never appear inside Kasar Dutse, Zamar, or Kwara proper in any Season One material; they exist entirely at and beyond the pass's northern threshold. See Master Encyclopedia ENTRY-05004 for the breed entry.

## 7. Everyday Transportation for Ordinary People and Goods (new)

Previously undocumented — the Character Bible, screenplays, and concept art all correctly assume common people mostly travel on foot or don't travel far at all, but nothing formalized it. For consistency across future concept art and video generation:

- **Foot travel** is the default for the overwhelming majority of ordinary people in all three powers' territory — this should be the assumed mode any time a background/incidental character (GMU-410-style role) is shown moving, unless a specific scene calls for otherwise.
- **Donkey and ox carts** handle local goods movement within cities and between nearby villages — market-bound produce within Birnin Zamar's districts, farm goods around Kantin Rafi (GMU-509). These are plain, undecorated, practical vehicles with no kingdom color-coding, consistent with GMU-509's "no banners, no ceremonial color-coding" note for the border village itself.
- **Pack mules** supplement mountain-horse stock for non-military goods transport within Kasar Dutse's own territory, distinct from the pass-crossing pack transport in Section 3.
- None of the above should ever be rendered with kingdom banners, ceremonial ornament, or any visual treatment implying official/military status — the moment a cart or pack animal looks "official," it stops reading as everyday transportation, which defeats the purpose of including this tier at all.
